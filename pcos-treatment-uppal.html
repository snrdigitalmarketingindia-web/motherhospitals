What is PCOS / PMOS?

The most common fertility condition in women ��� and the most treatable

PCOS (now officially renamed PMOS — Polycystic Ovarian Morphology Syndrome by the Endocrine Society in May 2026) affects 1 in 5 women of reproductive age in India. It causes irregular or absent periods, hormonal imbalance and difficulty conceiving — but with the right treatment, the vast majority of women with PCOS can achieve pregnancy.

📅

Irregular Periods

Cycles longer than 35 days or fewer than 8 cycles per year

⚖️

Weight Gain

Unexplained weight gain, especially around the abdomen

🌿

Excess Hair Growth

Unwanted facial or body hair (hirsutism) from elevated androgens

🔴

Acne & Oily Skin

Hormonal acne that persists beyond teenage years

👶

Difficulty Conceiving

Anovulation (lack of ovulation) is the main cause of PCOS infertility

🔬

Polycystic Ovaries

Multiple small follicles visible on ultrasound scan

The PCOS Treatment Ladder at Mother Hospitals

A step-by-step approach — starting simple, escalating only when needed

1

Lifestyle & Hormonal Management

Diet modification, weight management, insulin sensitisers (Metformin), and cycle regularisation. For women not yet trying to conceive, this first step often restores periods and reduces symptoms significantly.

Best first step · Non-invasive
2

Ovulation Induction

Oral medications — Letrozole (preferred) or Clomiphene — trigger egg release. Monitored by ultrasound scan to confirm ovulation. Timed intercourse or IUI can be combined. Success rate: 20–25% per cycle.

First-line fertility treatment for PCOS
3

IUI (Intrauterine Insemination)

After ovulation induction, processed sperm is placed directly into the uterus at the time of ovulation — increasing the chance of fertilisation. Recommended for 3–6 cycles before moving to IVF.

Minimally invasive · Low cost
4

IVF with Freeze-All Protocol

For PCOS patients at risk of OHSS (ovarian hyperstimulation), Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy uses a freeze-all approach — all embryos are frozen after egg retrieval and transferred in a subsequent cycle. This eliminates OHSS risk entirely while maintaining high success rates.

all-inclusive · OHSS-safe protocol
5

Needleless IVF for PCOS

Mother Hospitals offers Needleless IVF — a gentler stimulation protocol using oral medications instead of daily injections. PCOS patients with needle anxiety or mild OHSS risk are ideal candidates for this pain-free approach.

Unique to Mother Hospitals · No daily injections
