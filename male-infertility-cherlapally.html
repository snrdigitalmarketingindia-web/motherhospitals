Our Services

🔬 Semen Analysis — Start Here

Complete sperm assessment: count, motility, morphology, volume. WHO 2021 reference values. Results in 2–4 hours. Private sample collection room. If abnormal: repeat + DNA fragmentation test + hormonal workup.

🧬 ICSI — Works With Very Low Count

A single sperm injected directly into the egg under high magnification. Count below 1 million? ICSI still achieves 70–80% fertilisation. Most IVF cycles at Mother Hospitals use ICSI as standard.

⚕️ TESA for Azoospermia

No sperm in semen? TESA retrieves sperm directly from testis under local anaesthesia — 20 minutes, comfortable. Obstructive azoospermia: near-100% success. Non-obstructive: 30–50% yield. Same-day ICSI.

🧪 Sperm DNA Fragmentation

Normal count but IVF failing or recurrent miscarriage? Sperm DNA fragmentation may be the hidden cause. Test identifies it. Treatment: antioxidants, PICSI ICSI. A commonly missed diagnosis.

💊 Medical Treatment First

Varicocele repair. Hormonal treatment (clomiphene, FSH). Antioxidants — CoQ10, zinc, selenium. Lifestyle: stop smoking, lose weight, avoid heat. 3 months treatment → retest. May avoid IVF.

Frequently Asked Questions

How far is Mother Hospitals from Cherlapally?
~4 km via Kapra Road and Boduppal Ring Road — approximately 10 minutes by vehicle. Cherlapally Industrial Area and Cherlapally Colony residents find this a very convenient commute for fertility consultations.
Can a semen analysis be done near Cherlapally?
Yes — semen analysis is done at Mother Hospitals Boduppal, 10 minutes from Cherlapally. Private collection room available. Results in 2–4 hours on the same day. No appointment needed for semen analysis — call ahead to confirm timing.
My sperm count is 8 million — what treatment do I need?
8 million count: IUI may be possible (need to check washed motile count). ICSI IVF gives the most reliable result. Dr. Prashanthi reviews the full semen analysis — count, motility, morphology — and recommends the most appropriate path.
Does heat at work affect sperm count?
Yes — occupational heat exposure (near furnaces, boilers, machinery) raises scrotal temperature and reduces sperm production. Avoiding heat, cooling measures, and antioxidants help. If count is affected, discuss with Dr. Prashanthi — treatment adjusted accordingly.
Should both husband and wife come to the first consultation?
Ideally yes — both come together for first visit. Husband provides semen sample (processed same day). Wife has initial assessment. All results reviewed together. Saves a separate appointment.
