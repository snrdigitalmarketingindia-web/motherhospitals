What qualifications does Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy hold?+

Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy holds MBBS, DGO (Diploma in Gynaecology & Obstetrics), and a Diploma in Assisted Reproductive Technology (ART) from UKSH Kiel University, Germany — one of Europe's premier fertility training centres. What gynaecology conditions do you treat at Mother Hospitals?+

Mother Hospitals treats the full spectrum of gynaecological conditions including PCOS/PMOS, uterine fibroids, endometriosis, ovarian cysts, menstrual disorders (irregular, heavy, painful, or absent periods), white discharge, pelvic pain, adolescent gynaecology issues, cervical conditions (HPV, Pap smear), and menopause. Obstetric care covers normal delivery, C-section, high-risk pregnancy, and antenatal care. Fertility services include IVF, ICSI, IUI, needleless IVF, and egg freezing.

Does Dr. Prashanthi Reddy treat PCOS / PMOS?+

Yes. PCOS/PMOS (recently renamed from Polycystic Ovary Syndrome to Polycystic Metabolic and Ovulatory Syndrome by the Endocrine Society in May 2026) is one of the most frequently treated conditions at Mother Hospitals. Treatment includes hormonal correction, ovulation induction, metabolic management, weight management counselling, and fertility treatment when needed. Every plan is fully individualised — no generic protocols.

Can I see a gynaecologist for irregular periods?+

Absolutely. Irregular periods are one of the most common reasons women visit Mother Hospitals. Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy investigates the underlying hormonal, structural, or lifestyle cause — which may include PCOS/PMOS, thyroid disorders, endometriosis, or stress — and designs a targeted treatment plan. Early evaluation leads to faster resolution and protects your fertility for the future.

Does Mother Hospitals provide adolescent gynaecology?+

Yes. Adolescent gynaecology is a dedicated service at Mother Hospitals for girls aged 13 and above. Consultations are completely non-invasive and confidential, covering first menstrual period concerns, cycle irregularities, PCOS/PMOS, puberty-related hormonal changes, and general reproductive health education. Both the young patient and her parent/guardian are welcomed in a warm, reassuring environment.
