Key Fact: 1 in 20 men has a low sperm count. Oligospermia is diagnosed when count falls below 16 million/mL — the WHO 2021 reference value. The good news: ICSI can achieve pregnancy with even a single healthy sperm. A low count is not the end of the road.

Treatment by Oligospermia Grade

Personalised protocol at Mother Hospitals based on your semen analysis result

Mild Oligospermia
5–15 million/mL
IUI + ovulation induction (2–3 cycles). Antioxidant supplements for 3 months. Identify and treat cause (varicocele, hormones). Natural conception possible with optimised parameters.
Moderate Oligospermia
1–5 million/mL
IVF + ICSI. Antioxidant therapy alongside treatment. ICSI selects the best available sperm for injection — achieving pregnancy even with very few sperm.
Severe / Cryptozoospermia
<1 million/mL or trace
ICSI (even 1 sperm is enough). TESA if no sperm found in ejaculate. Sperm cryopreservation recommended before IVF cycle start to ensure sperm is available.

Antioxidants That Improve Sperm Count

Evidence-based supplements — require 3 months (one full sperm cycle) to show results

⚡
CoQ10 (Ubiquinol)
600mg/day
Strongest evidence for improving sperm count and motility. Powers mitochondria in sperm. Antioxidant protection for sperm DNA.
🍊
Vitamin C
1000mg/day
Reduces oxidative stress in seminal fluid. Protects sperm DNA from damage. Improves count and motility in smokers particularly.
🌻
Vitamin E
400 IU/day
Fat-soluble antioxidant. Works synergistically with Vitamin C and Selenium to protect sperm from free radical damage.
🔩
Zinc
25–50mg/day
Essential mineral for sperm production. Zinc deficiency directly reduces count and motility. Most Indian men are mildly zinc deficient.
🌱
Folic Acid
400mcg/day
Required for DNA synthesis during spermatogenesis. Low folate associated with higher DNA fragmentation and reduced count.
🍅
L-Carnitine
2–3g/day
Critical for sperm energy metabolism. L-Carnitine supplementation significantly improves both sperm count and motility.

Note: Supplements take 3 months to show results (one complete sperm production cycle). Prescribed after reviewing your semen analysis. Do not self-prescribe without medical advice.

Frequently Asked Questions — Oligospermia

What is oligospermia?
Oligospermia is the medical term for low sperm count — below 16 million/mL (WHO 2021). Grades: mild (5–15M/mL), moderate (1–5M/mL), severe (<1M/mL), cryptozoospermia (only found after centrifugation). It is one of the most common causes of male infertility and is treatable with IUI (mild) or ICSI (moderate to severe).
What is oligospermia treatment in Hyderabad?
At Mother Hospitals Boduppal Hyderabad: Mild oligospermia — IUI + antioxidants + treat cause. Moderate oligospermia — IVF+ICSI. Severe/crypto — ICSI (even 1 sperm is enough). All cases — 3-month antioxidant supplementation (CoQ10, Vitamin C/E, Zinc, L-Carnitine) + identify and treat underlying cause (varicocele repair, hormonal treatment).
Is oligospermia curable?
If the cause is correctable — varicocele repair, hormonal treatment, lifestyle changes — count can improve. Even if it cannot be corrected, ICSI achieves pregnancy with very few or even one sperm. 'Cure' is not always necessary — ICSI is effective regardless of the count.
Can oligospermia be treated without IVF?
Yes — for mild oligospermia. Options: (1) Treat the cause (varicocele, hormones, lifestyle). (2) Antioxidant supplements for 3 months. (3) IUI — for mild oligospermia with good motility. IVF/ICSI recommended for moderate to severe oligospermia, or if IUI attempts have failed.
What foods or supplements help increase sperm count?
Evidence-based supplements: CoQ10 (600mg/day), Vitamin C (1000mg), Vitamin E (400IU), Zinc (25–50mg), Folic acid (400mcg), L-Carnitine (2–3g). Foods: walnuts, tomatoes, pomegranate, dark leafy greens, eggs, omega-3 fish. Avoid: processed foods, excess soy, trans fats, anabolic steroids. Requires 3 months consistently.
