Understanding Ovulation

What happens during ovulation and why it matters for conception

Ovulation is the release of a mature egg from one of the ovaries. It occurs approximately 14 days before the next expected period, triggered by a surge in luteinising hormone (LH). The egg survives only 12–24 hours after release, making the fertile window — the 5 days before ovulation and ovulation day itself — critical for conception.

Signs You Are Ovulating

Common signs include clear, stretchy "egg-white" cervical mucus, mild one-sided pelvic pain (mittelschmerz), a slight rise in basal body temperature after ovulation, breast tenderness, and increased libido. Using OPKs (ovulation predictor kits) from Day 10 can detect the LH surge 24–36 hours before ovulation.

When Ovulation Doesn't Happen (Anovulation)

Anovulation (no ovulation) is the most common cause of irregular or absent periods and is strongly associated with PCOS, thyroid disorders, hyperprolactinaemia, low BMI, and excessive exercise. If you suspect you are not ovulating, consult Dr. Prashanthi Reddy for a hormonal panel and follicular monitoring scan.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate my ovulation date?

Ovulation occurs approximately 14 days before your next expected period. For a 28-day cycle, ovulation is around Day 14. For a 30-day cycle, it is around Day 16. This calculator uses your last period date and cycle length to predict your ovulation date automatically.

What is the fertile window?

The fertile window spans 6 days — the 5 days before ovulation and the day of ovulation itself. This is because sperm can survive in the reproductive tract for up to 5 days, while the egg survives only 12–24 hours after ovulation. The highest pregnancy chances are on the 2 days before ovulation and on ovulation day itself.

Can I use OPKs to confirm ovulation?

Yes. OPKs detect the LH surge that triggers ovulation 24–36 hours later. Start testing 2–3 days before your predicted ovulation date. A positive OPK (test line as dark or darker than control) means ovulation will occur within 12–36 hours. OPKs are especially useful for women with irregular cycles.

Do I always ovulate on Day 14?

No. Day 14 is average for a 28-day cycle only. Ovulation always occurs approximately 14 days BEFORE your next period, regardless of cycle length. A 35-day cycle would have ovulation around Day 21. Stress, illness, travel, and PCOS can all shift ovulation timing.

What are signs of ovulation?

Signs of ovulation include: clear, stretchy egg-white cervical mucus (most reliable physical sign), a slight rise in basal body temperature (BBT) of 0.2–0.5°C after ovulation, mild one-sided pelvic pain (mittelschmerz), breast tenderness, and a positive OPK result. Tracking multiple signs together (symptothermal method) is most accurate.

What if I have irregular cycles?

Irregular cycles make ovulation prediction harder. Options include tracking BBT daily, using OPKs for a longer window (starting earlier in the cycle), or monitoring follicle growth with transvaginal ultrasound at a fertility clinic.
