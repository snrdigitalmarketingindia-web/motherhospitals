Understanding Ovulation

What happens during ovulation and why it matters for conception

Ovulation is the release of a mature egg from one of the ovaries, triggered by a surge in LH hormone. The egg survives only 12–24 hours after release, making the fertile window — the 5 days before ovulation and ovulation day itself — critical for conception.

Signs You Are Ovulating

Clear, stretchy "egg-white" cervical mucus, mild one-sided pelvic pain (mittelschmerz), a rise in basal body temperature, and a positive OPK result. Using OPKs from Day 10 can detect the LH surge 24–36 hours before ovulation.

When Ovulation Doesn't Happen (Anovulation)

Anovulation is the most common cause of irregular or absent periods — associated with PCOS, thyroid disorders, low BMI, and excessive exercise. Consult Dr. Prashanthi Reddy for a hormonal panel and follicular monitoring scan.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate my ovulation date?

Ovulation occurs approximately 14 days before your next expected period. For a 28-day cycle, ovulation is around Day 14. This calculator uses your last period date and cycle length to predict it automatically.

What is the fertile window?

The fertile window spans 6 days — the 5 days before ovulation and the day of ovulation. Sperm survive up to 5 days; the egg survives only 12–24 hours. The highest chances are on the 2 days before ovulation and on ovulation day itself.

Can I use OPKs to confirm ovulation?

Yes. OPKs detect the LH surge 24–36 hours before ovulation. A positive OPK (dark test line) means ovulation is imminent. Start testing 2–3 days before your predicted ovulation date.

Do I always ovulate on Day 14?

No. Ovulation always occurs ~14 days BEFORE your next period, regardless of cycle length. A 35-day cycle has ovulation around Day 21. Stress, illness, and PCOS can shift ovulation timing.

What are signs of ovulation?

Egg-white cervical mucus, slight rise in BBT, mild one-sided pelvic pain (mittelschmerz), breast tenderness, and positive OPK. Tracking multiple signs together is most accurate.

What if I have irregular cycles?

Track BBT daily, use OPKs for a longer window, or get follicular monitoring scan at Mother Hospitals. If cycles vary by more than 7 days, call: 97059 93366.
