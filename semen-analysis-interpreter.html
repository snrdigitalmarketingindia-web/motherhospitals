    Understanding Your Semen Analysis

    What each parameter means and how it affects fertility

    A semen analysis is the most important initial test for male fertility. It measures multiple parameters that together reflect the ability of sperm to fertilise an egg. The WHO 2021 reference values are the most current standards.

    Why Morphology Matters

    Sperm morphology (shape) is assessed using Kruger strict criteria. Even in fertile men, only 4–14% of sperm have completely normal morphology. Low morphology (teratozoospermia) is associated with reduced natural conception but ICSI can overcome most morphology problems.

    What to Do If Results Are Abnormal

    Repeat after 3 months of lifestyle changes. A second abnormal result warrants specialist referral. Treatable causes include varicocele, hormonal imbalances, and lifestyle factors. ICSI achieves high fertilisation rates even with severe oligospermia.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    What are the WHO 2021 normal semen analysis values?

    WHO 2021 lower reference limits: Volume ≥1.4 mL, Sperm concentration ≥16 million/mL, Total motility ≥42%, Progressive motility ≥30%, Morphology ≥4%, Total sperm count ≥39 million per ejaculate.

    What is oligozoospermia?

    Oligozoospermia is low sperm concentration — below 16 million/mL. Mild: 10–15 M/mL. Moderate: 5–10 M/mL. Severe: <5 M/mL. Causes include varicocele, hormonal imbalances, testicular infections, and genetic factors.

    When is ICSI recommended?

    ICSI is recommended when: sperm concentration is below 5 million/mL, progressive motility is below 20%, morphology is below 1–2%, or surgical sperm retrieval (PESA/TESA) is needed. ICSI injects a single sperm directly into each egg.

    What does DNA fragmentation index (DFI) mean?

    DFI measures the percentage of sperm with damaged DNA. Normal: <15%. Borderline: 15–25%. High: >25%. High DFI is associated with recurrent miscarriage and failed IVF. Antioxidants and varicocele repair can reduce DFI.

    How long should I abstain before semen analysis?

    WHO recommends 2–7 days of abstinence. For a reliable result, abstain for 3–4 days exactly. The sample should be delivered within 30–60 minutes at body temperature.

    Can a poor semen analysis be improved?

    Yes. Quit smoking, reduce alcohol, maintain healthy BMI, avoid hot baths, take antioxidant supplements (CoQ10, Vitamin C/E, selenium, zinc, folic acid), treat varicocele. A repeat after 3 months often shows improvement. Call Mother Hospitals: 97059 93366.
