Egg Donation — Is It Right for You?

Medical Indications for Donor Eggs

• Premature Ovarian Insufficiency (POI/POF)
• Surgical menopause or cancer treatment
• Very low or undetectable AMH (<0.1 ng/mL)
• Repeated IVF failure with poor egg quality
• Advanced maternal age (>42–45) with poor own eggs
• Genetic conditions risking transmission
• Chromosomally abnormal embryos in all previous cycles

Why Donor Egg IVF Has High Success Rates

Success in IVF depends more on egg quality than the recipient's age. Young, healthy donors (typically 21–30 years) produce high-quality eggs with low aneuploidy rates. Recipient success rates of 55–70% per transfer are consistently achievable regardless of recipient age.

Process

Donor Egg IVF — Step by Step

1

Recipient Evaluation

Uterine cavity assessment (SIS or hysteroscopy), hormone profile, general health screening. The uterus must be able to support implantation — most women with ovarian failure have a normal uterus.

2

Donor Selection

Donor is matched through registered ART bank based on physical characteristics (skin tone, height, blood group). All donors are <30 years, healthy, non-smoking, medically and genetically cleared.

3

Synchronisation & Recipient Endometrial Preparation

Recipient's uterine lining is prepared with oestrogen and progesterone to reach optimal thickness (8–12 mm) and pattern (trilaminar) for implantation.

4

Donor Stimulation & Egg Retrieval

Donor is stimulated and eggs retrieved at our centre. Eggs are fertilised with partner's/donor sperm via ICSI. Embryos cultured to blastocyst stage.

5

Embryo Transfer & Pregnancy Confirmation

Best-quality embryo transferred into prepared uterus. Beta hCG test at 14 days. Success rates with fresh donor embryos: 55–70% per transfer.

Frequently Asked Questions — Egg Donation

Can I carry a pregnancy with donated eggs?

Yes. Even women with premature menopause or absent ovaries can carry a pregnancy — their uterus is typically normal. Pregnancy is supported with oestrogen and progesterone until the placenta takes over at 10–12 weeks. Women well into their 40s successfully carry donor egg pregnancies.

Will the baby be genetically related to me?

The baby will not share the mother's nuclear DNA, but will share mitochondrial DNA from the recipient's uterine environment and will be fully gestated and carried by the recipient. The baby is legally 100% the child of the recipient couple. Partner's sperm is used, making the father biologically related.

How long does the donor egg IVF process take?

From initial consultation to embryo transfer: typically 6–10 weeks. Donor selection, synchronisation, stimulation and retrieval, plus endometrial preparation takes this time. Some cycles can be faster if frozen donor eggs are available.
