🔍 What Does Hysteroscopy Find?

Uterine polyps — common, often invisible on scan. Intrauterine adhesions (Asherman's syndrome) — after D&C or infection. Uterine septum — congenital midline division. Submucous fibroids — compressing the cavity. All found and fixed in one visit.

🏥 Same-Day Procedure

No admission. Light sedation. 20–30 minutes. 1–2 hours recovery. Home by afternoon. Back to work next day (desk job). Procedure room — not full OT. Convenient for working professionals from Habsiguda/ECIL area.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is hysteroscopy different from ultrasound?
Ultrasound shows the uterus from outside — it can miss small polyps, thin adhesions, or a partial septum. Hysteroscopy is a direct camera view inside the uterus — much more accurate for uterine cavity assessment. Gold standard before IVF.
What if I had a D&C — do I need hysteroscopy?
Yes, strongly recommended. D&C (after miscarriage or MTP) can cause intrauterine adhesions in some women — Asherman's syndrome. Hysteroscopy diagnoses and treats this before you try again. Don't assume the uterus is fine after a D&C.
Is there any recovery time needed?
Light sedation wears off in 1–2 hours. Mild cramping same day — painkiller handles it. Most women feel normal by evening. Next morning: normal activity. Heavy exercise: avoid for 3–5 days. Intercourse: avoid for 1 week.
How far in advance should I book for hysteroscopy?
Book your appointment when you start your period — hysteroscopy is typically done Day 5–10 of your cycle. From Habsiguda, call 97059 93366 when your period starts and we'll schedule appropriately.
Is it safe for someone with previous C-section?
Yes. Hysteroscopy enters through the cervix — unrelated to the C-section scar (which is on the uterine wall). C-section niche (isthmocele) can actually be evaluated via hysteroscopy. Very common and safe procedure.
