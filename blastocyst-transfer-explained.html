Blastocyst Transfer Explained

Day 5 blastocyst transfer gives embryos the best chance of implantation. Understand blastocyst grading (3AA, 4AB, 5BB), why Day 5 is better than Day 3, and what your embryo's grade really means — explained by Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy, Mother Hospitals & IVF Center, Boduppal, Hyderabad.

Quick Answer: A blastocyst is a Day 5 embryo with two cell types — the inner cell mass (future baby) and trophectoderm (future placenta). Blastocysts are graded by expansion (1–6) and quality of ICM and TE (A/B/C). Top grades are 4AA or 5AA. Even 3BB or 4AB blastocysts regularly result in successful pregnancies. Day 5 blastocyst transfer has higher implantation rates than Day 3 transfer.

What Is a Blastocyst?

An embryo's journey from fertilised egg to blastocyst takes 5 days.

Key fact: In a natural conception, the embryo reaches the uterus as a blastocyst on Day 5. Blastocyst transfer synchronises the embryo and uterine lining — improving implantation.

Blastocyst Grading Table — Complete Guide

The Gardner grading system — used internationally and at Mother Hospitals — grades blastocysts by expansion stage + ICM quality + TE quality.

Day 3 vs Day 5 Transfer — Which Is Better?

🔵 Day 5 Blastocyst Transfer

  • Higher implantation rate per embryo transferred
  • Better synchrony with uterine lining
  • Self-selection — only competent embryos survive to Day 5
  • Fewer embryos transferred needed for pregnancy
  • Recommended when ≥4 embryos available on Day 3
  • Better candidates for PGT-A chromosomal testing

🟣 Day 3 Cleavage Transfer

  • Appropriate when ≤3 embryos available
  • Reduces risk of total embryo arrest in culture
  • Some embryos develop better in the uterus than in the lab
  • Good option for older patients or poor ovarian reserve
  • Same cumulative success rates in many studies
  • Embryologist decides based on your specific embryo development
Mother Hospitals policy: We aim for Day 5 blastocyst culture in all cycles where ≥4 embryos are developing. The embryologist contacts you daily with development updates and recommends the optimal transfer day for your individual cycle.

Fresh Blastocyst Transfer vs Frozen Blastocyst Transfer (FET)

Both are excellent options. The choice depends on your stimulation response and endometrial lining.

Fresh Blastocyst Transfer

  • Transfer on Day 5 of the same stimulation cycle
  • Appropriate if stimulation response was moderate (no OHSS risk)
  • Endometrial lining assessed before transfer
  • Results available within the same treatment cycle
  • Cost-effective — no additional FET cycle charge

Frozen Blastocyst Transfer (FET)

  • All blastocysts vitrified; transfer in next natural or medicated cycle
  • Recommended if stimulation was heavy (OHSS risk)
  • Lining has fully recovered from stimulation medications
  • May have equal or higher success rates than fresh in some patients
  • Vitrification survival rate >95% at Mother Hospitals
  • Extra embryos stored for future siblings

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a blastocyst and when does it form?
A blastocyst is an embryo that has reached Day 5 of development. It has two distinct cell types: the inner cell mass (ICM — becomes the baby) and the trophectoderm (TE — becomes the placenta). Blastocysts have a significantly higher implantation rate than Day 3 embryos because they have proven their developmental competence.
What does my blastocyst grade (like 4AB or 3BB) mean?
Blastocyst grading uses the Gardner system. The number (3–6) indicates expansion: 3 = full blastocyst, 4 = expanded, 5 = hatching, 6 = hatched. The first letter (A/B/C) grades the inner cell mass: A = tightly packed cells, B = loosely grouped, C = very few cells. The second letter grades the trophectoderm: A = many cohesive cells, B = few loose cells, C = very few large cells. 4AA and 5AA are top grades. 4AB, 3AA, and 4BA are all excellent. Even 3BB blastocysts frequently result in successful pregnancies.
Is Day 5 blastocyst transfer better than Day 3?
Day 5 blastocyst transfer generally has higher implantation rates per embryo transferred. The embryo is more synchronised with the uterine lining, and only developmentally competent embryos reach Day 5. However, Day 3 transfer is appropriate when fewer embryos are available — extending culture risks all embryos arresting. The embryologist decides based on your specific embryo development.
What happens if none of my embryos reach blastocyst stage?
If embryos do not reach blastocyst by Day 5, the embryologist may transfer on Day 3 instead. If all embryos arrest, this provides diagnostic information. The team will adjust the next cycle: optimise stimulation, add antioxidants, use ICSI if IVF was used, or investigate sperm DNA fragmentation.
Can a lower-grade blastocyst (3BB or 4BC) still result in pregnancy?
Yes. Many healthy pregnancies and births have resulted from lower-grade blastocysts. Grade is one factor among many — the embryo's chromosomal status (confirmed by PGT-A) and uterine environment are equally important. Never dismiss a lower-grade blastocyst without discussion with your doctor.
How long can blastocysts be stored frozen?
Vitrified blastocysts can be stored indefinitely with no significant degradation in quality. Most clinics store embryos for 5–10 years under a signed consent agreement. At Mother Hospitals, vitrified blastocysts have a survival rate >95% on thawing.
