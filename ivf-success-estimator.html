      IVF Success Rates — What the Research Shows

      How age, AMH, and diagnosis affect your IVF outcome

      IVF success rates vary significantly based on age, ovarian reserve (AMH), number of embryos available, diagnosis, and laboratory quality. Age is the single most important factor because it directly determines egg quality, which determines embryo quality and implantation potential.

      Why Age Matters Most in IVF

      As women age, the proportion of chromosomally normal eggs decreases. At 35, approximately 50% of eggs are chromosomally normal. By 40, this falls to around 30%, and by 43, only 10–15% of eggs may be normal. PGT-A (preimplantation genetic testing) can identify the best embryos, significantly improving outcomes especially in women over 38.

      What is the average IVF success rate in India by age?

These rates are higher with donor eggs. Mother Hospitals achieves rates comparable to national averages due to Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy's expertise.

      How does AMH affect IVF success?

      AMH predicts the number of eggs retrieved during IVF stimulation. Very low AMH means fewer eggs and lower retrieval success. However, AMH does not predict embryo quality — age does. Even with low AMH, some women achieve pregnancy in IVF. Age remains the primary determinant of embryo quality and IVF success.

      What is the cumulative IVF success rate over 3 cycles?

      If the per-transfer success rate is 35%, the cumulative probability over 3 cycles is approximately 1-(1-0.35)^3 = 72.5%. Studies show 80–90% of women who will succeed with IVF do so within 3 cycles. This is why multiple cycles are often recommended.

      Does PCOS affect IVF success?

      PCOS generally does not negatively affect IVF embryo quality or success rates. Women with PCOS often produce many eggs during stimulation. The main concern is ovarian hyperstimulation syndrome (OHSS). With a modified antagonist protocol and careful monitoring, PCOS patients achieve excellent IVF outcomes at Mother Hospitals.

      When should I consider donor eggs for IVF?

      Donor eggs are recommended when: AMH is very low (<0.3 ng/mL), age is over 43, there have been 3+ IVF failures with own eggs, or there is premature ovarian insufficiency. Donor egg IVF has a significantly higher success rate (55–65% per transfer) regardless of the recipient's age.
