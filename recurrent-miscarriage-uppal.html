What We Offer — Serving Uppal Patients

🧬 Chromosomal Testing (Karyotype)

Both partners' karyotype tested for chromosomal translocations or inversions that cause repeated miscarriage. Products of conception (POC) karyotype to identify embryo chromosome errors.

🩸 Thrombophilia & Blood Clotting Panel

Anti-phospholipid syndrome (APS), lupus anticoagulant, Factor V Leiden, MTHFR — clotting disorders that cause miscarriage are diagnosable and treatable with aspirin and heparin.

🏥 Uterine Anatomy Assessment

3D ultrasound and hysteroscopy to identify uterine septum, fibroids, polyps, or Asherman syndrome — anatomical causes of recurrent miscarriage that are correctable by surgery.

🔬 Immunological & Autoimmune Panel

Natural killer (NK) cell activity, thyroid antibodies, ANA, APS panel — immune-mediated pregnancy losses are treatable with immunotherapy and steroids.

📋 Hormonal Assessment

Thyroid (TSH, T3, T4), prolactin, progesterone — low progesterone or thyroid disorders are a correctable cause of early pregnancy loss.

🌱 Preimplantation Genetic Testing (PGT-A)

For couples whose RPL is chromosomally driven — PGT-A tests embryos before transfer, selecting only chromosomally normal embryos to reduce miscarriage risk significantly.

FAQs — Uppal Patients

How many miscarriages before investigation near Uppal?
Current guidelines recommend investigation after 2 consecutive pregnancy losses — you do not need to wait for 3. At Mother Hospitals near Uppal, Dr. Prashanthi begins a comprehensive RPL workup after 2 losses, rather than asking you to "try again and see."
What causes recurrent miscarriage?
Identified causes include: chromosomal abnormalities in embryos (most common, ~50%), uterine anomalies (septum, fibroids, polyps), thrombophilia (clotting disorders like APS), hormonal imbalances (thyroid, progesterone), and immunological factors (NK cell overactivity, autoimmunity).
Can recurrent miscarriage be treated near Uppal?
Yes — the majority of recurrent miscarriage cases have a treatable cause once identified. Blood thinners (heparin + aspirin) for thrombophilia, surgery for uterine anomalies, thyroid treatment, progesterone supplementation, or PGT-A embryo selection — targeted treatment significantly improves outcomes.
What is PGT-A and how does it help recurrent miscarriage?
Preimplantation Genetic Testing for Aneuploidies (PGT-A) tests IVF embryos for chromosomal abnormalities before transfer. Only chromosomally normal embryos are transferred — significantly reducing the risk of chromosomally driven miscarriage. Available at Mother Hospitals near Uppal.
