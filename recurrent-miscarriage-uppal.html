🔍 Why Does Recurrent Miscarriage Happen?

Chromosomal issues in embryo (most common, 50–60% of cases). Uterine abnormalities — polyps, septum, adhesions (15–20%). Blood clotting disorders — antiphospholipid syndrome (10–15%). Hormonal — thyroid, PCOS, prolactin. Immune factors. Male factor (sperm DNA fragmentation). Finding the cause is step 1.

🧬 RPL Investigation Panel

Karyotyping of both partners. Uterine assessment — hysteroscopy. Blood clotting tests — APS panel, factor V Leiden. Hormonal panel — thyroid, prolactin, AMH, FSH. Sperm DNA fragmentation. Sometimes PGT-A (genetic testing of embryos).

💊 Treatment by Cause

Chromosomal issues: PGT-A IVF (select only normal embryos). Uterine abnormality: hysteroscopy + correction. APS: low-dose aspirin + heparin during pregnancy. Thyroid: stabilise TSH before conception. Sperm DNA: antioxidants or ICSI with PICSI.

🧪 PGT-A — Genetic Testing of Embryos

For couples with chromosomal causes or repeated good-embryo failures — PGT-A biopsies embryos, tests all chromosomes, selects only genetically normal ones for transfer. Miscarriage rate drops significantly.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many miscarriages before starting RPL investigation?
After 2 consecutive pregnancy losses, investigation is recommended. After 3, it is standard of care. We investigate after 2 — no point waiting for a third loss when testing can find the cause now.
My chromosomes are normal — why do I keep miscarrying?
Normal karyotype means the big structural chromosomes are fine — but subtle issues (balanced translocations, microdeletions) and uterine causes are still possible. Embryo chromosomes may still be abnormal even if yours are normal. Full RPL panel + hysteroscopy typically finds the answer.
My doctor said "it's just bad luck, try again." Is that right?
For one miscarriage — often true. For two or more — it is NOT acceptable to say "just try again" without investigation. Recurrent miscarriage has identifiable causes in 50–80% of couples when fully investigated. Seek a second opinion if you've been dismissed.
Can stress cause recurrent miscarriage?
Stress alone is NOT a medically established cause of recurrent miscarriage. While high cortisol may have minor hormonal effects, stress does not cause chromosome abnormalities or uterine pathology. Please don't blame yourself — there is almost always a physical cause.
What is the success rate after RPL treatment?
Depends on cause: APS treatment success (live birth) ~75%. Uterine correction: 70–80% next pregnancy success. PGT-A IVF: 60–70% per transfer. Even without finding a cause, supportive care (progesterone, aspirin, early monitoring) improves outcomes significantly.
