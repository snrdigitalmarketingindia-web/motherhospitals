🔍 What is Hysteroscopy?

A thin telescope (hysteroscope) is passed through the cervix into the uterus — no incision needed. The uterine cavity is directly visualised. Polyps, fibroids, adhesions (Asherman's), septum — all detected and often removed in the same procedure.

⚠️ When is Hysteroscopy Needed?

Recurrent IVF failure with good-quality embryos. Recurrent miscarriage (2+ losses). Irregular bleeding, heavy periods. Suspected polyps or fibroids on ultrasound. Before starting first IVF — to confirm uterine cavity is normal.

⚕️ Procedure Details

Day procedure — no hospital admission needed. Light sedation (comfortable, not full GA). 20–30 minutes. Same-day discharge. Back to light activity next day. Polyps/septum removed in the same sitting — separate surgery not needed.

📈 Hysteroscopy Improves IVF Success

Studies show correcting uterine abnormalities before IVF improves implantation rates by 25–40%. If previous cycles failed with good embryos — uterine factor should be ruled out. Hysteroscopy gives a clear picture.

🔗 Diagnostic vs Operative

Diagnostic hysteroscopy: look and confirm. Operative hysteroscopy: look and fix in same procedure. Mother Hospitals does both — if a polyp is found, it is removed immediately. No second procedure needed.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is hysteroscopy painful?
Under light sedation: no pain during the procedure. After: mild cramping for a few hours (similar to period cramps). Painkiller tablet sufficient. Most women go home within 2 hours and are comfortable by evening.
How long before IVF should hysteroscopy be done?
Ideally 1–3 months before IVF. If polyps/adhesions are found and treated, the uterine lining needs 1–2 months to heal before embryo transfer. Dr. Prashanthi will plan your IVF timeline accordingly.
Will hysteroscopy affect my periods?
Minor procedures (small polyp removal) — periods usually return normally next cycle. Adhesion removal may cause a lighter-than-usual period for 1–2 cycles, then normal. Septum repair generally no effect on periods.
Do I need to be admitted overnight?
No — hysteroscopy is a day procedure. Arrive fasting (nil by mouth 6 hours before). Procedure 20–30 min. 1–2 hours recovery. Discharge same day. Bring someone to drive you home.
Can hysteroscopy find the reason my IVF keeps failing?
Yes — uterine cavity problems are one major reason for repeated good-embryo-transfer failures. If your lining looks fine on ultrasound but embryos don't implant — a direct look via hysteroscopy often finds subtle polyps, small adhesions, or a partial septum that ultrasound misses.
