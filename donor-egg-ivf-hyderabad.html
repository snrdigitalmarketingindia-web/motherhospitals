Who Needs Donor Egg IVF?

When Is Donor Egg IVF Recommended?

Donor egg IVF is the most effective path to pregnancy when a woman's own eggs cannot produce a viable embryo. These are the most common situations where it is recommended.

Low AMH / Diminished Ovarian Reserve

AMH below 0.5 ng/mL or very few antral follicles — when the ovaries no longer have enough eggs to produce viable embryos with own eggs, donor eggs significantly improve outcomes.

Premature Ovarian Failure (POF)

Menopause before age 40 — when ovarian function has ceased early due to autoimmune causes, genetic factors, or prior treatment, donor eggs allow pregnancy through a prepared uterus.

Advanced Maternal Age

Women over 42 where own egg quality is a limiting factor — donor eggs from a young donor dramatically improve embryo quality and success rates regardless of recipient age.

Repeated IVF Failure

3+ failed IVF cycles with poor embryo quality — when multiple own-egg IVF cycles have produced poor quality embryos or no embryos at all, donor eggs offer a genuine alternative path.

Genetic Disease

Carriers of serious genetic conditions who prefer not to use own eggs to avoid transmission — donor egg IVF removes the risk while still allowing the recipient to experience pregnancy and birth.

Surgical Removal of Ovaries

Women with no remaining ovarian tissue following oophorectomy — the uterus is intact and can receive a donor embryo with appropriate hormonal preparation.

Step by Step

Donor Egg IVF Process at Mother Hospitals

1

Consultation & Assessment

Review of previous treatment history, uterine evaluation (hysteroscopy or 3D scan), and mock cycle for ERA if implantation failure is suspected.

2

Donor Matching

Anonymous, healthy donor aged 21–30, screened for genetic diseases, infections (HIV, Hepatitis B/C, TORCH), and hormone profile. Donor program is fully ART Act 2021 compliant.

3

Synchronisation

Recipient's uterine lining is prepared with estrogen and progesterone while the donor undergoes ovarian stimulation — both are timed together.

4

Egg Retrieval from Donor

Donor undergoes oocyte pick-up (OPU) under mild sedation. Eggs are retrieved transvaginally using ultrasound guidance — a 20-minute procedure.

5

Fertilisation with ICSI

Donor eggs are fertilised with the partner's (or donor) sperm using ICSI micromanipulation. Fertilisation is confirmed at 16–18 hours.

6

Embryo Culture & Grading

3–5 day culture to blastocyst stage. Each embryo is graded for quality. Only the strongest embryo is selected for transfer; surplus embryos are vitrified.

7

Embryo Transfer

Best embryo transferred to the recipient's uterus via a fine catheter under ultrasound guidance. A simple, painless procedure — no anaesthesia required.

8

Pregnancy Test

Beta hCG blood test at 14 days post-transfer confirms pregnancy. A positive result is followed by an early viability ultrasound at 6–7 weeks.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the success rate of donor egg IVF?+

Short answer: 60–70% per embryo transfer. Why so high? Donor eggs come from young (21–30), healthy, screened women with excellent ovarian reserve. The embryo quality is superior to own eggs in women with diminished reserve or advanced age. At Mother Hospitals, recipients receive a realistic success estimate based on uterine health and individual factors — not just the standard figure.

Is donor egg IVF legal in India?+

Short answer: Yes — fully legal under ART Act 2021. Details: Donor egg IVF is regulated under the ART (Regulation) Act 2021 by ICMR. Donors must be anonymous, altruistic, and registered with the National Registry. Recipients cannot know the donor's identity. Mother Hospitals is ART Act 2021 certified and operates through an ICMR-registered donor program.

Will the baby be genetically related to me?+

Short answer: The baby carries the donor's genes and the father's genes — not the recipient mother's genetic material. However: Research in epigenetics shows that the recipient mother's uterine environment influences which genes are expressed in the developing baby (epigenetic influence). Many recipient mothers feel a deep bond from pregnancy, birth, and breastfeeding — carrying and nurturing a baby creates a profound biological and emotional connection.

Can I use a known donor — a friend or relative?+

Short answer: No — the ART Act 2021 does not permit known donors. Why: India's ART Act requires all egg donors to be anonymous to protect the rights of all parties — donor, recipient, and child. Donors must be registered through an ART bank and cannot be personally known to the recipient. Mother Hospitals works with an ART-Act-registered donor program with thoroughly screened, healthy donors.

How long does the donor egg IVF process take?+

Short answer: 6–10 weeks from donor matching to embryo transfer. Breakdown: Donor matching typically takes 1–3 weeks. Uterine preparation (estrogen and progesterone) takes 2–3 weeks. Donor stimulation is synchronised with recipient preparation. Embryo transfer occurs at the optimal point. If embryos are vitrified (frozen), transfer can be scheduled at any convenient time in a later cycle.
