Our Services

🦋 PCOS — Diagnosis First

Ultrasound (AFC count), blood tests (LH, FSH, testosterone, AMH, insulin, thyroid). PCOS confirmed when 2 of 3 criteria met: irregular periods, high androgens, polycystic ovaries on scan. Full workup in one visit.

🥗 Lifestyle — First Line

5–10% weight loss in overweight PCOS → ovulation often returns naturally. Low glycemic diet (reduce white rice, maida). 30 min daily walking. Inositol supplement. This alone helps many women conceive within 6 months.

💊 Ovulation Induction

Letrozole or clomiphene tablets (Day 2–6). Follicle monitoring scans. Timed intercourse or IUI on ovulation. 20–30% success per cycle. Simple, effective — most PCOS patients start here before considering IVF.

🔬 IUI for PCOS

If ovulation induction alone isn't working — combine with IUI. Husband's washed sperm placed directly in uterus at ovulation. Increases chance per cycle significantly. 3 cycles usually tried before IVF.

💉 IVF for PCOS

PCOS patients actually respond very well to IVF — many eggs available. Low-dose protocol to prevent OHSS (ovarian hyperstimulation). Freeze-all strategy used — transfer next cycle when OHSS risk is zero.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can PCOS be cured permanently?
PCOS is a lifelong hormonal condition — it can't be "cured" but it can be very well managed. With the right treatment, most women with PCOS achieve pregnancy, have regular periods, and live symptom-free. It doesn't have to limit your life.
I have PCOS — how long before I get pregnant?
Depends on severity and age. With ovulation induction (tablets): many women conceive within 3–6 cycles (3–6 months). With IUI: 3 cycles. With IVF: often first or second transfer. Younger patients with mild PCOS: fastest results.
Does PCOS cause miscarriage?
PCOS slightly increases miscarriage risk due to higher LH and insulin resistance. Dr. Prashanthi addresses this with progesterone support, metformin, and close early-pregnancy monitoring to reduce miscarriage risk.
What blood tests do I need for PCOS?
LH, FSH (Day 2–3 of period), Testosterone (total + free), DHEA-S, Prolactin, Thyroid (TSH, FT4), Fasting insulin + glucose (HOMA-IR), AMH. We run the full panel in one visit — results often same or next day.
Is metformin needed for PCOS treatment?
Not always. Metformin helps mainly when there is insulin resistance (high fasting insulin, HOMA-IR > 2.5). Dr. Prashanthi checks insulin levels before prescribing — targeted treatment, not blanket prescription.
