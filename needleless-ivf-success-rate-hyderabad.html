⚠️ An Important Note on Success Rate Claims

Be cautious of clinics advertising "70–80% success rates" without context. Success rates depend heavily on patient age, ovarian reserve, diagnosis, and how success is measured (biochemical vs clinical vs live birth). At Mother Hospitals, we provide honest, context-appropriate success estimates — not inflated marketing numbers.

Age & Success

Success Rate by Age Group

Age is the single most important factor in IVF success — including Needleless IVF. Egg quality declines with age, which directly affects fertilisation and embryo quality.

Age Group Needleless IVF Success Rate Eggs Retrieved (avg) Notes
Under 30 50–60% 5–10 eggs Excellent candidates. Best results, especially with PCOS.
30–34 45–55% 5–8 eggs Good candidates. Success comparable to conventional IVF.
35–37 35–45% 4–6 eggs Acceptable. May consider conventional IVF if reserve is borderline.
38–40 25–35% 3–5 eggs Success declines. Conventional IVF or PGT-A often recommended.
40–42 15–25% 2–4 eggs Lower success. Doctor will assess individually. Donor eggs may be discussed.
Over 42 Individualised 1–3 eggs Needleless IVF may not be the best option. Consultation essential.

* Figures are approximate ranges based on published literature and our clinical experience. Individual results vary. PCOS patients in the under-35 group may achieve rates at the higher end of the range.

What Affects Your Outcome

Factors That Help & Reduce Success

Understanding what affects Needleless IVF success helps you and your doctor optimise your treatment plan and set realistic expectations.

✅ Factors That Improve Success

  • Age under 35
  • Diagnosis of PCOS (good follicle pool)
  • Normal uterine anatomy (no fibroids, polyps)
  • Good ovarian reserve (AMH > 1.5, AFC > 10)
  • Normal semen parameters or ICSI-suitable sperm
  • Healthy BMI (18.5–24.9)
  • Non-smoker / no alcohol
  • Good thyroid function (TSH 1–2.5)
  • Low vitamin D deficiency
  • Blastocyst (Day 5) embryo transfer
  • First or second IVF attempt
  • Preconception lifestyle optimisation (3+ months)

△ Factors That May Reduce Success

  • Age over 38
  • Diminished ovarian reserve (AMH < 1.0, AFC < 6)
  • Poor egg or embryo quality
  • Untreated endometriosis (moderate/severe)
  • Uterine fibroids, polyps, or adhesions
  • Obesity (BMI > 32) or underweight (BMI < 18)
  • Smoking or heavy alcohol use
  • Uncontrolled thyroid or diabetes
  • Severe male factor infertility
  • Previous repeated implantation failure
  • Poor Needleless IVF response (fewer than 2 eggs)
Comparing Outcomes

Needleless IVF vs Conventional IVF — Success Rate Comparison

A common question: does giving up injections mean lower success rates? For the right patients — no.

For PCOS patients and good responders (under 35)

Needleless IVF achieves comparable clinical pregnancy rates to conventional IVF. A 2023 meta-analysis in Reproductive BioMedicine Online found oral stimulation protocols achieved similar live birth rates in normo-responders under 37, with significantly lower OHSS risk.

For PCOS patients specifically, the reduced stimulation of Needleless IVF is actually safer — eliminating the serious risk of severe OHSS (ovarian hyperstimulation syndrome) that can occur with high-dose conventional stimulation.

For poor responders and older patients (over 37)

Conventional high-dose injectable IVF generally retrieves more eggs per cycle in poor responders and older patients, giving a better chance of finding a chromosomally normal embryo. In these cases, Dr. Prashanthi Reddy will recommend conventional IVF or a modified natural cycle protocol rather than Needleless IVF.

The bottom line

Needleless IVF is not a compromise — it is the right choice for specific patients. Selecting the right protocol for the right patient is how we maximise your individual success rate. That is why a thorough consultation and fertility workup is essential before deciding on your IVF approach.

Optimise Your Outcome

How to Improve Your Needleless IVF Success Rate

Small lifestyle and health changes in the 2–3 months before your cycle can meaningfully improve egg quality, embryo quality, and implantation rates.

🥗
Mediterranean Diet
High in antioxidants, lean protein, healthy fats, and vegetables. Associated with better egg quality and embryo development in multiple studies.
⚖️
Healthy BMI
Aim for BMI 18.5–24.9. Even a 5–10% weight reduction in overweight women significantly improves IVF success rates.
🚭
Quit Smoking
Smoking dramatically reduces egg quality and IVF success rates. Quit at least 3 months before your cycle for measurable benefit.
🧘
Stress Management
Chronic stress elevates cortisol, which can impair implantation. Yoga, meditation, and adequate sleep (7–8 hrs) are well-supported approaches.
💊
Supplements
Take prescribed folic acid (400–800 mcg), CoQ10 (200–600 mg for egg quality), vitamin D if deficient, and omega-3 fatty acids. Always under doctor guidance.
🩸
Manage PCOS & Thyroid
Optimise TSH to 1–2.5 mIU/L before treatment. Control blood sugar if pre-diabetic. These significantly affect implantation and early pregnancy.
🏃
Moderate Exercise
30 minutes of moderate activity (walking, swimming, yoga) 4–5 times per week improves ovarian blood flow and reduces inflammation.
🩺
Preconception Review
A dedicated preconception appointment 2–3 months before IVF allows us to identify and address any correctable factors before your cycle begins.
Frequently Asked Questions

Success Rate — FAQs

What is the success rate of Needleless IVF in Hyderabad? +

At Mother Hospitals, Needleless IVF achieves a 40–55% clinical pregnancy rate per cycle for well-selected candidates — women under 35 with PCOS or good ovarian reserve. For older patients or those with low AMH, rates are lower and are assessed individually.

Is Needleless IVF success rate the same as conventional IVF? +

For appropriately selected patients, yes — success rates are comparable. The difference is in egg numbers retrieved (4–8 vs 8–15). For patients who need many eggs, conventional IVF may offer higher per-cycle success.

Does PCOS improve chances with Needleless IVF? +

Yes. PCOS patients often have excellent ovarian reserves and respond very well to oral stimulation. They are among the best candidates for Needleless IVF, with good egg numbers and very low OHSS risk.

What is the cumulative success rate over 2–3 Needleless IVF cycles? +

Cumulative rates improve significantly. After 2 cycles, success can reach 65–75% for suitable candidates. After 3 cycles, 75–85% in many patient groups. Frozen embryos from early cycles can also be transferred without full re-stimulation.

What happens if the first Needleless IVF cycle doesn't work? +

Dr. Prashanthi Reddy reviews your full cycle data — stimulation response, fertilisation, embryo quality, and transfer. Next steps may include another Needleless IVF cycle, switching to conventional IVF, adding PGT-A, or investigating uterine/immune factors.

How many eggs are needed for a successful Needleless IVF? +

You need at least 1 good-quality embryo for transfer. Getting 4–6 eggs typically gives a reasonable chance of generating 1–2 transferable embryos. For most Needleless IVF cycles, this is achievable, especially in women under 35.

Will my AMH level affect Needleless IVF success? +

Yes. AMH (anti-Müllerian hormone) is the best predictor of ovarian reserve and egg yield. A good AMH (above 1.5 ng/mL) means more eggs are likely with oral stimulation. Very low AMH (below 0.5) usually means fewer eggs and is often better managed with conventional high-dose IVF.

Can I improve my Needleless IVF success rate with supplements? +

Folic acid, CoQ10, vitamin D, and omega-3 supplements have supporting evidence for improving egg quality. However, take supplements only under medical supervision — not all supplements are safe or beneficial in every case. Ask Dr. Prashanthi at your consultation.
