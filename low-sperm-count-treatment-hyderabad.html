Common Causes of Low Sperm Count

Identifying the cause helps choose the most effective treatment

🩺
Varicocele (Most Common)
Dilated veins in the scrotum raise testicular temperature and impair sperm production. Found in 35–40% of infertile men. Correctable with varicocelectomy — can significantly improve sperm count.
⚗️
Hormonal Imbalance
Low testosterone, elevated FSH, or high prolactin suppress sperm production. Hormone panel (FSH, LH, testosterone, prolactin) identifies this. Treatable with hormonal medication.
🚬
Smoking & Alcohol
Smoking reduces sperm count by 20–40% and significantly damages motility and morphology. Heavy alcohol lowers testosterone. Quitting smoking improves parameters within 3 months.
🌡️
Heat Exposure
Frequent hot baths, saunas, laptops on the lap, or tight underwear raise scrotal temperature and impair sperm production. Sperm require 2–4°C below body temperature to develop normally.
💊
Anabolic Steroids
Anabolic steroids (bodybuilding supplements) severely suppress sperm production — sometimes to zero. This can persist for months or years after stopping. Always disclose steroid use to your doctor.
🧬
Genetic Causes
Y-chromosome microdeletion and Klinefelter's syndrome (47,XXY) cause severe oligospermia or azoospermia. Karyotyping and Y-deletion testing identify genetic causes, guiding further treatment.

Treatment Pathway for Low Sperm Count

Personalised step-by-step approach at Mother Hospitals Hyderabad

  • 1

    Semen Analysis + Hormone Panel

    Full semen analysis (WHO 2021) measuring count, motility, morphology, volume. Hormone panel (FSH, LH, testosterone, prolactin). Results explained in plain language — no confusing reports handed without discussion.

  • 2

    Identify & Treat the Cause

    Varicocele repair if varicocele is detected. Hormonal medication if hormones are abnormal. Lifestyle changes — quit smoking, reduce alcohol, avoid scrotal heat. Antioxidant supplements for 3 months (CoQ10, Vitamin C/E, Zinc, L-Carnitine).

  • 3

    IUI (Mild Cases)

    For mild oligospermia (5–15 million/mL) with good motility, IUI — placing concentrated sperm inside the uterus — is a lower-cost first step. Usually tried for 2–3 cycles before proceeding to IVF/ICSI.

  • 4

    IVF + ICSI (Moderate to Severe)

    For moderate or severe oligospermia, ICSI — where a single sperm is injected directly into an egg under high magnification — is the gold-standard treatment. Mother Hospitals has performed 5,000+ ICSI cycles with excellent success rates.

  • 5

    TESA / PESA if No Sperm Found

    If no sperm are found in the ejaculate (cryptozoospermia progressing to azoospermia), TESA or PESA retrieves sperm directly from the testis or epididymis for use in ICSI. Available at Mother Hospitals as an outpatient day procedure.

Why ICSI Works for Low Sperm Count

Even 1 Sperm is Enough for ICSI

In conventional IVF, hundreds of thousands of sperm compete to fertilise an egg — which rules out severe oligospermia patients. ICSI completely bypasses this barrier. A single sperm is selected — the healthiest-looking, best-moving one — and injected directly into the egg under a high-powered microscope.

  • Works with as few as 1 sperm in the sample
  • Sperm motility is less critical — the embryologist selects and injects it
  • Morphology (shape) matters less — the embryologist picks the best sperm
  • 60–80% fertilisation rate per mature egg at Mother Hospitals
  • Same day procedure for both egg collection and sperm collection
  • Cryopreservation of extra embryos for future attempts

Frequently Asked Questions — Low Sperm Count

What is low sperm count (oligospermia)?
Low sperm count, medically called oligospermia, means fewer than 16 million sperm per millilitre of semen (WHO 2021 criteria). Grades: mild (5–15 million/mL), moderate (1–5 million/mL), severe (below 1 million/mL), cryptozoospermia (only found after centrifugation). All grades are treatable with ICSI — even one sperm is enough to fertilise an egg.
Can a man with very low sperm count father a child?
Yes. Even men with extremely low sperm counts (severe oligospermia or cryptozoospermia) can father biological children through ICSI. In ICSI, a single healthy sperm is selected under a microscope and injected directly into an egg. Mother Hospitals Hyderabad has helped hundreds of men with low sperm count achieve successful pregnancies through ICSI.
What causes low sperm count?
Common causes include: varicocele (dilated scrotal veins — most common correctable cause), hormonal imbalances (low testosterone, high FSH/prolactin), past infections (mumps orchitis), heat exposure (hot baths, laptops, tight underwear), anabolic steroid use, smoking, heavy alcohol, obesity, genetic factors (Y-chromosome microdeletion), and undescended testes. A full semen analysis plus hormone panel identifies the cause.
What is the treatment for low sperm count in Hyderabad?
Treatment depends on severity: Mild oligospermia — IUI + ovulation induction. Moderate/Severe oligospermia — IVF+ICSI. All grades — antioxidant supplementation (CoQ10, Vitamin C/E, Zinc, L-Carnitine) for 3 months to improve parameters, plus hormonal treatment if indicated. Varicocele repair if varicocele is the cause. At Mother Hospitals Hyderabad, all these options are available under Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy.
How long does it take to improve sperm count naturally?
Sperm production takes approximately 72–90 days (2.5–3 months). Lifestyle improvements — quitting smoking, reducing alcohol, avoiding scrotal heat, losing weight, taking antioxidant supplements — take at least 3 months to show results in a semen analysis. During this time, IVF/ICSI can be planned in parallel if conception is urgent.
