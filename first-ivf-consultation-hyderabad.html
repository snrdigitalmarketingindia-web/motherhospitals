Step by Step

What Happens at Your First Consultation

Your 45–60 minute appointment covers everything needed to build your personalised fertility plan — no need for a second visit before you have answers.

1

Medical History Review

Dr. Prashanthi takes a detailed history covering duration of trying, previous treatments, menstrual history, medical conditions, medications, and lifestyle factors. Both partners' histories are reviewed.

2

Pelvic Ultrasound

Antral follicle count (AFC) to assess ovarian reserve, uterine cavity check, and evaluation for any fibroids or cysts. Findings are explained to you immediately during the scan.

3

Blood Tests Ordered

AMH (ovarian reserve), Day 2/3 FSH, LH, Estradiol, thyroid (TSH), prolactin, and HIV/Hepatitis screening. Results for most tests are available within 24–48 hours.

4

Semen Analysis for Partner

Sperm count, motility, and morphology assessed — sample collected same day or existing report reviewed if done within the last 3 months.

5

Diagnosis Discussion

Dr. Prashanthi explains the likely cause of infertility based on ultrasound findings and available test results in clear, understandable language.

6

Treatment Recommendation

IVF, IUI, ovulation induction, or other options are explained with pros and cons specific to your case — not a one-size-fits-all protocol.

7

Protocol Planning

If IVF is recommended: stimulation protocol, full timeline from today to embryo transfer, and medication plan are explained in detail.

8

Cost & Support

Package cost, financing options, NRI coordination logistics, and emotional support resources are all explained. No hidden charges policy.

Preparation Checklist

What to Bring to Your Appointment

Bringing these documents helps Dr. Prashanthi build a complete picture and avoid repeating tests already done.

Medical Records

Previous fertility test reports (AMH, semen analysis, HSG)
Previous treatment records (IUI cycles, prior IVF cycles if any)
OB/GYN reports, scan reports, operation notes (laparoscopy/hysteroscopy)
List of current medications

Identity & Registration

Both partners' ID proof (required for ART Act 2021 registration)
Marriage certificate (for ART registration)

Tips for Your First Visit

Come on Day 2, 3, or 4 of your period if possible — hormone tests can be done same day
Both partners should ideally attend together
Bring a written list of your questions — you will not forget them
Avoid heavy exercise on the day of the pelvic scan
Male partner: abstain from ejaculation for 2–5 days before the appointment if providing a semen sample
NRI patients: bring your local scan and lab reports — saves time and avoids repeated tests
Be Prepared

Questions to Ask Your Doctor

Write these down before you come. The consultation is your time — ask everything on your mind.

  • What is my AMH level and what does it mean for my chances?
  • What protocol do you recommend for me and why?
  • How many eggs do you expect to retrieve?
  • What is my estimated success rate for one IVF cycle?
  • Should we do PGT-A / ERA / any additional tests?
  • What lifestyle changes should I make before starting?
  • What is the timeline from today to embryo transfer?
  • What happens if the first cycle doesn't work?
Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Do both partners need to attend the first consultation?+

Ideally yes — the male partner provides a semen sample and both partners can hear the diagnosis and ask questions together. If the male partner cannot attend, a semen analysis report done within 3 months is acceptable.

How do I book a first consultation at Mother Hospitals?+

Call 97059 93366 or 97059 93355, WhatsApp 90520 74999, or fill in the form on this page. OPD timings: 10:30 AM – 1:30 PM daily. NRI patients can book a video consultation.

Should I come on a specific day of my cycle?+

If possible, come on Day 2, 3, or 4 of your period — this allows the AMH and Day-2 hormone panel (FSH, LH, Estradiol) to be done in the same visit. However, consultations are available any day of the cycle.

Will I know my diagnosis at the first visit?+

The ultrasound findings are explained immediately. Blood test results (AMH, hormones) take 24–48 hours. A follow-up call or second appointment to discuss all results and finalise the treatment plan is scheduled within 2–3 days.

Is the consultation free?+

A nominal consultation fee applies. Call 97059 93366 for current fees. Mother Hospitals offers free second-opinion consultations for couples who have already done IVF elsewhere.
