Why BMI Matters for IVF Specifically

BMI affects IVF at every stage: ovarian stimulation (overweight women often need higher FSH doses), egg retrieval (technically harder above BMI 35 due to access), egg quality (excess adipose tissue raises oestradiol and disrupts follicle development), embryo implantation (uterine lining quality and receptivity are impaired), and pregnancy outcomes (higher risk of gestational diabetes, pre-eclampsia, and miscarriage above BMI 30).

PCOS, Weight & Fertility in Indian Women

Indian women are more susceptible to metabolic effects of weight gain due to higher visceral fat at lower BMI levels (the "thin-fat Indian" phenotype). For women with PCOS, even a BMI of 23–25 can cause significant insulin resistance and hormonal disruption. Weight loss of just 5–10% can restore ovulation in up to 80% of PCOS women, making it the most cost-effective fertility intervention before IVF.

Safe Weight Loss Before IVF

Crash dieting before IVF is counterproductive — it causes nutritional deficiencies that harm egg quality. The recommended approach is: (1) moderate caloric deficit (300–500 kcal/day), (2) high-protein diet to preserve lean mass, (3) 150–200 minutes of moderate exercise per week, (4) optimising Vitamin D, folate, and iron, and (5) managing insulin resistance with Metformin or Inositol if prescribed. Aim for 0.5–1 kg loss per week. Dr. Prashanthi Reddy can create a pre-IVF weight optimisation plan for you.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the ideal BMI for IVF in India?

Most IVF clinics in India recommend a BMI between 18.5 and 30 for IVF. The sweet spot for best outcomes is BMI 20–25. For Indian women, Asian BMI cutoffs apply: normal is 18.5–22.9, overweight starts at 23, and obese at 27.5. Many clinics treat women up to BMI 35 but success rates decline above 30.

Does being overweight affect IVF success?

Yes. Obesity (BMI above 30) reduces IVF success rates by 30–50% compared to normal BMI. It causes hormonal imbalances, impairs ovarian response, lowers egg quality, increases miscarriage risk, and raises pregnancy complication rates. Even 5–10% weight loss before IVF significantly improves outcomes.

Can I do IVF if I am overweight?

Yes — most Indian IVF centres treat women with BMI up to 35. Above BMI 35–40, some centres require weight reduction before starting due to anaesthesia and retrieval risks. Dr. Prashanthi Reddy assesses each case individually and advises on pre-IVF weight optimisation.

How does PCOS affect BMI and fertility?

PCOS and obesity form a vicious cycle — insulin resistance promotes weight gain, and excess weight worsens hormonal imbalance. A 5% weight reduction can restore ovulation in up to 80% of PCOS women. Weight loss is often the most effective first-line fertility treatment for PCOS before medications or IVF.

Is being underweight bad for IVF?

Yes. Underweight (BMI below 18.5) disrupts the hormonal axis, causing irregular or absent periods. Underweight women produce less oestrogen, impairing follicle development, egg quality, and endometrial growth. Weight gain to a healthy BMI significantly improves IVF outcomes.

How much weight do I need to lose before IVF?

There is no fixed target, but losing 5–10% of body weight improves IVF outcomes for overweight and obese women. For a woman weighing 80kg, losing just 4–8kg can meaningfully improve egg quality, hormonal balance, and implantation success. Dr. Prashanthi Reddy can advise on a safe pre-IVF weight plan.
