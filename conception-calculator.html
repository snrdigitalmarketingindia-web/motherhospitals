How Conception Date Is Calculated

The difference between gestational and embryonic dating

In natural conception, the conception date is estimated by working backwards from the last menstrual period (LMP). Ovulation typically occurs 14 days after LMP in a 28-day cycle, and fertilisation happens within 24 hours of ovulation. Therefore, conception date ≈ LMP + 14 days.

IVF Dating — The Most Precise Method

In IVF, the exact fertilisation date is known from laboratory records. For a Day 5 blastocyst transfer, fertilisation was exactly 5 days before transfer. For a Day 3 transfer, fertilisation was 3 days before transfer. This makes IVF conception dating more precise than natural cycle dating.

Why Gestational and Embryonic Age Differ

Gestational age (from LMP) is always 2 weeks greater than embryonic age (from fertilisation). At a "6-week scan," the embryo is actually only 4 weeks old from conception. Both are valid dating systems — doctors use gestational age; embryologists use embryonic age in IVF.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is the conception date calculated from a due date?

The EDD is calculated as LMP + 280 days. Working backwards, the conception date is approximately EDD minus 266 days. Because ovulation can vary by several days, a ±3 day window is given for the conception date estimate.

How is conception date calculated for IVF?

In IVF, the exact fertilisation date is known. For a Day 5 blastocyst transfer, fertilisation occurred 5 days before transfer. For a Day 3 transfer, fertilisation occurred 3 days before transfer. The conception date is therefore precisely known in IVF.

What is the difference between gestational age and embryonic age?

Gestational age is measured from the first day of LMP. Embryonic age is measured from the day of fertilisation. Gestational age is always approximately 2 weeks greater than embryonic age. In IVF, both dates are precisely known.

Can I know exactly when I conceived?

In natural conception, the exact date is rarely known — this calculator provides an estimate. The conception window is typically ±3 days. In IVF, the exact fertilisation date is known from laboratory records. First-trimester ultrasound gives the most accurate pregnancy dating.

Does conception happen on the day of intercourse?

Not necessarily. Sperm can survive for up to 5 days. The egg survives only 12–24 hours after ovulation. This is why the exact conception date is an estimate in natural conception.
