Did you know? Male infertility is responsible for 40–50% of all cases where a couple cannot conceive. A low sperm count or abnormal semen report does not mean fatherhood is impossible — ICSI can achieve pregnancy even with very few or abnormal sperm.

Common Causes of Male Infertility

Diagnosed and treated at Mother Hospitals, Boduppal

📉
Low Sperm Count (Oligospermia)
Fewer than 15 million sperm per ml. Treated with ICSI — even a single healthy sperm is sufficient to fertilise an egg.
🚫
No Sperm (Azoospermia)
Complete absence of sperm in ejaculate. Obstructive azoospermia treated with TESA/PESA to retrieve sperm directly from the testis.
🐢
Poor Motility (Asthenospermia)
Sperm cannot swim effectively to reach the egg. ICSI bypasses motility completely — the sperm is injected directly.
🔬
Abnormal Morphology (Teratospermia)
Irregular sperm shape affects fertilisation ability. ICSI selects the best-shaped sperm for injection — morphology issues become manageable.
🧬
Sperm DNA Fragmentation
Damaged DNA inside sperm causes repeated IVF failure and miscarriages — even when sperm count looks normal. Tested and managed at Mother Hospitals.
⚗️
Hormonal Imbalance
Low testosterone or high FSH can impair sperm production. Hormone profile testing and medical management available.

Male Infertility Treatments at Boduppal

Evidence-based procedures performed at Mother Hospitals & IVF Center

Gold Standard

ICSI — Intracytoplasmic Sperm Injection

A single sperm is selected and injected directly into an egg using a fine needle under high magnification. ICSI is the definitive treatment for all forms of male factor infertility — low count, poor motility, abnormal shape, or surgically retrieved sperm. Mother Hospitals has performed 5,000+ ICSI cycles.

For Azoospermia

TESA — Testicular Sperm Aspiration

For men with obstructive azoospermia (no sperm in ejaculate), sperm is retrieved directly from the testis using a fine needle. The retrieved sperm is immediately used in ICSI. Performed under local anaesthesia — a day procedure with minimal discomfort.

For Azoospermia

PESA — Percutaneous Epididymal Sperm Aspiration

Sperm is aspirated from the epididymis — a tube attached to the testis — in men with obstruction. PESA is an alternative to TESA, chosen based on the specific cause and anatomy of azoospermia. Combined with ICSI for fertilisation.

First-Line Option

IUI — Intrauterine Insemination

Processed, concentrated sperm is placed directly into the uterus at the time of ovulation. Recommended for mild male factor infertility — borderline sperm count or motility. A simpler, less expensive step before moving to IVF/ICSI.

Diagnosis First

Advanced Semen Analysis

Comprehensive semen analysis at Mother Hospitals measures count, motility, morphology, volume, pH and — where indicated — sperm DNA fragmentation index (DFI). Results explained clearly. No confusing reports handed without explanation.

Pre-Treatment Support

Antioxidant & Medical Therapy

Antioxidants (CoQ10, Vitamin E, C, Selenium, L-Carnitine) reduce oxidative stress and improve sperm parameters over 3 months. Combined with hormonal treatment if required. Prescribed after reviewing semen analysis results.

Honest answers to questions men often hesitate to ask

My semen report is abnormal. Can I still have a baby?

In most cases, yes. ICSI requires only a single healthy sperm to fertilise an egg — regardless of your overall count, motility or morphology. Even men with very severe oligospermia or surgically retrieved sperm (TESA) have fathered biological children through ICSI. The only way to know your specific options is a consultation — call or WhatsApp us.

What is ICSI treatment and is it available in Boduppal?

ICSI (Intracytoplasmic Sperm Injection) is the most advanced male infertility treatment — a single sperm is injected directly into an egg under a microscope. It is the gold standard for all male factor cases. ICSI is available at Mother Hospitals & IVF Center, Boduppal, as part of an all-inclusive IVF package.

Can azoospermia (no sperm) be treated in Boduppal?

Yes. For obstructive azoospermia — where sperm is produced but cannot exit due to a blockage — TESA or PESA retrieves sperm directly from the testis or epididymis. The retrieved sperm is used in ICSI. For non-obstructive azoospermia (production failure), the options are more limited but an honest assessment is provided at consultation.

My IVF failed repeatedly despite normal sperm. Why?

Repeated IVF failure with apparently normal sperm is often caused by high sperm DNA fragmentation — damage to the genetic material inside the sperm that standard semen analysis cannot detect. At Mother Hospitals, sperm DNA fragmentation testing can identify this issue and guide targeted treatment — antioxidants, TESA-retrieved sperm, or ERA testing for the uterus.
