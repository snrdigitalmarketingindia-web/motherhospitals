Needleless IVF

Does Needleless IVF Have the Same Success Rate?

One of the most common questions we receive. The short answer: yes — for appropriate candidates.

Our Needleless IVF uses oral tablets and skin patches instead of daily injectable hormones to stimulate the ovaries. For selected patients — especially those with normal to good ovarian reserve — the egg quality and embryo development outcomes are comparable to conventional injectable IVF, while eliminating the discomfort, bruising, and anxiety of daily injections.

Who is Needleless IVF best suited for?

  • Women with normal AMH and AFC
  • PCOS patients (reduces OHSS risk)
  • Needle-phobic or anxious patients
  • Working professionals needing simpler protocols
  • Second or third IVF cycle patients

Key Factors

What Affects Your IVF Success Rate?

Success is shaped by several factors — some biological, some modifiable. Understanding these helps you prepare better.

Biological Factors

  • Age — most important single factor
  • AMH level and antral follicle count
  • Egg and embryo quality
  • Uterine lining (endometrium) thickness
  • Sperm quality (motility, morphology, DNA)
  • Presence of fibroids, polyps, or hydrosalpinx

Modifiable Lifestyle Factors

  • BMI — overweight or underweight reduces success
  • Smoking — significantly reduces egg and sperm quality
  • Alcohol — impairs embryo development
  • Stress — affects hormonal balance
  • Sleep — critical for hormonal recovery
  • Diet — antioxidant-rich foods support egg quality
Improve Your Chances

10 Ways to Improve Your IVF Success Rate

These evidence-based steps can make a meaningful difference before and during your IVF cycle.

1

Start Early — Age Matters

Every year after 35 reduces egg quality and quantity. If you're considering IVF, early consultation gives you more options and better outcomes.

2

Get a Complete Pre-IVF Workup

AMH test, antral follicle count, semen analysis, and uterine evaluation reveal correctable problems before your cycle begins.

3

Optimise Your BMI

A BMI of 18.5–25 is optimal for ovarian response and implantation. Even a 5–10% weight reduction can significantly improve outcomes for overweight patients.

4

Take Prescribed Supplements

CoQ10 (300–600mg/day), Vitamin D, folic acid, and DHEA (where recommended) support egg mitochondrial health and ovarian reserve.

5

Quit Smoking Completely

Smoking reduces ovarian reserve, impairs uterine blood flow, and increases miscarriage risk. Stopping at least 3 months before IVF is strongly recommended.

6

Eat an Antioxidant-Rich Diet

Mediterranean-style diet — rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, olive oil, and nuts — is associated with better IVF outcomes in multiple studies.

7

Manage Stress Actively

Yoga, mindfulness, or counselling during IVF reduces cortisol levels which interfere with progesterone and implantation. Our team provides in-clinic counselling support.

8

Correct Any Uterine Issues First

Submucosal fibroids, polyps, or septum should be addressed before embryo transfer. A hysteroscopy before IVF improves implantation rates by 15–20%.

9

Trust the Freeze-All Strategy

Freezing all embryos and transferring in a subsequent cycle (when the uterus is ready) often gives better success rates than a fresh transfer, particularly for PCOS patients.

10

Plan for Multiple Cycles if Needed

Cumulative success over 3 cycles is much higher than a single cycle. Our affordable packages make multi-cycle planning financially realistic for most families.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions About IVF Success Rates

What is the IVF success rate in Hyderabad?
IVF success rates in Hyderabad range from 55–65% per cycle for women under 35, declining to 35–45% for ages 35–40 and 20–30% for ages 40–43. At Mother Hospitals, our Needleless IVF protocol achieves success rates above the national average with significantly fewer injections and side-effects.
Does age affect IVF success rate?
Yes, age is the single biggest factor. Women under 35 have the highest egg quality and quantity, so IVF works best in this group. Success rates decline gradually from age 35 and more steeply after 40 due to reduced ovarian reserve and egg quality.
What is the IVF success rate for PCOS patients?
Women with PCOS actually have very good IVF outcomes because they typically produce many eggs. Success rates can reach 60–70% per cycle for PCOS patients under 35. The main risk is OHSS (ovarian hyperstimulation syndrome), which our Freeze-All protocol effectively prevents.
What is the IVF success rate for low AMH?
Low AMH reduces the number of eggs retrieved but does not eliminate IVF success. With specialised low-AMH protocols including DHA, DHEA pre-treatment, and micro-stimulation, many patients achieve live births. Success depends on egg quality, not just quantity.
How many IVF cycles does it take to get pregnant?
Most patients who succeed do so within 3 IVF cycles. Cumulative success rates over 3 cycles for women under 38 can reach 80% or higher. We plan your protocol to maximise each cycle rather than encourage repeated attempts unnecessarily.
Does Needleless IVF have a lower success rate than traditional IVF?
No. Our Needleless IVF (oral and transdermal stimulation) achieves comparable success rates to injectable IVF for appropriate candidates. The protocol is selected based on your individual profile to ensure the best outcome.
What factors affect my IVF success rate?
Key factors include your age, AMH level, antral follicle count, uterine health, sperm quality, embryo quality, and lifestyle factors like BMI, smoking, and stress. Our pre-IVF workup identifies and addresses all modifiable factors before your cycle begins.
Can I improve my IVF success rate naturally?
Yes. Optimising BMI, quitting smoking, reducing alcohol, managing stress, taking prescribed supplements (CoQ10, Vitamin D, DHEA where indicated), and following our dietary and lifestyle advice can meaningfully improve outcomes. We provide a detailed pre-IVF lifestyle guide at your consultation.
How do I know if IVF will work for me?
A thorough pre-IVF evaluation — AMH test, antral follicle count ultrasound, semen analysis, and uterine assessment — helps predict your likely response. Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy provides a personalised success estimate at your first consultation.
What is a good number of embryos to freeze for future cycles?
Having 2–4 good-quality frozen embryos from one stimulation cycle gives you a strong cumulative success rate without needing repeat egg retrievals. Our Freeze-All strategy prioritises building your embryo bank for future use.
