What We Offer — Serving Habsiguda Patients

🧊 Medicated FET Protocol

Oestrogen builds the endometrial lining over 12–14 days. Progesterone added for 5–6 days. Embryo transfer when lining is 7–10+ mm — the most controlled approach.

🌱 Natural Cycle FET

Timed to natural ovulation — no stimulation hormones. Ideal for women with regular cycles who prefer fewer medications.

🔬 ERA — Personalised Transfer Timing

Endometrial biopsy identifies your exact “window of implantation.” Crucial after FET failures — personalised transfer timing dramatically improves implantation.

📊 Freeze-All IVF Strategy

Freeze all embryos after egg retrieval — avoiding stimulation's effect on implantation. Then a dedicated FET cycle 4–6 weeks later for optimal lining.

⏰ IT-Compatible FET Monitoring

FET monitoring scans at morning OPD (10:30 AM). Only 2 key visits require time off — lining scan and transfer day. Compatible with Habsiguda work schedules.

🧬 PGT-A Euploid Embryo FET

FAQs — Habsiguda Patients

How long does an FET cycle take near Habsiguda?
Medicated FET: approximately 20–25 days. Oestrogen phase: 12–14 days (lining scan required). Progesterone phase: 5–6 days. Transfer day — 1 day. Then 2-week wait. Only 2–3 clinic visits needed — IT-compatible from Habsiguda.
What is ERA testing and should I have it near Habsiguda?
ERA (Endometrial Receptivity Array) biopsies the lining to find your personal “window of implantation.” Some women are pre-receptive or post-receptive — standard transfer timing misses the window. ERA identifies the right moment — improving implantation in women with 2+ failed FET cycles despite good embryos.
What if my endometrium is thin for FET near Habsiguda?
A thin endometrium (below 7 mm) is associated with lower FET success. Strategies to improve lining: higher oestrogen dose, vaginal sildenafil, G-CSF (granulocyte-colony stimulating factor) instillation, low-dose aspirin, and acupuncture. Dr. Prashanthi works through these options systematically.
Can I do FET if my embryos were frozen elsewhere?
Yes — embryos frozen at another clinic can be transported to Mother Hospitals for FET (cryotransport). Contact us to discuss the embryo transport process from your previous clinic.
