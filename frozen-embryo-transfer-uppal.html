What We Offer — Serving Uppal Patients

❄️ Natural Cycle FET

Embryo is transferred in the natural menstrual cycle — around ovulation — without heavy hormone medication. Best for women with regular cycles and good endometrial lining.

💊 Medicated (Hormone Replacement) FET

Oestrogen and progesterone tablets prepare the uterine lining artificially — giving precise control over the transfer window. Suitable for women with irregular cycles or after hysteroscopy.

🔬 ERA-Guided FET

Endometrial Receptivity Array (ERA) test identifies your personal implantation window — so the embryo is transferred at the exact right time. Significantly improves FET success rates in women with previous implantation failure.

🌱 Vitrified Embryo Thaw

Our embryology team uses the latest warming protocols for vitrified embryos — achieving 95%+ embryo survival on thaw.

🏥 Endometrial Preparation Monitoring

Serial ultrasound monitoring of endometrial thickness and pattern. Transfer only when endometrium is optimal (triple-line pattern, minimum 7 mm).

📋 Luteal Phase Support

Progesterone supplementation from transfer day — personalised dosage and duration based on your lining, embryo quality, and progesterone levels.

FAQs — Uppal Patients

How does FET work near Uppal?
In a FET cycle near Uppal, previously frozen embryos from your IVF cycle are thawed and transferred to your uterus. The uterine lining is prepared either naturally (following your ovulation) or with oestrogen + progesterone tablets. Transfer takes about 5 minutes — like a Pap smear — no anaesthesia required.
Is FET success rate good near Uppal?
FET success rates at Mother Hospitals are comparable to fresh IVF transfer — and in many cases better, because the uterus has a full cycle to recover from stimulation before the embryo is transferred. Blastocyst FET (Day 5 embryo) success rates are particularly good.
What is the best type of FET — natural or medicated near Uppal?
Both have similar success rates. Natural cycle FET (following ovulation) has fewer medications but requires cycle monitoring. Medicated FET provides precise control over timing. Dr. Prashanthi selects the best approach based on your cycle regularity and previous history.
What is ERA and do I need it for FET near Uppal?
ERA (Endometrial Receptivity Array) is a biopsy that identifies your personal window of implantation — which varies between women. If you have had 2+ failed FETs with good embryos, ERA is strongly recommended. It changes the transfer timing by 12–24 hours, significantly improving success.
