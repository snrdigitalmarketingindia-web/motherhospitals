      Pregnancy Week by Week

      What happens inside your body during each stage of pregnancy

      Pregnancy is divided into three trimesters, each with distinct developmental milestones. The first trimester (Weeks 1-13) is when all major organs form. The second trimester (Weeks 14-27) is often the most comfortable — nausea fades and the baby grows rapidly. The third trimester (Weeks 28-40) focuses on growth, lung maturation, and delivery preparation.

      Why Regular Scans Matter

      Antenatal scans track baby's growth, check amniotic fluid, assess placental position, and screen for potential problems early when they are most treatable. Mother Hospitals provides comprehensive antenatal monitoring with experienced sonographers and Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy's obstetric expertise.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      How is gestational age calculated in pregnancy?

      Gestational age is counted from the first day of the last menstrual period (LMP), even though conception occurs approximately 14 days later. A full-term pregnancy is 40 weeks (280 days) from LMP. Week 1-2 technically precede conception. This convention is used because the LMP date is more reliably known than the exact conception date.

      When will I feel my baby move?

      First-time mothers typically feel baby movements (quickening) between 18-22 weeks. Women who have been pregnant before may notice movements as early as 16 weeks. Movements feel like flutters, bubbles, or gentle taps initially. By 28 weeks, movements should be regular — at least 10 movements within 2 hours (kick counting).

      When should I have my first scan?

      The first scan (dating/viability scan) should ideally be done between 7-9 weeks to confirm heartbeat, dating, and rule out ectopic pregnancy. The nuchal translucency scan is at 11-14 weeks. The anomaly (anatomy) scan is at 18-22 weeks. Growth scans are typically at 28, 32, and 36 weeks.

      What is the difference between trimesters?

      First trimester (Weeks 1-13): All organs form; highest miscarriage risk; extreme fatigue and nausea common. Second trimester (Weeks 14-27): Nausea improves, energy returns, baby grows rapidly; anatomy scan at Week 20. Third trimester (Weeks 28-40): Baby gains weight, lung maturation; delivery preparation begins; term from Week 37.

      Is it safe to exercise during pregnancy?

      Yes — moderate exercise is safe and beneficial during a normal pregnancy. Walking, swimming, prenatal yoga, and stationary cycling are excellent choices. Exercise improves mood, reduces gestational diabetes risk, eases back pain, and prepares the body for labour. Avoid contact sports and high-impact activities after 16 weeks.

      What foods should I avoid during pregnancy?

      Avoid: raw or undercooked meat and eggs, unpasteurised dairy products, high-mercury fish (shark, swordfish), liver products (excess Vitamin A), raw sprouts, and excess caffeine (limit to 200mg/day).
