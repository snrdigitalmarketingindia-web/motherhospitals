Understanding the Procedure

What Is Injection-Free IVF?

Injection-Free IVF — also called Needleless IVF or Oral IVF — replaces the daily injectable hormone injections used in conventional IVF with oral tablet medications (letrozole, clomiphene) to stimulate the ovaries. The rest of the procedure — egg retrieval under anaesthesia, ICSI fertilisation, embryo culture, and embryo transfer — is identical to conventional IVF.

The result: a gentler, more comfortable fertility journey that eliminates needle anxiety while delivering comparable outcomes in suitable candidates.

How It Works — Step by Step

The Injection-Free IVF Process

From your first consultation to the pregnancy test — here is exactly what to expect at Mother Hospitals.

1

Fertility Assessment & Consultation

Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy reviews your full fertility profile — AMH levels, antral follicle count (AFC) scan, hormone panel, sperm analysis, and medical history — to confirm you are a suitable candidate for the oral protocol.

2

Oral Stimulation (Days 2–12 of Cycle)

You take oral tablets (letrozole or clomiphene) daily at home for 8–12 days. These gently stimulate the ovaries to develop follicles — without injections.

3

Follicle Monitoring Scans

Ultrasound scans at our Boduppal centre track follicle growth and check your response to the oral medication. Your doctor adjusts the protocol if needed.

4

Trigger & Egg Retrieval (OPU)

When follicles are mature, a trigger medication is given. Egg retrieval (OPU) is performed under anaesthesia 36 hours later — a minor day procedure. You go home the same day.

5

ICSI Fertilisation & Embryo Culture

Eggs are fertilised with partner sperm using ICSI (intracytoplasmic sperm injection) in our embryology laboratory. Embryos are cultured for 3–5 days to blastocyst stage.

6

Embryo Transfer & Pregnancy Test

The best quality embryo is transferred to the uterus — a painless procedure taking under 15 minutes. A blood pregnancy test (beta hCG) is done 14 days later.

Is It Right for You?

Who Is a Good Candidate?

Injection-Free IVF is not one-size-fits-all. Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy carefully evaluates each patient before recommending the oral protocol.

✅ Ideal Candidates

  • Women with PCOS — lower OHSS risk with oral stimulation
  • Needle phobia or severe injection anxiety
  • Normal to good ovarian reserve (AMH ≥ 1.0)
  • Younger women (under 38) with good egg quality
  • Those preferring a gentler, less medicalised approach
  • Patients who want faster recovery between cycles
  • Women for whom conventional high stimulation is not advised

ℹ️ When Conventional IVF May Be Better

  • Low ovarian reserve (very low AMH or low AFC)
  • Advanced maternal age (above 40) needing more eggs
  • Previous poor response to stimulation
  • When PGT-A genetic testing requires more embryos
  • Specific medical conditions requiring higher egg yield

Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy will guide you to the right protocol for your unique clinical profile.

Frequently Asked Questions

Injection-Free IVF — FAQs

What exactly is injection-free IVF? +

Injection-free IVF uses oral medications (letrozole or clomiphene tablets) instead of daily injectable gonadotropins to stimulate the ovaries. The egg retrieval, ICSI fertilisation, embryo culture, and embryo transfer steps remain identical to conventional IVF — only the stimulation method changes.

Is injection-free IVF available in Hyderabad? +

Yes. Mother Hospitals & IVF Center at Boduppal, Hyderabad offers Injection-Free (Needleless) IVF under the expert care of Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy. Call 97059 93366 to book a consultation and find out if you are a suitable candidate.

Am I a candidate for injection-free IVF? +

Ideal candidates are women with PCOS, normal to good ovarian reserve (AMH ≥ 1.0), needle phobia, or those seeking a gentler approach. Women with very low AMH or advanced age may be better suited to conventional high-stimulation IVF. Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy will assess your complete fertility profile at consultation.

How many eggs does injection-free IVF produce? +

Typically 3–8 eggs per cycle. While this is fewer than conventional IVF, the eggs are often high quality in suitable candidates. The philosophy is quality over quantity — one or two high-quality blastocysts are all that is needed for a successful transfer.

Is injection-free IVF safe for PCOS patients? +

Injection-free IVF is particularly well-suited for PCOS patients. The oral stimulation protocol dramatically reduces the risk of Ovarian Hyperstimulation Syndrome (OHSS) — a potentially serious complication that is more common in PCOS patients with conventional injectable stimulation.

What is the success rate of injection-free IVF at Mother Hospitals? +

For suitable candidates, our success rates are comparable to conventional IVF. The exact expected success rate depends on your age, AMH, AFC, embryo quality, and uterine factors. Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy will give you a personalised success estimate at your first consultation based on your specific profile.

Does the egg retrieval still require anaesthesia? +

Yes. The egg retrieval (OPU) procedure is performed under light sedation (anaesthesia) regardless of stimulation protocol. It is a minor day procedure — you are comfortable throughout and go home the same day, typically within 2–3 hours of arrival.
