Our Services

🔍 RPL — What We Test For

Chromosomal: karyotyping of both partners, PGT-A of embryos. Uterine: hysteroscopy for polyps, septum, adhesions. Immune: antiphospholipid antibodies, NK cell activity. Hormonal: thyroid, prolactin, PCOS markers. Male: sperm DNA fragmentation. Thrombophilia panel.

🧬 Chromosomal Testing

Even if you and your husband have normal chromosomes — embryo chromosomes may be abnormal. PGT-A biopsies embryos at Day 5 and tests all 24 chromosomes. Only genetically normal embryos transferred. Miscarriage rate drops from 30–50% to under 5%.

💉 Blood Clotting Disorders

Antiphospholipid syndrome (APS) — causes clots in placenta, cutting off blood to embryo. Treated with low-dose aspirin + heparin injections during pregnancy. Simple, effective. Success rate 75%+ with treatment. Often missed without proper testing.

🏥 Hysteroscopy — Uterine Cause

Uterine septum, polyps, adhesions — all cause recurrent miscarriage. Found and fixed via hysteroscopy. Day procedure at Mother Hospitals — no admission. If found, corrected in same sitting.

🧪 Progesterone Support Protocol

For unexplained RPL, progesterone supplementation from early pregnancy reduces miscarriage risk significantly (PROMISE trial evidence). Dr. Prashanthi starts progesterone at first positive pregnancy test and monitors closely through first trimester.

Frequently Asked Questions

What tests should I bring to first consultation?
Any previous pregnancy test reports, ultrasound scans from previous pregnancies, previous blood test reports if any, and husband's semen analysis if done. Don't worry if you don't have them — we can arrange everything fresh. Just come.
My thyroid was slightly off — can that cause miscarriage?
Yes — hypothyroidism (even subclinical, TSH above 2.5 in pregnancy) increases miscarriage risk. Thyroid must be properly controlled (TSH under 2.5) before conception. Simple to treat with medication. Often overlooked cause of RPL.
Can PCOS cause recurrent miscarriage?
Yes — elevated LH, insulin resistance, and androgen excess in PCOS can interfere with implantation and early pregnancy. Managing PCOS before conception (weight, metformin, inositol) reduces miscarriage risk.
After 2 miscarriages, how long before I can try again?
Physically: most doctors recommend waiting 1–3 months (allow one normal period). At Mother Hospitals: we use this time to complete RPL investigation — so by the time you're ready to try, you have a plan. Don't wait without investigating.
Is there hope after 4+ miscarriages?
Yes. Even after 4–5 losses, most couples successfully carry a pregnancy with proper diagnosis and treatment. PGT-A IVF, APS treatment, progesterone support — these interventions have strong evidence. Please don't give up before a full investigation.
