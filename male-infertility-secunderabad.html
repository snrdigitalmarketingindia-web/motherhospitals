Our Services

🔬 Semen Analysis — First Step

Count, motility, morphology, volume, pH — all measured against WHO 2021 reference values. Results in 2–4 hours. Private sample collection room at Mother Hospitals. If abnormal: repeat + sperm DNA fragmentation test.

📉 What Causes Low Sperm Count?

Varicocele (scrotum vein swelling) — most common, treatable. Hormonal imbalance (low testosterone, high FSH). Infection/inflammation history. Lifestyle: smoking, alcohol, heat, obesity. Genetic (Y-chromosome microdeletion). Identifying cause guides treatment.

🧬 ICSI — Works Even With Very Low Count

Single sperm injected directly into egg. Works with count as low as 100,000 or even less. Fertilisation rate 70–80%. Most IVF cycles at Mother Hospitals include ICSI — no reason for a man with low count to lose hope.

⚕️ TESA for Zero Sperm (Azoospermia)

No sperm in semen? TESA retrieves sperm from testis under local anaesthesia. 30–50% of non-obstructive azoospermia yields sperm. Obstructive azoospermia (blocked tubes) — near-100% TESA success. Same-day ICSI with retrieved sperm.

🧪 Sperm DNA Fragmentation Test

Normal count + poor fertilisation or recurrent miscarriage? Sperm DNA fragmentation may be the hidden cause. Test: SDF index. If high: antioxidants, lifestyle changes, PICSI ICSI. A common missed diagnosis.

💊 Lifestyle & Medical Treatment

Varicocele repair. Hormonal treatment (clomiphene, FSH injections). Antioxidants (CoQ10, zinc, selenium, vitamin C). Smoking cessation, weight loss, heat avoidance. 3-month treatment + retest before deciding IVF.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can male infertility be treated near Secunderabad at Mother Hospitals?
Yes. Mother Hospitals handles complete male infertility workup and treatment — semen analysis, sperm DNA fragmentation, hormonal testing, TESA, ICSI, PICSI — all at Boduppal, 35 minutes from Secunderabad. Dr. Prashanthi treats male factor with the same expertise as female factor.
My sperm count is 3 million — is ICSI the only option?
With 3 million total count, ICSI is the best path to pregnancy. IUI requires 5–10 million washed motile sperm — which 3 million total may not provide. ICSI injects a single sperm directly into the egg — fertilisation rate 70–80%. Very effective.
Does TESA hurt?
TESA is done under local anaesthesia — you're awake but feel no pain during the procedure. Mild soreness for 1–2 days after. Painkillers handle it easily. 2–3 days rest, then normal activity. Most men are surprised at how manageable it is.
Does the husband need to come for every visit?
For semen analysis: yes. For wife's IVF monitoring scans: no — wife comes alone. For egg retrieval day and IUI day: husband provides fresh sample at the clinic. For consultation and results review: ideally both come together.
Sperm count improved after 3 months of treatment — is IVF still needed?
If count improved to 15M+ with good motility — IUI is possible and can be tried first. If count improved but still below 5–10M washed — ICSI IVF is still recommended for best results. Dr. Prashanthi reviews the post-treatment semen analysis to advise.
