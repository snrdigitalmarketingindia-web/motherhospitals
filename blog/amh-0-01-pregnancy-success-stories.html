AMH 0.01 and Pregnancy — Is It Possible? What Happens at This Level?

AMH 0.01 is undetectable on most assays. Most clinics immediately say donor eggs. But the decision is not always that simple. Dr. Prashanthi Reddy explains the full picture — when own-egg IVF is still worth trying, and when donor egg IVF is the wiser path.

Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy

MBBS, DGO · Diploma in ART (Kiel University, Germany) · TGMC Reg: 50624 · 20+ Years

AMH Below 0.1 — Is Pregnancy Still Possible?

AMH 0.01 represents the lowest detectable range on most AMH assays. It is sometimes reported as "<0.1" or "undetectable." For most fertility clinics, this triggers an immediate recommendation for donor egg IVF. But that is not always the only path.

At Mother Hospitals, Dr. Prashanthi Reddy evaluates every extreme low AMH patient individually — because AMH 0.01 does not always mean zero follicles. Some patients with "undetectable" AMH still have 1–3 antral follicles visible on ultrasound, and those follicles can produce viable eggs.

Who Gets AMH This Low?

  • Women with premature ovarian insufficiency (POI) — ovaries failing before natural menopause age
  • Women who had ovarian surgery (cystectomy, especially bilateral) damaging ovarian tissue
  • Women after chemotherapy or pelvic radiation
  • Women with Turner syndrome or other chromosomal conditions
  • Rarely — otherwise healthy women with idiopathic (unexplained) very early ovarian aging

What Happens When You Try IVF with AMH 0.01?

The honest answer: the cycle may cancel. If stimulation is started and no follicles grow, egg retrieval cannot happen. This is a real risk that Dr. Prashanthi Reddy explains clearly before starting.

However, in some cases with AMH 0.01:

  • 1–2 follicles still respond to FSH stimulation
  • 1 egg is retrieved, fertilised via ICSI, and develops to blastocyst
  • Transfer results in pregnancy

These are not common outcomes — but they do happen. And each cycle attempt gives information that guides the next step.

DHEA Priming for AMH 0.01

DHEA is worth trying even at AMH 0.01 — especially for women under 38. Cases of AMH rising from 0.01 to 0.3–0.5 after 90 days of DHEA are documented in literature (though not guaranteed). Dr. Prashanthi Reddy monitors DHEAS levels to ensure DHEA is being metabolised correctly before IVF stimulation.

The Honest Decision Tree at AMH 0.01

  1. Check AFC on ultrasound — If zero antral follicles visible, own-egg IVF is very unlikely to succeed. Move to step 4.
  2. If 1–3 follicles visible — Try DHEA priming for 90 days, then antagonist IVF. One attempt is reasonable.
  3. If IVF cycle cancels or no embryo results — Repeat once more with modified protocol, or proceed to step 4.
  4. Donor Egg IVF — ART Act 2021 compliant anonymous donor. Your uterus is healthy; AMH doesn't affect implantation. Success rate 55–65% per transfer regardless of your AMH.

Donor Egg IVF — Not a Failure, a Strategy

Many patients feel donor egg IVF represents "giving up." Dr. Prashanthi Reddy reframes this clearly: your uterus, your pregnancy, your baby. The egg is different, but everything else — implantation, carrying, delivering, feeding — is entirely yours. And the success rate is the same as a 25-year-old's own eggs regardless of your age or AMH.

Donor egg IVF at Mother Hospitals is fully anonymous, ART Act 2021 compliant, and handled with complete confidentiality.

Real Patient Outcomes at Mother Hospitals

Dr. Prashanthi Reddy has treated patients with AMH as low as 0.01–0.05 who have:

  • Conceived after DHEA priming brought AMH up to 0.2–0.3 (allowing IVF)
  • Successfully conceived via own-egg IVF retrieving 1–2 eggs
  • Delivered healthy babies via donor egg IVF

There is no single answer that fits every AMH 0.01 patient. The right answer depends on your age, AFC, uterine health, and how many attempts you are willing to make.
