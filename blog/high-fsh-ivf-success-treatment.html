FSH 15, 18, 20 or Higher — Can You Get Pregnant? IVF Success Rates

FSH 15, 18, 20+ does not automatically mean the end of own-egg IVF. The number tells you reserve is low — not that eggs are gone. Here is what the success rates look like and exactly what Dr. Prashanthi Reddy does differently.

What Is FSH and Why Does the Number Matter?

FSH (Follicle Stimulating Hormone) is the hormone your pituitary gland releases to stimulate the ovaries to produce eggs. When ovarian reserve drops, the brain senses fewer follicles responding and turns up the FSH signal — like shouting louder at someone who can't hear well. A high Day 3 FSH is therefore a warning signal, not a disease itself.

Normal Day 3 FSH: <10 mIU/mL  |  Mildly elevated: 10–14.9  |  High: 15–24.9  |  Very high: ≥25

Important caveat: FSH fluctuates cycle to cycle. A single high value does not seal your fate. At Mother Hospitals, Dr. Prashanthi Reddy always interprets FSH alongside AMH (which does not fluctuate) and AFC (antral follicle count on ultrasound) before drawing conclusions.

FSH 15 — Can I Get Pregnant or Do IVF?

FSH 15 is elevated but not hopeless. Success depends heavily on age and AFC:

  • Under 35 with FSH 15 and AFC ≥6: IVF is worth attempting. Poor response is likely but not certain.
  • 35–38 with FSH 15: Proceed to IVF without delay — time is the real constraint now.
  • Over 40 with FSH 15: Donor egg IVF gives a much better success rate than own-egg at this combination.

FSH 25 or Higher — What Are the Options?

At FSH 25+, own-egg IVF is very challenging — but not always impossible. The protocol at Mother Hospitals for FSH 25+:

  1. Retest the FSH — check on cycle Day 2 or 3 the following month. FSH can normalise between cycles.
  2. Check AMH — if AMH is 0.5–1.0 despite high FSH, IVF is still worth attempting. If AMH is <0.1, donor egg IVF is the realistic path.
  3. Estrogen priming — adding estradiol in the late luteal phase before stimulation can suppress FSH to a more manageable level before trigger.
  4. DHEA + CoQ10 priming — 90-day protocol before IVF to improve follicular response.
  5. Mini-IVF / Mild stimulation — lower doses, less aggressive. Aim for quality over quantity.

Does High FSH Mean Menopause?

Not automatically. Perimenopausal FSH values are consistently >40 mIU/mL across multiple tests, with absent periods. A single FSH of 15–25 in a woman with regular cycles is poor ovarian reserve — not menopause. Still treatable with the right approach.

What Reduces FSH Naturally?

FSH cannot be reliably "reduced" by lifestyle — it is a consequence of low follicle count, not a cause. DHEA priming can sometimes improve ovarian response despite high FSH by improving the quality of remaining follicles. Acupuncture has no consistent evidence.
