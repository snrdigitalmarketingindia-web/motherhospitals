10 Common IVF Myths Debunked — Facts Every Couple Should Know

✍️ Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy📅 2026-05-17⏱️ 7 min read
IVF MythsFactsInfertilityIndiaHyderabad

Misinformation about IVF is rampant — especially in India, where social stigma and old beliefs create a fog of confusion around fertility treatment. As a result, many couples either avoid seeking help for years, or walk in with completely wrong expectations. Let's clear the air.

Myth 1: "IVF Babies are Different from Normal Babies"

FACT: IVF babies are completely normal. Decades of research on millions of IVF-conceived children worldwide show no difference in health, intelligence, or development compared to naturally conceived children. The fertilisation happens in a lab, but everything after that — pregnancy, development, birth — is identical to natural conception.

Myth 2: "IVF Always Works on the First Try"

FACT: IVF success rates range from 40-60%+ per transfer depending on age and diagnosis. This means a meaningful percentage of patients will need more than one cycle. This is not a failure — it is the nature of the treatment. Many successful pregnancies happen on the 2nd or 3rd cycle with protocol adjustments.

Myth 3: "IVF is Only for Women Over 35"

FACT: IVF is indicated for any woman or couple with fertility challenges that cannot be addressed by simpler treatments — regardless of age. Blocked tubes, severe male factor infertility, and PCOS with failed ovulation induction are reasons for IVF in women in their 20s too.

Myth 4: "IVF is Extremely Painful"

FACT: While traditional IVF involves daily injections for 10-14 days, the discomfort is manageable and temporary. The egg retrieval is done under sedation — you feel nothing. At Mother Hospitals, we offer Needleless IVF — eliminating the daily injections entirely for eligible patients.

Myth 5: "IVF is Not Natural — It's Playing God"

FACT: IVF assists nature — it doesn't replace it. The sperm and egg belong to the couple (in most cases). The embryo is transferred to the mother's own womb. The pregnancy, birth, and parenting are completely natural. Most major religious authorities around the world permit IVF for married couples.

Myth 6: "IVF Children Have More Health Problems"

FACT: Extensive studies show IVF children are as healthy as naturally conceived children. The slight statistical increase in certain complications (like preterm birth) in early studies was largely due to the then-common practice of transferring multiple embryos. With modern single embryo transfer (SET) practice, outcomes are virtually identical to natural conception.

Myth 7: "You Can Do IVF at Any Age — No Rush"

FACT: Age is the single most important factor in IVF success. Every year of delay after 35 significantly reduces both egg quality and quantity. A 38-year-old who delays until 42 may have a very different outcome than if she had sought treatment at 38. Do not delay fertility evaluation and treatment.

Myth 8: "IVF Always Leads to Twins or Triplets"

FACT: Modern IVF practice — including at Mother Hospitals — emphasises single embryo transfer (SET) to avoid multiple pregnancies. Twins from IVF are not inevitable; they result only when multiple embryos are transferred. SET gives excellent outcomes while keeping pregnancy safe.

Myth 9: "Stress Causes IVF to Fail"

FACT: Moderate stress does not cause IVF to fail. While extreme chronic stress may have some hormonal impact, the vast majority of studies show no direct link between stress and IVF failure. Do not blame yourself if a cycle is unsuccessful. Seek counselling support — it helps your emotional wellbeing, not your hormone levels.

Myth 10: "IVF is Too Expensive — Only for the Rich"

FACT: IVF costs in India are a fraction of what they cost in the US or Europe. At Mother Hospitals, our all-inclusive ₹99,000 IVF package makes this treatment accessible for middle-class families. EMI options are also available. The cost of NOT treating infertility — emotionally and socially — can be far greater.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are IVF babies normal?

Yes — completely. Extensive worldwide research shows IVF babies have the same health, intelligence, and development as naturally conceived children. There is no meaningful difference.

Does IVF always work on the first try?

No. IVF success rates are 40-60%+ per transfer depending on age and diagnosis. Some patients conceive on the first cycle; others need 2-3 cycles. Each cycle provides valuable information to improve the next.

Is IVF painful?

Traditional IVF involves daily injections (manageable) and egg retrieval under mild sedation (painless). Mother Hospitals offers Needleless IVF, eliminating the daily injections for eligible patients.

Will IVF result in twins?

Not necessarily. Modern single embryo transfer (SET) practice means IVF does not have to result in twins. Multiple pregnancies only occur when more than one embryo is transferred.

Is there an age limit for IVF in India?

Yes. Under ART Act 2021, IVF with own eggs is performed for women up to 50 years. Donor egg IVF may be available up to 45-50 years at certified centres. The best outcomes are for women under 38.
