3. ART Act 2021 Certification

A good fertility doctor will give you realistic success rate expectations based on your specific age, AMH, and diagnosis — not inflated promises. Dr. Prashanthi is known for transparent, patient-first communication in every consultation.

First Fertility Consultation — What to Expect

If you have never seen a fertility specialist before, the first appointment can feel daunting. Here is exactly what happens at your first fertility consultation with Dr. Prashanthi at Mother Hospitals:

  1. Detailed History: Duration of trying, menstrual cycle regularity, previous pregnancies or miscarriages, past surgeries, existing medical conditions, and medications.
  2. Basic Tests Ordered:
    • AMH (Anti-Müllerian Hormone): A blood test that estimates ovarian reserve — how many eggs remain.
    • AFC (Antral Follicle Count): Transvaginal ultrasound to count resting follicles — a direct measure of egg reserve.
    • Semen Analysis: For the male partner — count, motility, morphology, and DNA fragmentation if indicated.
  3. Review of Previous Records: Any prior test reports, IUI cycles, or previous IVF records are reviewed and factored into the plan.
  4. Personalised Treatment Plan: Based on all findings, Dr. Prashanthi discusses treatment options — whether that is timed intercourse, IUI, IVF, ICSI, or a specialised protocol — along with realistic success expectations and cost transparency.

The consultation is unhurried. You are encouraged to ask every question you have.

When Should You See a Fertility Specialist?

Many couples wait too long before seeking help. Here are the clinical guidelines:

  • Under 35 years: If you have been trying to conceive for 12 months without success, see a specialist.
  • Over 35 years: Consult after just 6 months of trying — ovarian reserve declines faster after 35.
  • Immediately, regardless of duration, if:
    • Known PCOS or endometriosis
    • Irregular or absent periods
    • Known male factor (low sperm count, azoospermia)
    • Previous pelvic surgery or blocked tubes
  • After 2 or more miscarriages: Recurrent pregnancy loss requires specialist evaluation — don't keep trying without finding the cause.

Remember: Time is an important factor in fertility. Ovarian reserve does not wait. If you have any doubts, an early consultation costs very little — but waiting can reduce your options significantly.

Questions to Ask Your Fertility Doctor

Walking into a consultation prepared makes a significant difference. Here are 8 important questions to ask any fertility doctor near Uppal or Boduppal:

  1. "What is my personal success rate for IVF given my age and AMH?" — Not the clinic average. Your specific number.
  2. "How many IVF cycles do you recommend I plan for?" — Understand realistic expectations upfront.
  3. "What is included in the IVF package? Are injections, scans, and ICSI extra?" — Avoid hidden cost surprises.
  4. "What happens if the first cycle fails?" — Ask about the review process and protocol changes.
  5. "Do you offer frozen embryo transfer (FET)?" — Frozen transfers often yield better results in certain patients.
  6. "Is your clinic ART Act 2021 certified?" — Non-compliance is a red flag.
  7. "What is your fertilisation rate and blastocyst development rate?" — These reveal lab quality.
  8. "Can I have consultations in Telugu?" — Comfort in communication is important for accurate history-giving.
