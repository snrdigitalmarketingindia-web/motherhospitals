IVF at 38, 40, 42 — Realistic Success Rates and What to Do Differently

At 38, 40, 42 — own-egg IVF is still possible but the success rates, protocols, and decisions are different. Here are the honest numbers, the DHEA + PGT-A approach, and when Dr. Prashanthi Reddy recommends donor egg IVF.

Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy

Fertility After 38 — Why Age Accelerates After 35

Female fertility declines gradually until 32, more rapidly from 32–37, then steeply after 38. The reason is egg quality — specifically the increasing rate of chromosomal errors (aneuploidy) in eggs as women age. At 38, approximately 50–60% of eggs are chromosomally abnormal. At 42, this rises to 80–90%.

This is why IVF success rates drop sharply with age — more eggs retrieved, but fewer that produce viable embryos. And it is why time urgency is real: each month's delay at 38+ corresponds to measurably lower chances.

IVF at 38 — Own Eggs vs Donor Eggs

At 38, own-egg IVF is still the right first choice if AMH is above 0.5 and AFC is 5+. Multiple cycles may be needed — but the cumulative success over 2–3 cycles at 38 is 60–75%. PGT-A (genetic testing of embryos before transfer) is strongly recommended at 38 to select only chromosomally normal embryos, significantly reducing miscarriage rate.

IVF at 40 — Is It Too Late?

No. 40 is not too late. Women in their early 40s conceive via IVF regularly at Mother Hospitals. The considerations at 40:

  • Start immediately — do not wait months for lifestyle optimisation
  • DHEA + CoQ10 for 60–90 days before IVF to maximise egg quality
  • PGT-A on all embryos — at 40, the chance of an unselected embryo being chromosomally normal is ~40–50%
  • Embryo banking across 2 retrievals if first retrieval yields <2 blastocysts
  • Set a clear decision point: after 2–3 own-egg attempts, if no viable embryo, discuss donor eggs

IVF at 42 — Honest Conversation

At 42, the success rate per own-egg transfer drops to 15–25%. The key statistic: cumulative success after 3 IVF cycles at 42 is approximately 35–45% — meaningful but requiring persistence. Donor egg IVF at any age gives 55–65% and bypasses the egg quality problem entirely.

Dr. Prashanthi Reddy's approach: try own eggs if AMH >0.3 and the patient is strongly motivated, with a clear 2-cycle limit before reassessing. Set expectations clearly: one good blastocyst from 2 retrievals at 42 is a realistic and positive outcome.

PGT-A After 38 — Why It Changes the Calculation

PGT-A tests embryos for chromosomal normality before transfer. At 38–42, 50–70% of embryos are abnormal. Without PGT-A: you might transfer 3 embryos over 3 cycles before getting one normal one, each failed transfer being a miscarriage or failed implantation. With PGT-A: you transfer only the normal embryo — higher success per transfer, lower miscarriage rate. The trade-off: ₹35,000–50,000 additional cost per IVF cycle, and the risk that no embryos pass testing.
