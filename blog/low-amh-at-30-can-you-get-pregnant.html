Low AMH at 30 — Can You Still Get Pregnant?

An AMH of 0.5 or 1.0 at 30 is frightening. But low AMH does not equal infertility. Here is what the science actually says — and how Mother Hospitals treats low AMH for patients from Uppal, Nagole, Ghatkesar, and East Hyderabad.

What Is AMH?

AMH (Anti-Müllerian Hormone) is produced by the small follicles in your ovaries. It is the best blood marker for ovarian reserve — how many eggs you have remaining. A low AMH means fewer eggs than average for your age.

What Does Low AMH at 30 Mean?

For a 30-year-old woman, a "normal" AMH is typically 1.5–4.0 ng/mL. An AMH below 1.0 is considered low; below 0.5 is considered very low. But here is the critical point most labs and doctors do not explain clearly:

AMH measures quantity — not quality. Egg quality at age 30 is typically still excellent, regardless of AMH. The risk from low AMH is that you have fewer eggs to retrieve in an IVF cycle — not that your eggs are genetically abnormal.

Can You Get Pregnant with Low AMH at 30?

Yes — many women do. Dr. Prashanthi Reddy at Mother Hospitals has treated patients with AMH as low as 0.3 ng/mL at age 30 who have gone on to successful pregnancies. The approach depends on how low the AMH is and what your fertility goals are.

Natural Conception with Low AMH at 30

If you have been trying naturally for 6–12 months without success and your AMH is low, it is time to see a fertility specialist. However, a low AMH alone does not mean you cannot conceive naturally — it means your window may be shorter, and waiting is not advisable.

Treatment Options at Mother Hospitals

  • DHEA + CoQ10 Priming: 60–90 days of DHEA 25–75mg and CoQ10 600mg daily before IVF. Shown to improve egg quality and quantity in poor ovarian reserve — particularly relevant for young women (under 35) with low AMH.
  • Mini-IVF / Natural Cycle IVF: Gentle stimulation protocol retrieving 2–4 eggs. Multiple cycles banked to accumulate embryos. Lower cost per cycle; suitable for very low AMH where high-dose stimulation does not produce more eggs.
  • Antagonist Protocol (conventional IVF): Standard dose FSH stimulation to retrieve maximum eggs. If AMH is 0.5–1.0, this may still yield 4–8 eggs and 1–3 usable blastocysts.
  • Embryo Banking Strategy: Rather than transferring after each cycle, all good-quality embryos are frozen. After 2–3 cycles, the best embryo is selected for transfer — maximising chances.
  • Needleless IVF: Oral/transdermal stimulation. Suitable for selected patients with low AMH who prefer to avoid injections. May be combined with DHEA priming.
  • Donor Egg IVF: If your own eggs fail repeatedly, donor egg IVF using an anonymous ART-Act-2021-compliant donor gives 55–65% success rate regardless of age.

The Most Important Thing to Do Right Now

If you are 30 with a low AMH result and want to have children — do not wait. Every 6 months you wait, your AMH can decline further. Book a consultation, get a complete fertility assessment, and understand your specific situation before making a decision.
