High AMH 6, 8, 10 with PCOS — IVF Protocol, OHSS Risk & Treatment

High AMH means many follicles — good for egg quantity, risky for OHSS. Here is the protocol Dr. Prashanthi Reddy uses for AMH 6, 8, and 10+ in PCOS patients, including Needleless IVF and the freeze-all strategy.

What Does High AMH Mean?

Most fertility content focuses on low AMH. But high AMH is equally important — and equally misunderstood. AMH above 4.0 ng/mL is considered elevated. Above 6.0–8.0 is very high. Above 10 is almost always associated with PCOS (Polycystic Ovary Syndrome) or PMOS (Polycystic/Metabolic Ovarian Syndrome — the updated 2026 classification).

High AMH means many follicles — which sounds like good news, and it partly is. But it also means a significantly higher risk of Ovarian Hyperstimulation Syndrome (OHSS) during IVF, which can be dangerous if the stimulation protocol is not adjusted.

Freeze-All Strategy for High AMH

For women with AMH 6+ or AFC 20+, Dr. Prashanthi Reddy typically recommends a freeze-all cycle: all eggs fertilised, all embryos frozen, zero fresh transfer. This eliminates the risk of OHSS worsening after a fresh transfer (because pregnancy HCG would stimulate OHSS further). The frozen embryo transfer (FET) is done in the next cycle — when the ovaries have calmed down. Success rates of FET in high-AMH patients are excellent.

Needleless IVF for PCOS / High AMH

Mother Hospitals is the only clinic in East Hyderabad offering Needleless IVF — an oral/transdermal protocol. For PCOS patients with high AMH, Needleless IVF provides gentler stimulation that reduces OHSS risk while still retrieving viable eggs. The protocol is specifically tailored for the PCOS/high AMH profile.

High AMH + PCOS — Natural Pregnancy Still Possible?

Yes — absolutely. High AMH just means many follicles. The problem in PCOS is that these follicles do not ovulate regularly (anovulation), not that the eggs are bad. Ovulation induction with Letrozole (Femara) + trigger injection gives 20–25% success per cycle for PCOS women under 35. Losing 5–10% body weight also significantly improves spontaneous ovulation in PCOS.
