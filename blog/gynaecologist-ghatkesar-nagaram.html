Who needs high risk pregnancy care?

  • Women aged 35 or older (advanced maternal age)
  • Twin or multiple pregnancies, including IVF-conceived multiples
  • Previous caesarean section with plans for VBAC (vaginal birth after caesarean)
  • Gestational diabetes mellitus (GDM) — increasingly common among the working-age population in Ghatkesar
  • Hypertensive disorders of pregnancy including pre-eclampsia
  • Thyroid disorders, autoimmune conditions, or cardiac disease in pregnancy
  • Previous pregnancy losses, preterm deliveries, or foetal anomalies
  • Foetal growth restriction (IUGR) detected on scan

Monitoring protocol at Mother Hospitals: Dr. Prashanthi's programme for high risk pregnancies includes enhanced antenatal visit frequency, detailed anomaly scanning, Doppler studies for foetal wellbeing, serial growth scans, and a written delivery plan. Gestational diabetes is managed with structured dietary counselling, home blood glucose monitoring guidance, and — when clinically indicated — insulin or oral agent therapy, always in close patient collaboration.

PCOS & Hormonal Disorders — Specialist near Ghatkesar

PCOS — now referred to as PMOS (Polycystic Morphology and Ovarian Syndrome) following the 2023 international nomenclature update — affects approximately 1 in 5 urban Indian women of reproductive age. In the rapidly growing residential areas around Ghatkesar and Nagaram, where sedentary IT work lifestyles and dietary patterns can exacerbate insulin resistance, access to a specialist PCOS physician is particularly valuable.

How PCOS is diagnosed at Mother Hospitals:

  • Detailed menstrual history, acne, hair, and weight symptom review
  • Comprehensive hormonal blood panel: LH, FSH ratio, total testosterone, DHEAS, prolactin, thyroid (TSH, T3, T4), fasting insulin, AMH
  • Transvaginal or pelvic ultrasound: ovarian volume, antral follicle count (AFC), endometrial thickness

Treatment is goal-directed:

  • Not planning pregnancy now: Cycle regulation, anti-androgen therapy for hair and skin, weight and insulin management, long-term metabolic monitoring
  • Planning to conceive: Ovulation induction with letrozole or clomiphene, close follicular monitoring, IUI, or IVF when required

Dr. Prashanthi's PCOS management goes beyond symptom control — the root metabolic and hormonal drivers are addressed, improving both short-term wellbeing and long-term reproductive outcomes.

Laparoscopy & Hysteroscopy near Nagaram

Minimally invasive gynaecological surgery has replaced open surgery for the vast majority of gynaecological conditions, offering patients shorter hospital stays, faster recovery, and less post-operative discomfort. Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy is trained and experienced in both laparoscopy and hysteroscopy, and performs these procedures regularly at Mother Hospitals, Boduppal — accessible from Nagaram in under 20 minutes.

Laparoscopy is indicated for:

  • Endometriosis — both diagnosis and surgical treatment of all stages
  • Fibroid removal (laparoscopic myomectomy) when surgery is recommended
  • Ovarian cyst drainage or removal (laparoscopic cystectomy)
  • Assessment and treatment of blocked or damaged fallopian tubes
  • Chronic pelvic pain — diagnostic and therapeutic
  • Ectopic pregnancy management
  • Adhesiolysis (removal of pelvic adhesions)

Hysteroscopy is indicated for:

  • Removal of uterine polyps or submucosal fibroids
  • Investigation and treatment of heavy or abnormal uterine bleeding
  • Pre-IVF uterine cavity assessment and optimisation
  • Uterine septum resection for patients with recurrent miscarriage
  • Management of intrauterine adhesions (Asherman's syndrome)
  • Removal of retained products of conception

Most laparoscopic and hysteroscopic procedures at Mother Hospitals are performed as day-care or short-stay admissions. Patients from Nagaram and Ghatkesar typically return home the same day or within 24 hours, and resume normal activities within 1–2 weeks.
