High Risk Pregnancy Specialist near Nacharam

A pregnancy is classified as high risk when there are medical or obstetric factors that increase the likelihood of complications for mother, baby, or both. For women from Nacharam, Habsiguda, and the surrounding East Hyderabad belt, having access to a high risk pregnancy specialist just 5–6 km away can be genuinely reassuring.

Conditions that make a pregnancy high risk include:

  • Gestational Diabetes Mellitus (GDM): Blood sugar that rises during pregnancy and requires close dietary and medication management
  • Hypertension in pregnancy: Including pre-eclampsia, which can be life-threatening if unmonitored
  • Twin or multiple pregnancies: Higher risk of preterm birth, low birth weight, and complications
  • Advanced maternal age (35+): Increased risk of chromosomal abnormalities and pregnancy complications
  • Previous C-section: Risk of uterine rupture in subsequent pregnancies requires specialist oversight
  • Thyroid disorders in pregnancy: Hypothyroidism and hyperthyroidism both require careful management
  • Anaemia and nutritional deficiencies: Particularly in women with poor dietary intake
  • Recurrent pregnancy loss: Two or more consecutive miscarriages that warrant investigation

Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy manages all these conditions with a structured, protocol-driven approach. Monitoring schedules are tailored to each patient's risk profile, and the team is equipped for both planned and emergency deliveries.

When Should You See a Gynaecologist?

Many women delay visiting a gynaecologist because symptoms seem minor or they are uncertain whether they need specialist attention. Here are ten signs that you should book an appointment at Mother Hospitals:

  1. Irregular periods: Cycles shorter than 21 days or longer than 35 days, or periods that stop for more than 3 months
  2. Painful periods (dysmenorrhoea): Pain that interferes with daily life or work may indicate endometriosis or fibroids
  3. PCOS signs: Facial hair, acne, weight gain around the abdomen, and irregular periods together suggest PCOS
  4. Difficulty conceiving: If you have been trying for 12 months (6 months if over 35) without success, see a specialist
  5. Recurrent miscarriage: Two or more consecutive pregnancy losses require thorough investigation
  6. Abnormal vaginal discharge: Unusual colour, odour, or volume that persists beyond a few days
  7. Pelvic pain: Persistent or cyclical lower abdominal pain not related to periods
  8. Postcoital bleeding: Bleeding after intercourse should always be evaluated
  9. Urinary symptoms: Frequent urination, burning, or incontinence in women may have gynaecological causes
  10. Breast lumps or nipple discharge: Always warrants prompt specialist evaluation

None of these symptoms should be dismissed. An early consultation with our gynaecology team can prevent a minor issue from becoming a complex one.
