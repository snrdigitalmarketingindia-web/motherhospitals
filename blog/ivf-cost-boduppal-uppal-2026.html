What's NOT Included (Hidden Costs to Watch For)

Being honest about what is not in the standard package is just as important as listing what is. Here are additional procedures that may be recommended based on your individual situation — and what they typically cost:

  • ERA Test (Endometrial Receptivity Analysis) — approximately ₹25,000. Recommended for patients with recurrent implantation failure to identify the optimal window for embryo transfer. Not needed for all patients.
  • PGT-A / PGT-M (Preimplantation Genetic Testing) — ₹50,000 and above, depending on number of embryos tested. Recommended for patients with recurrent miscarriage, advanced maternal age, or known genetic conditions.
  • Donor egg fees — included in our Donor Egg IVF package, but worth clarifying at other clinics where donor coordination may be billed separately.
  • Sperm freezing or TESA/PESA — surgical sperm retrieval for azoospermia is an additional procedure with its own fee. Please ask specifically if this applies to you.
  • Laser Assisted Hatching — a small additional cost for patients where this technique improves implantation prospects.

At Mother Hospitals, we will always tell you upfront if any of these additional tests or procedures are recommended for you — and why. You will never find these silently added to your bill. Injections, which are a major hidden cost at many clinics, are fully included in our packages.

Why IVF Cost Varies — Factors That Affect Price

If you have received quotes from multiple clinics and found them confusingly different, here is why IVF cost legitimately varies from patient to patient:

  • Age: Older patients often require higher doses of stimulation medication and more monitoring cycles, which increases cost even within the same package structure.
  • AMH level: Low AMH may require a modified stimulation protocol with higher-dose or different medications. Conversely, high AMH (as in PCOS) needs careful management to avoid OHSS.
  • Protocol type: Long protocol, short protocol, antagonist protocol, and natural cycle IVF each have slightly different cost structures due to medication differences.
  • Number of cycles: If a first cycle does not succeed, a second or third cycle adds cost. FET cycles from frozen embryos are significantly cheaper than a fresh stimulation cycle.
  • Add-ons like ERA or PGT-A: These are meaningful additional costs if your doctor recommends them based on your history.
  • Sperm factor: Mild male factor infertility requires ICSI rather than basic IVF. Severe azoospermia may require TESA/PESA as an additional procedure.

This is why it is important to get a personalised assessment rather than comparing advertised prices directly. Two couples at the same clinic may pay very different amounts depending on what their individual situation requires.

Is Cheap IVF Worth It? What to Watch Out For

Across Hyderabad, you will encounter IVF advertisements quoting prices as low as ₹60,000 or ₹70,000 per cycle. It is natural to be tempted by these figures. But there are serious risks to choosing a clinic based on the lowest advertised price alone.

Here is what budget IVF centres sometimes cut corners on:

  • Lab quality: The IVF laboratory environment — temperature, humidity, air quality, CO₂ levels — is critical to embryo survival and development. Maintaining a proper IVF lab is expensive. Very low-cost centres may use shared or inadequate laboratory infrastructure, directly affecting embryo quality.
  • ART Act 2021 certification: The Assisted Reproductive Technology (Regulation) Act 2021 mandates licensing of all IVF centres in India. Unlicensed centres operate outside legal oversight. Always verify ART certification before committing to treatment.
  • Embryologist experience: The embryologist — the scientist who handles your eggs and embryos — is as important as the doctor. High turnover of junior staff in budget clinics is a real concern.
  • Hidden charges: A ₹70,000 advertised price that excludes injections (₹40,000+), monitoring scans, and the laboratory procedure fee can quickly exceed ₹1.5 lakh once fully itemised.
  • Counselling and support: The emotional journey of IVF is as important as the clinical one. Budget centres often have no dedicated counselling support.
