6-Week Heartbeat Scan After IVF — What to Expect at Your Viability Scan

The first scan after a positive beta hCG is one of the most emotional moments of an IVF journey. Here is exactly what the ultrasound shows, what normal looks like, and what happens when the heartbeat is not yet visible.

Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy

MBBS, DGO · Diploma in ART (Kiel University, Germany) · TGMC Reg: 50624 · 19+ Years
IVF Specialist, Mother Hospitals & IVF Center, Boduppal, Hyderabad

Last medically reviewed: 25 May 2026

After the long wait of embryo transfer and the nerve-wracking beta hCG blood test, the 6-week viability scan is the moment most IVF patients are counting down to. It is the first real look inside — the first chance to see whether the embryo has implanted correctly, is growing in the right place, and whether there is a heartbeat.

For many patients, this scan is more emotionally charged than the positive pregnancy test. A heartbeat at 6 weeks means the pregnancy has crossed one of its most significant early milestones. This guide explains exactly what to expect.

About this guide: Written for patients who conceived through IVF at Mother Hospitals, Boduppal, Hyderabad.

When Is the 6-Week Scan Scheduled After IVF?

In IVF, pregnancy is counted from the date of egg retrieval (equivalent to ovulation day), not from the embryo transfer itself. A day-5 blastocyst transferred is already considered to be at approximately 19–20 days of gestational age. The scan is typically scheduled around:

Transfer TypeScan Booked AtWhy
Day-5 blastocyst (fresh or frozen)~4 weeks post-transferAt this point gestational age is ~6 weeks
Day-3 cleavage stage embryo~4–4.5 weeks post-transferSlightly earlier gestational count
Beta hCG very strong (>5,000)Sometimes earlier at 5 weeks 5 daysHigher betas suggest more developed sac
Anxious patient or suspected bleedAny time after 5 weeks 3 daysConfirms intrauterine vs. ectopic location first
Mother Hospitals practice: We typically schedule the viability scan at 6 weeks 0 days to 6 weeks 4 days after a successful IVF beta hCG. This window balances the chance of seeing a heartbeat against the risk of unnecessary anxiety from a scan done too early.

What Does the 6-Week Scan Show? The Four Things We Look For

Finding 1

Gestational Sac

A fluid-filled oval or round structure inside the uterine cavity. This is the first confirmation that the pregnancy is in the uterus (not ectopic). Normal diameter at 6 weeks: 15–25 mm.

Finding 2

Yolk Sac

A small white ring visible inside the gestational sac — like a tiny doughnut. It nourishes the embryo before the placenta forms. Normal diameter: 3–5 mm. Presence confirms a developing pregnancy.

Finding 3

Fetal Pole (Embryo)

The first visible embryonic tissue — a small thickening adjacent to the yolk sac. Crown-rump length (CRL) at 6 weeks: approximately 2–6 mm (about the size of a grain of rice).

Finding 4

Fetal Heartbeat

Visible as a rapid flickering movement within the fetal pole. Measured in beats per minute (bpm). At 6 weeks: 90–110 bpm. This is the moment every IVF patient has been waiting for.

✔ The presence of all four findings — gestational sac + yolk sac + fetal pole + heartbeat — at 6 weeks is the most reassuring outcome of the viability scan. Miscarriage risk drops from ~20% (at positive beta) to approximately 5–8% after a heartbeat is confirmed.

Normal Heartbeat Rates at 6 Weeks and Beyond

Fetal heart rate changes significantly through the first trimester. Understanding what is normal helps reduce anxiety when comparing numbers:

Fetal Heart Rate by Week — Normal Ranges
5 weeks 5d – 6 weeks80–110 bpm — earliest detectable rangeFlickering; may need repeat in 5–7 days
6 weeks 3d – 7 weeks100–140 bpm — well-established rangeMost reassuring range at this stage
7 – 8 weeks120–160 bpm — rising as heart maturesClearly audible on Doppler by 8 weeks
8 – 10 weeks140–170 bpm — peak rate of first trimesterNormal to seem "fast" — this is expected
< 90 bpm at 6–7 weeksBelow-normal — requires urgent repeat scanStrong predictor of early pregnancy loss
> 180 bpm sustainedTachycardia — warrants evaluationRare but needs monitoring
What the sonographer will say: A common way findings are reported is: "Gestational sac seen. Yolk sac present. Fetal pole with cardiac activity at 105 bpm. CRL 4 mm, corresponding to 6 weeks 2 days. Intrauterine pregnancy confirmed." This is the report every IVF patient hopes to hear.

How Is the 6-Week Scan Done?

After IVF, the 6-week scan is almost always performed transvaginally — using an internal ultrasound probe. Here is what to expect:

  1. Empty your bladder before the scan — unlike an abdominal scan, a full bladder is not required and can actually make the image less clear at this stage.
  2. A narrow ultrasound probe (transducer) is gently inserted into the vagina. This is the same type of probe used during the egg retrieval monitoring scans you would have had during your IVF cycle.
  3. The uterus is visualised in multiple planes. The sonographer will systematically look for the gestational sac location, sac size, yolk sac, fetal pole, and cardiac activity.
  4. Heartbeat is confirmed visually (as a flickering motion) and measured in bpm using Doppler or M-mode ultrasound. You will often see this on screen as a moving squiggly line.
  5. The ovaries are also checked — particularly looking for any remaining ovarian cysts from the stimulation or early signs of residual OHSS.
  6. The whole procedure takes 10–15 minutes. It is not painful, though some patients find the probe slightly uncomfortable. It is safe and will not disturb the pregnancy.
Is transvaginal ultrasound safe in early pregnancy? Yes. Multiple studies confirm that transvaginal ultrasound does not increase miscarriage risk. The probe does not touch the cervix or uterus directly — it sits in the vaginal vault and uses sound waves, not radiation, to create images.

What If There Is No Heartbeat at the 6-Week Scan?

This is the question every IVF patient dreads. If no heartbeat is seen at the 6-week scan, it does not automatically mean the pregnancy has failed. The most common reason is simply timing.

Possible Reasons for No Heartbeat at 6 Weeks

FindingMost Likely MeaningAction
Sac + yolk sac, no fetal poleToo early — embryo not yet visibleRepeat scan in 7 days
Sac + yolk sac + fetal pole, no heartbeatToo early or early missed miscarriageRepeat scan in 5–7 days — do NOT conclude yet
Sac present, no yolk sac, sac > 20 mmPossible blighted ovum (anembryonic pregnancy)Repeat scan in 7–10 days to confirm before any action
Fetal pole visible, clearly no cardiac activity on Doppler/M-modeMissed miscarriage — requires confirmationSecond scan by senior doctor before management decision
No sac in uterus but beta hCG positivePossible ectopic pregnancy — urgentEmergency review — call immediately
The 7-day rule: At Mother Hospitals, we never make a final decision about a pregnancy based on a single scan showing no heartbeat. A confirmed repeat scan at least 7 days later is standard before any management decision is made. This is in line with NICE (UK) and AIUM guidelines and protects against the rare but real risk of a scan being done slightly too early.

What If Only One Gestational Sac Is Seen After Two Embryo Transfers?

This is completely normal. Even when two embryos are transferred, it is common for only one to implant. The absence of the second sac is not a cause for concern — the first sac is the focus. Approximately 70–80% of two-embryo transfers result in a singleton pregnancy. The remaining 20–30% result in twins.

Twin IVF pregnancies: If two sacs (or two heartbeats) are seen, the pregnancy is classified as a dichorionic-diamniotic (DCDA) twin pregnancy in most cases after IVF. Twin pregnancies are higher risk and require more frequent monitoring. They are managed under a high-risk pregnancy protocol at Mother Hospitals.

Emotional Preparation for the 6-Week Scan

How to Prepare Mentally for Your Viability Scan

  • It is normal to feel terrified. Every IVF patient walks into this scan with enormous emotional weight. Your feelings are valid — this scan carries years of hope.
  • Bring your partner or a support person if possible. Having someone with you makes both good news and difficult news easier to process in the moment.
  • Ask the sonographer to talk you through what they are seeing in real time — many patients find a running commentary less anxiety-provoking than silence.
  • If no heartbeat is seen, do not catastrophise immediately. Wait for the repeat scan. The 7-day rule exists precisely because of how emotionally charged this moment is.
  • After a positive scan — take the rest of the day gently. Many patients experience a sudden emotional release (tears, shaking, euphoria) after a good scan. This is completely normal.
  • Ask about counselling if anxiety is overwhelming your daily life. Mother Hospitals can refer you to a specialist who works with IVF patients.

Frequently Asked Questions — 6-Week Scan After IVF

When is the 6-week scan done after IVF?
Approximately 4 weeks after a day-5 blastocyst transfer, when the gestational age is 6 weeks 0 days to 6 weeks 4 days. At Mother Hospitals, we schedule the scan in this window to maximise the chance of seeing a heartbeat while avoiding a premature scan that creates unnecessary anxiety.
What does the 6-week scan show after IVF?
The ideal scan shows four things: a gestational sac inside the uterus, a yolk sac within the sac, a fetal pole (tiny embryo), and a heartbeat at 90–110 bpm. The crown-rump length (CRL) is approximately 2–6 mm at this stage — about the size of a grain of rice.
What is a normal heartbeat at 6 weeks on an IVF scan?
At exactly 6 weeks, 90–110 bpm is normal. By 7 weeks it rises to 120–140 bpm and by 8–9 weeks it peaks at 140–170 bpm. A rate below 90 bpm at 6–7 weeks is a warning sign that requires close monitoring with a repeat scan in 5–7 days.
What does it mean if there is no heartbeat at the 6-week scan?
No heartbeat at 6 weeks is not automatically a miscarriage. The most common reason is that the scan was done slightly too early. If the gestational sac and yolk sac are present, a repeat scan in 7 days is standard. A final decision is never made on a single scan. Only if there is no heartbeat at 7 weeks with a visible fetal pole and confirmed Doppler silence would a missed miscarriage be diagnosed.
Is the 6-week scan done transvaginally or abdominally?
Transvaginally (internally) after IVF. An abdominal scan cannot reliably see a 2–4 mm fetal pole at this stage. The transvaginal probe provides much higher resolution. It is safe, not painful for most patients, and does not disturb the pregnancy in any way.
Can I see the heartbeat on a scan at exactly 6 weeks IVF?
At 6 weeks 0 days exactly, roughly 60–70% of viable IVF pregnancies will show a clear heartbeat. By 6 weeks 4 days, this rises to 85–90%. By 7 weeks, it should be visible in all normally developing pregnancies. This is why we prefer to scan at 6 weeks 3–4 days rather than exactly 6 weeks 0 days.
What happens after a good 6-week scan?
Continue all progesterone medications as prescribed. The next scan is at 8–10 weeks (growth scan). Miscarriage risk drops to approximately 5–8% after a confirmed heartbeat. Many patients choose this milestone to quietly share news with close family. The 11–13 week NT scan follows, after which you transition out of the fertility clinic and into full antenatal care.
What if I have twins — what does the 6-week scan show?
If both embryos implanted, the scan shows two gestational sacs with two heartbeats (DCDA twins — the most common type after IVF). Twin pregnancies are classified as high-risk and require more frequent monitoring. They are managed under Mother Hospitals' high-risk pregnancy programme.

6-వారాల స్కాన్‌లో ఏమి కనిపిస్తుంది?

నిర్ధారణ 1

Gestational Sac (గర్భసంచి)

గర్భాశయం లోపల ద్రవం నిండిన చిన్న నిర్మాణం. గర్భం గర్భాశయంలోనే ఉందని (ectopic కాదని) నిర్ధారిస్తుంది. 6 వారాల్లో సాధారణ పరిమాణం: 15–25 mm.

నిర్ధారణ 2

Yolk Sac (పోషక సంచి)

Gestational sac లోపల కనిపించే చిన్న తెల్లటి వలయం. గర్భం అభివృద్ధి చెందుతోందని తెలిపే ముఖ్యమైన సంకేతం. సాధారణ పరిమాణం: 3–5 mm.

నిర్ధారణ 3

Fetal Pole (భ్రూణ నిర్మాణం)

Yolk sac పక్కన కనిపించే చిన్న మందమైన నిర్మాణం — మొదటి భ్రూణ కణజాలం. 6 వారాల్లో CRL (పొడవు): 2–6 mm (అన్నం గింజ పరిమాణం).

నిర్ధారణ 4

గుండె చప్పుడు (Heartbeat)

Fetal pole లోపల వేగంగా మెరుస్తున్న కదలిక. 6 వారాల్లో సాధారణ రేటు: 90–110 bpm. ప్రతి IVF రోగి ఎదురుచూసే అమూల్యమైన క్షణం ఇది.

✔ నాలుగు నిర్ధారణలూ — gestational sac + yolk sac + fetal pole + గుండె చప్పుడు — కనిపిస్తే, అది అత్యంత సంతోషకరమైన ఫలితం. గుండె చప్పుడు నిర్ధారణ తర్వాత గర్భస్రావం ప్రమాదం ~20% నుండి 5–8% కి తగ్గుతుంది.
