Thyroid (TSH) and IVF — What Level Is Safe? How It Affects Success

TSH 2.5 is the fertility threshold — stricter than the lab normal range of 4.0. Even borderline thyroid affects IVF implantation rates and miscarriage risk. Here is exactly what needs to be checked and fixed before your IVF cycle.

Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy

MBBS, DGO · Diploma in ART (Kiel University, Germany) · TGMC Reg: 50624 · 20+ Years
IVF Specialist, Mother Hospitals & IVF Center, Boduppal, Hyderabad

Last medically reviewed: 25 June 2026

Does Thyroid Disease Affect IVF Success?

Yes — significantly. The thyroid gland regulates metabolism, hormone balance, and uterine receptivity. Even subclinical hypothyroidism (TSH slightly elevated, no symptoms) has been linked to lower IVF implantation rates and higher miscarriage risk. This is one of the most important — and most easily corrected — factors before IVF.

What TSH Level Is Safe for IVF?

Standard practice for IVF: TSH <2.5 mIU/L before starting stimulation. Ideally <2.0. This is stricter than the "normal" lab range (0.4–4.0) because IVF involves hormonal changes that increase the burden on the thyroid — FSH injections can transiently raise TSH, and early pregnancy doubles the thyroid's workload.

TPO Antibodies and IVF

Even when TSH is normal, high TPO antibodies (anti-thyroid antibodies) are associated with a higher miscarriage rate after IVF. Research shows that women with elevated TPO antibodies have a 2× higher miscarriage risk in IVF cycles, even when TSH is well-controlled. Some evidence supports low-dose Levothyroxine even with normal TSH but high TPO Ab — Dr. Prashanthi Reddy reviews this individually.

How Long to Treat Before IVF?

Levothyroxine takes 6–8 weeks to stabilise TSH. If TSH is 3.0–4.0, starting 25–50 mcg Levothyroxine today and rechecking at 6 weeks will typically bring it below 2.5 — and IVF can begin immediately after.

TSH During IVF Stimulation

FSH injections given during IVF stimulation can temporarily increase TSH by 0.5–1.5 points. Women who enter stimulation with TSH 2.0–2.5 may see it rise to 3.0–3.5 during stimulation. For this reason, Dr. Prashanthi Reddy often increases Levothyroxine dose by 25 mcg at the start of stimulation in women who are already on treatment.
