Pregnancy After IVF — What to Expect, Week by Week

Your First Confirmation — The Beta-hCG Blood Test

Unlike natural conception (confirmed by a urine test at home), an IVF pregnancy is confirmed by a beta-hCG blood test performed at the clinic on Day 12–14 after embryo transfer.

  • What beta-hCG measures: The hormone human chorionic gonadotrophin, produced by implanting trophoblast cells
  • What a positive result looks like: Any value above 25 mIU/mL on Day 12–14 is generally considered positive; higher values suggest more robust implantation
  • The 48-hour repeat: hCG should approximately double every 48 hours in a viable early pregnancy. A rising but slowly doubling hCG may be monitored more carefully
  • Why home tests are unreliable: The trigger injection used during IVF stimulation contains hCG, which can cause false positives on home tests for up to 10–14 days after administration

Medications You Will Continue After a Positive IVF Test

Your medications are the most important part of early IVF pregnancy management. The body's own hormonal support mechanisms are partially replaced by medications until the placenta is established. Here is what you are likely taking and why:

Critical: Never stop progesterone, oestrogen, or any other IVF medication without Dr. Prashanthi Reddy's explicit instruction — even if you feel well, have no symptoms, or have read that "the placenta takes over at 8 weeks." The taper timing is individualised based on your blood levels.

The 6-Week Heartbeat Scan — The Milestone That Changes Everything

For almost every IVF patient, the 6–7 week viability scan is the single most anticipated moment in early pregnancy. It is where the abstract reality of a positive beta-hCG becomes a visible, measurable heartbeat.

  • What the scan checks: Location (intrauterine vs ectopic), number of sacs, presence of yolk sac, fetal pole, and cardiac activity
  • How it is done: Transvaginal ultrasound (TVS) — brief, gentle, no full bladder needed
  • Normal heartbeat at 6 weeks: 90–110 bpm (rises to 150–170 bpm by Week 8–10)
  • If no heartbeat is seen at exactly 6 weeks: A repeat scan in 5–7 days is standard — sometimes dates are slightly off and the heartbeat develops a few days later
After the heartbeat is confirmed: Miscarriage risk drops from 15–25% to approximately 5%. Most IVF patients describe the moment of seeing the heartbeat as the point where the pregnancy finally feels real. Allow yourself to feel it.

When Does IVF Pregnancy Become a "Normal" Pregnancy?

At around 10–12 weeks — after a confirmed heartbeat, confirmed fetal growth, and a normal NT scan — your IVF pregnancy transitions into routine obstetric care. At this point:

  • Miscarriage risk is under 2% — comparable to any naturally conceived pregnancy at the same stage
  • Medications are tapered and stopped
  • You move from fertility clinic monitoring to standard antenatal care
  • Future pregnancy milestones (anatomy scan, growth scans, delivery) follow the same timeline as any pregnancy

Frequently Asked Questions

What happens after a positive IVF pregnancy test?
After a positive beta-hCG blood test (Day 12–14 after transfer), your clinic repeats the test in 48 hours to confirm levels are doubling. You continue all medications and book a 6–7 week viability scan to confirm a heartbeat and rule out ectopic pregnancy.
Is an IVF pregnancy higher risk than a natural pregnancy?
IVF pregnancies require closer early monitoring — not because IVF itself is dangerous, but because patients tend to be older, may carry twins, and benefit from early confirmation. With appropriate monitoring, the vast majority of IVF pregnancies result in healthy deliveries.
When does IVF pregnancy become a normal pregnancy?
Most IVF patients transition from fertility clinic to routine obstetric care at 10–12 weeks — after a confirmed heartbeat at 6–7 weeks, growth confirmation at 8–10 weeks, and a normal 12-week NT scan. At this point IVF pregnancy risk is comparable to naturally conceived pregnancy.
When will I have my first pregnancy scan after IVF?
Your first pregnancy scan after IVF is the viability scan at 6–7 weeks. This transvaginal ultrasound confirms an intrauterine pregnancy, checks for a heartbeat, rules out ectopic pregnancy, and determines whether you are carrying a singleton or twins.
When do I stop progesterone after successful IVF?
Progesterone supplements are tapered from Week 8–10, once the placenta begins producing its own progesterone. Most patients are fully off by Week 12. Never stop progesterone without Dr. Prashanthi Reddy's instruction — sudden withdrawal can cause bleeding.
Can I have a normal delivery after IVF?
Yes. IVF pregnancy does not automatically mean caesarean delivery. The delivery mode is decided by the same obstetric factors as any pregnancy — baby's position, maternal health, placenta location. Many IVF patients have normal vaginal deliveries.
What medications do I continue after a positive IVF test?
Continue progesterone and oestrogen supplements exactly as prescribed. Also continue aspirin (if prescribed), folic acid (5mg), and Vitamin D. Do not stop or modify any medication without explicit instruction from Dr. Prashanthi Reddy.
Do IVF pregnancies have more miscarriage risk?
The miscarriage rate after IVF is approximately 15–25%, slightly higher than natural conception because IVF patients are older on average. Once a heartbeat is confirmed at 7 weeks, miscarriage risk drops to around 5% — comparable to naturally conceived pregnancies at the same stage.
