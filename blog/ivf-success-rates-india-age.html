IVF Success Rates by Age in India — What the Numbers Actually Mean for You

IVF success rates range from 55–65% under 35 to 20–30% over 40. But these numbers can be misleading. Here is how to interpret them — and what they mean specifically for patients in Hyderabad.

What These Numbers Actually Mean

A "55% success rate" means: if 100 women under 35 start an IVF cycle at this clinic, approximately 55 will have a confirmed clinical pregnancy (heartbeat on scan). Of those, approximately 90% will go on to a live birth — so the live birth rate is approximately 50%.

This also means 45 out of 100 cycles do not result in pregnancy. That is not a failure of the clinic — it is the biological reality of human reproduction. Understanding this prevents false expectations.

What Affects YOUR IVF Success Rate?

Age is the single biggest factor — because egg quality declines with age. But age alone does not tell the whole story. Your specific success rate also depends on:

  • AMH (ovarian reserve) — how many eggs are available
  • Sperm quality — DNA fragmentation, motility, count
  • Uterine health — fibroids, polyps, thin endometrium, adhesions
  • Diagnosis — PCOS, endometriosis, tubal factor, unexplained
  • Previous IVF attempts — recurrent failure may need ERA test or PGT-A
  • BMI — obesity reduces implantation rates
  • Lab quality — embryo culture conditions, vitrification technique

Questions to Ask Any IVF Clinic Before Starting

  1. What is your live birth rate per transfer — not just clinical pregnancy rate?
  2. Do you include all patients in your statistics — or only easy cases?
  3. What is your blastocyst formation rate (Day 5 embryo quality)?
  4. Is the embryologist on-site every day — or outsourced?
  5. Who will personally handle my monitoring and retrieval — or will it rotate among doctors?
