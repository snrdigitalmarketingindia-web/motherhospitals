Sperm Morphology 1% or 2% — Can ICSI Still Work? Success Rates & Treatment

Even 1% morphology is enough for ICSI — the embryologist needs one good-shaped sperm per egg. Here is what the fertilisation rates look like, when IMSI/PICSI helps, and how to improve morphology before your IVF cycle.

What Is Sperm Morphology?

Morphology refers to the percentage of sperm with normal shape. WHO 2021 reference: ≥4% normal forms (Kruger strict criteria). Below 4% is called teratospermia (or teratozoospermia). This is one of the most misunderstood fertility parameters — because even 1% normal morphology has been used successfully for ICSI.

Why? Because with ICSI, the embryologist manually selects the best-shaped sperm under 200–400× magnification. You do not need millions of normal-shaped sperm. You need one normal sperm per egg — and even samples with 1% morphology typically contain hundreds of acceptable sperm per ejaculate.

Morphology 1% or 2% — Can ICSI Still Work?

Yes — and this is well-documented in fertility literature. At Mother Hospitals, patients with morphology as low as 1% have achieved successful fertilisation and pregnancy via ICSI. The success rate depends more on egg quality and embryo development than on morphology percentage.

IMSI and PICSI for Low Morphology

IMSI (Intracytoplasmic Morphologically-Selected Sperm Injection) uses 6,000× magnification (vs 400× in standard ICSI) to identify sperm with no visible vacuoles in the nucleus. For men with morphology 1–2%, IMSI can improve fertilisation rates by selecting the truly best sperm from an already abnormal pool.

PICSI selects sperm based on hyaluronic acid binding — mature sperm with intact DNA bind to hyaluronic acid. Reduces DNA fragmentation rates in the selected sperm. Both techniques are available at Mother Hospitals.

Can Morphology Improve Before IVF?

Sperm take 72–90 days to mature (spermatogenesis cycle). Interventions that can improve morphology over this period:

  • Antioxidant supplement stack: CoQ10 300–600mg + Vitamin C 1g + Vitamin E 400 IU + Lycopene 10mg + Zinc 25mg
  • Varicocele treatment if present — increases blood flow and reduces testicular heat
  • Avoiding laptop on lap, hot baths, tight underwear (scrotal temperature)
  • Stopping smoking (direct DNA damage to sperm)
  • Treating infections (leukocytes in semen = white blood cells = oxidative damage)
