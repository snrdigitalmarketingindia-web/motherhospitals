Advanced Treatments Including Needleless IVF and ERA Test

Mother Hospitals is one of the few centres in the Uppal–Boduppal belt to offer Needleless IVF — a protocol that significantly reduces the number of injections during stimulation, making the cycle more comfortable and improving patient compliance. The centre also offers the ERA Test (Endometrial Receptivity Analysis), which identifies the precise implantation window in the uterine lining — critical for patients with recurrent IVF failure or unexplained implantation issues.

ICSI (Intracytoplasmic Sperm Injection) is performed routinely for male factor infertility cases, and Donor Egg IVF is available for women with diminished ovarian reserve or premature ovarian insufficiency. The laboratory operates under stringent quality protocols, with controlled atmosphere incubators and an experienced embryology team.

IVF Success Rates at Mother Hospitals Boduppal

One of the most important questions couples ask before beginning IVF is: "What are the success rates?" At Mother Hospitals, we believe in giving honest, age-stratified data rather than inflated marketing claims.

  • Women under 35: 50–65% success rate per transfer cycle — the best possible outcomes, particularly for women with good ovarian reserve and normal uterine anatomy
  • Ages 35–37: 40–50% per transfer — still strong results, though egg quality begins to show age-related changes
  • Ages 38–40: 30–40% per transfer — viable outcomes, often enhanced with ERA Test and blastocyst-stage transfer
  • Ages 41–42: 20–30% with own eggs; Donor Egg IVF at this stage can restore success rates to 50–60%+

Dr. Prashanthi's Note: "Success rates are not just about age. A 38-year-old with good AMH and a healthy uterus may have better outcomes than a 32-year-old with severe PCOS and a thin endometrium. I personalise every treatment plan from the very first consultation."

These figures are benchmarked against ICMR national standards and reflect live birth rates per transfer — the most meaningful metric to look for when comparing clinics.

  • Hysteroscopy: Minimally invasive uterine evaluation and treatment for polyps, fibroids, septum, and adhesions
