Sperm Count 1 Million, 5 Million — ICSI Success Rate & Treatment

Even 1 million sperm/mL is enough for ICSI — the embryologist needs exactly one sperm per egg. Here is the treatment pathway by sperm count, what to try first, and what to do if count is extremely low.

Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy

MBBS, DGO · Diploma in ART (Kiel University, Germany) · TGMC Reg: 50624 · 20+ Years
IVF Specialist, Mother Hospitals & IVF Center, Boduppal, Hyderabad

Last medically reviewed: 25 June 2026

What Is a Low Sperm Count? The Numbers Explained

WHO 2021 reference values: Normal = ≥16 million sperm per mL (≥39 million per ejaculate). Below normal is called oligospermia. Below 5 million/mL is severe oligospermia. Below 1 million/mL is extremely severe oligospermia (cryptozoospermia).

But here is the key point: you need exactly ONE sperm to fertilise one egg. With ICSI (Intracytoplasmic Sperm Injection), a single viable sperm is all that is needed per egg. Even sperm count at 1 million/mL gives 1 million sperm to work with.

Sperm Count 1 Million/mL — Can ICSI Work?

Yes. ICSI with 1 million sperm/mL is a standard procedure at Mother Hospitals. The embryologist will select the best-shaped, motile sperm under high magnification (IMSI) and inject it directly into the egg. At 1 million/mL, there are still thousands of sperm to choose from in the sample.

Sperm Count 5 Million/mL — ICSI vs Natural Conception

At 5 million/mL, natural conception is very unlikely (most sperm do not reach the egg at this count). IUI has a 10–15% success rate per cycle but requires at least 5 million motile sperm after washing. ICSI-IVF bypasses this entirely and gives 45–55% success per transfer regardless of sperm count — the embryologist picks the single best sperm for each egg.

Improving Sperm Count Before ICSI

For men with 1–5 million/mL: a 90-day protocol of Antioxidants (CoQ10 300mg, Vitamin C 1g, Vitamin E 400 IU, Selenium 200mcg) + Folic acid 5mg has been shown to improve count and DNA fragmentation. Combined with treating any varicocele if present, this can sometimes shift sperm count above 5 million before the IVF cycle — increasing the choice of sperm for ICSI and potentially reducing DNA fragmentation rates.

TESA — When Ejaculated Sperm Are Too Few

If sperm count falls below the threshold needed even for ICSI — or if DNA fragmentation is very high in ejaculated sperm — TESA (Testicular Sperm Aspiration) retrieves sperm directly from the testis. Testicular sperm often have lower DNA fragmentation than ejaculated sperm, which can improve fertilisation rates in severe oligospermia.
