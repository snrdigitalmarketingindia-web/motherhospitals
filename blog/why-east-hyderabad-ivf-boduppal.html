The Old Problem: IVF Meant a 30–45 Minute Drive Every Other Day

An IVF cycle requires monitoring visits every 2–3 days for 10–12 days during stimulation: ultrasound scans, blood tests, adjusting medication doses. For patients in East Hyderabad — Uppal, Boduppal, Nagole, Ghatkesar, ECIL, Medipally, Chengicherla — this meant travelling 30–45 minutes each way to reach clinics in Jubilee Hills, Banjara Hills, or Begumpet.

That is 60–90 minutes per monitoring visit. With 5–6 visits in a cycle, patients were spending 6–9 hours commuting just during stimulation — before accounting for the emotional and physical stress of an IVF cycle itself.

What Makes Mother Hospitals Different from Chain IVF Clinics

Large chain IVF clinics — while offering good infrastructure — rotate patients among multiple doctors. You may meet your assigned doctor at consultation, but the monitoring scan on day 7 is done by a different doctor, the egg retrieval by another. There is no single person who knows your cycle inside-out.

At Mother Hospitals, Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy personally handles every IVF cycle from consultation to embryo transfer. That continuity — combined with her Germany training in ART — is difficult to find in chain clinics.
