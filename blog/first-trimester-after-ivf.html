First Trimester After IVF — Risks, Monitoring & What's Different

The first 12 weeks after a successful IVF transfer are both exciting and nerve-wracking. Here is what monitoring you need, when miscarriage risk decreases, and when you can finally breathe easier.

Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy

MBBS, DGO · Diploma in ART (Kiel University, Germany) · TGMC Reg: 50624 · 19+ Years
IVF Specialist, Mother Hospitals & IVF Center, Boduppal, Hyderabad

Last medically reviewed: 25 May 2026

For many IVF patients, the first trimester is the most anxiety-filled period of their entire fertility journey. After years of trying and months of treatment, every twinge, every moment of nausea disappearing, every day without a scan feels loaded with meaning. This guide is written to help you understand exactly what is happening, what to watch for, and — importantly — when you can genuinely begin to relax.

Note: This guide is specifically for patients who conceived through IVF at Mother Hospitals. For a complete week-by-week early pregnancy guide, see Early Pregnancy Care After IVF.

Miscarriage Risk After IVF: The Timeline

Miscarriage risk decreases significantly at each milestone:

MilestoneApproximate Miscarriage Risk
Positive beta hCG (Day 14)15–25% overall (higher in older patients)
Heartbeat seen at 6–7 weeksDrops to ~5–8%
Normal 10-week scanDrops to ~2–3%
Normal 12-week NT scanUnder 1–2%
PGT-A euploid embryo transferredMiscarriage risk as low as 5% regardless of age
The most reassuring milestone: Seeing a strong heartbeat at the 7-week scan. Once a heartbeat at 130–160 bpm is confirmed, miscarriage risk in the first trimester drops significantly — to around 5–8%. This is the scan most IVF patients look forward to most. See our detailed 6-week heartbeat scan guide.

Bleeding in Early IVF Pregnancy: What Is Normal?

Bleeding in early IVF pregnancy is common and causes enormous anxiety — but it is not always a sign of miscarriage. For a complete guide to causes, severity classification, and when to call, see our dedicated bleeding in early IVF pregnancy guide.

Usually harmless:
  • Implantation spotting — light brown/pink spotting around Days 7–10 post-transfer
  • Pessary irritation — progesterone pessaries can cause light cervical spotting after insertion
  • Subchorionic haematoma — small blood collection near gestational sac; usually resolves; requires monitoring
Requires immediate medical attention:
  • Heavy red bleeding (heavier than a period)
  • Severe cramping with bleeding
  • One-sided abdominal pain (ectopic warning)
  • Passing tissue or clots

Progesterone Tapering: When and How

Many IVF patients are anxious about stopping progesterone. Here is what happens:

  • The placenta begins producing progesterone at around 8–10 weeks
  • Progesterone supplements are gradually reduced (tapered) from Week 10
  • Most patients are fully off progesterone by Week 12
  • Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy checks blood progesterone levels before tapering to confirm placental function
  • Never stop progesterone abruptly or without instruction — sudden withdrawal can cause bleeding and increase miscarriage risk

IVF Symptoms in the First Trimester

Understanding which symptoms are from the pregnancy, which are medication side effects, and which are warning signs is crucial in early IVF pregnancy. For a comprehensive day-by-day guide to IVF pregnancy symptoms after embryo transfer, see our IVF pregnancy symptoms guide.

Common normal symptoms in the first trimester:
  • Nausea (usually begins Week 6, peaks Week 8–10, resolves by Week 14)
  • Breast tenderness and changes
  • Fatigue and tiredness
  • Frequent urination (from rising hCG and later, the growing uterus)
  • Mild abdominal bloating and fullness
  • Heightened sense of smell
  • Light-headedness (from progesterone)

First Trimester Activity and Lifestyle After IVF

For a complete guide to what to eat, what to avoid, exercise rules, travel guidelines, and work in early IVF pregnancy, see our Early Pregnancy Care After IVF guide. Key principles:

  • Light activity is safe — daily walks, gentle yoga, household tasks. Bed rest is not recommended and has no evidence of benefit.
  • Avoid heavy lifting (>5 kg), hot baths, saunas, and long-distance travel in the first 2 weeks post-transfer
  • No alcohol, no smoking, and caffeine below 200 mg/day
  • Avoid raw foods that carry infection risk (raw meat, raw eggs, unpasteurised dairy)

Emotional Wellbeing in the First Trimester

IVF patients often experience heightened anxiety in pregnancy compared to naturally conceived pregnancies — this is completely normal and expected after a difficult fertility journey. Strategies that help:

  • Focus on today — try not to project beyond the next scan
  • Limit frequent home pregnancy testing — the anxiety of watching lines can become counterproductive after a confirmed beta hCG
  • Stay connected to your care team — call the clinic for reassurance rather than sitting alone with worry
  • Connect with others — fertility support groups and peer networks are extremely valuable
  • Professional counselling — Mother Hospitals can refer you if anxiety is overwhelming daily life

When Can You Relax?

There is no universal moment — but most IVF patients find that anxiety significantly eases after:

  1. Heartbeat confirmed at the 7-week scan ✓
  2. Normal growth at the 10-week scan ✓
  3. Normal NT scan at 12–13 weeks ✓
  4. Successfully transitioning off progesterone ✓

After the 12-week scan with normal results, you are in the same risk category as any naturally conceived pregnancy. Most IVF patients choose this milestone to share their news with family.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the miscarriage rate after IVF?
Approximately 15–25% overall, slightly higher than natural pregnancy due to older maternal age. After a heartbeat at 7 weeks, risk drops to ~5%. PGT-A tested euploid embryos reduce miscarriage risk to approximately 5% regardless of age.
Is bleeding normal after IVF?
Light spotting is common — caused by implantation, progesterone pessaries, or a subchorionic haematoma. Heavy red bleeding with cramping or one-sided pain requires immediate medical attention. Call 97059 93366. For a complete guide see our bleeding guide.
When does miscarriage risk decrease after IVF?
Risk drops significantly after each milestone: positive beta hCG → heartbeat at 6–7 weeks (risk ~5%) → normal 10-week scan (~2%) → normal 12-week NT scan (under 2%).
Can I travel in the first trimester after IVF?
Avoid long travel for the first 2 weeks after transfer. After a normal 7-week scan, short travel is generally fine. Discuss flying with your doctor — most cases are manageable but some high-risk patients are advised to minimise travel until 12 weeks.
When can I stop progesterone after IVF pregnancy?
Never stop progesterone without explicit instruction. Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy checks blood progesterone levels and tapers the medication from Week 10 — most patients are off by Week 12. Abrupt withdrawal increases miscarriage risk.

తరచుగా అడిగే ప్రశ్నలు

IVF తర్వాత గర్భస్రావం అవకాశం ఎంత?
IVF తర్వాత గర్భస్రావం అవకాశం సాధారణంగా 15–25% — రోగి వయసు మరియు భ్రూణ chromosomal నాణ్యతపై ఆధారపడి ఉంటుంది. 6–7 వారాల స్కాన్‌లో గుండె చప్పుడు నిర్ధారణ తర్వాత ఈ ప్రమాదం ~5% కి తగ్గుతుంది. PGT-A tested euploid భ్రూణాలు వయసుతో సంబంధం లేకుండా miscarriage రేటును ~5–10% కి తగ్గిస్తాయి.
IVF గర్భంలో progesterone ఎందుకు అవసరం, ఎప్పుడు ఆపాలి?
Progesterone గర్భాశయ పొరను నిలబెట్టి భ్రూణ implantation మద్దతు ఇస్తుంది. 10 వారాల తర్వాత మావి progesterone ఉత్పత్తి ప్రారంభిస్తుంది — అప్పుడు క్రమంగా తగ్గిస్తాం. 12 వారాల నాటికి చాలా మంది రోగులు పూర్తిగా ఆపగలుగుతారు. డాక్టర్ అనుమతి లేకుండా ఆపకండి.
