High Risk Pregnancy near Kothapet & Dilsukhnagar

A pregnancy is classified as high risk when there are maternal, foetal, or obstetric factors that increase the chance of complications. For women near Kothapet and Dilsukhnagar, specialist high risk pregnancy care is available just 7–9 km away at Mother Hospitals, Boduppal.

Who needs high risk pregnancy care?

  • Women aged 35 years or older (advanced maternal age)
  • Twin or multiple pregnancies (especially IVF-conceived)
  • Previous caesarean section (VBAC counselling available)
  • Gestational diabetes mellitus (GDM)
  • Pre-eclampsia or hypertension in pregnancy
  • Thyroid disorders, anaemia, or other medical conditions
  • Previous pregnancy losses or preterm deliveries
  • Foetal growth restriction (IUGR)

Monitoring protocol at Mother Hospitals: Dr. Prashanthi's high risk pregnancy programme includes more frequent antenatal visits, detailed anomaly scans, Doppler studies for foetal wellbeing, glucose tolerance testing, and a coordinated plan for safe delivery. Gestational diabetes is managed with dietary counselling, blood glucose monitoring, and — when required — insulin therapy, always in close coordination with the patient.

Dr. Prashanthi's Approach: "High risk does not mean something will go wrong. It means we watch more carefully, we plan more thoroughly, and we act faster when needed. Most high-risk pregnancies result in healthy mothers and babies."

PCOS & Hormonal Disorders — Specialist near Kothapet

PCOS — now more accurately termed PMOS (Polycystic Morphology and Ovarian Syndrome) since the 2023 international nomenclature revision — is one of the most common hormonal disorders affecting women of reproductive age. Studies suggest that up to 1 in 5 women in urban India may have PCOS, making specialist access near Kothapet particularly important.

PCOS diagnosis at Mother Hospitals includes:

  • Detailed menstrual and symptom history
  • Hormonal blood panel: LH, FSH, testosterone, DHEAS, prolactin, thyroid, insulin, AMH
  • Pelvic ultrasound to assess ovarian morphology and antral follicle count (AFC)

Treatment is tailored to your goals:

  • If you are not trying to conceive: cycle regulation, anti-androgen therapy, metabolic management, skin and hair care
  • If you are trying to conceive: ovulation induction, letrozole/clomiphene protocols, IUI, or IVF when needed

Insulin resistance is a key driver in many PCOS cases. Dr. Prashanthi's approach addresses the root metabolic cause — not just the surface symptoms — for lasting improvement.

Laparoscopy & Hysteroscopy near Dilsukhnagar

Minimally invasive gynaecological surgery has transformed women's health — shorter recovery, smaller incisions, and faster return to normal life compared to open surgery. Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy is trained and experienced in both laparoscopy (abdominal keyhole surgery) and hysteroscopy (camera-guided uterine surgery).

When is laparoscopy needed?

  • Endometriosis diagnosis and treatment
  • Fibroid removal (myomectomy) when surgery is indicated
  • Ovarian cyst removal (cystectomy)
  • Fallopian tube assessment and treatment (hydrosalpinx, tubal blockage)
  • Chronic pelvic pain investigation
  • Ectopic pregnancy management

When is hysteroscopy needed?

  • Uterine polyp or fibroid removal (submucosal)
  • Investigation and treatment of abnormal uterine bleeding
  • Assessment of the uterine cavity before IVF
  • Uterine septum resection
  • Removal of retained products (RPOC)

Most laparoscopic procedures at Mother Hospitals are day-care or short-stay — patients from Kothapet and Dilsukhnagar typically return home within 24 hours and resume normal activity within 1–2 weeks.
