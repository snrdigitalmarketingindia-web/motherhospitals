Low AFC (Antral Follicle Count 2, 3, 4) — IVF Success Rate & Treatment

AFC 2 or 3 is very low — but not zero. Here is the honest success rate data, the banking strategy, and exactly how Mother Hospitals approaches very low antral follicle count IVF cycles.

Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy

MBBS, DGO · Diploma in ART (Kiel University, Germany) · TGMC Reg: 50624 · 20+ Years

What Is Antral Follicle Count (AFC)?

The AFC is the number of small (2–10mm) fluid-filled follicles visible in both ovaries on a Day 2–5 ultrasound scan. Each antral follicle has the potential to grow and produce a mature egg during an IVF stimulation cycle. AFC is one of the best real-time predictors of how many eggs an IVF cycle will produce.

Normal AFC: 15–30  |  Low-normal: 8–14  |  Low: 4–7  |  Very low: 1–3

AFC 2 or 3 — Is IVF Still Possible?

Yes — an AFC of 2–3 means IVF is difficult but not impossible. At Mother Hospitals, patients with AFC 2–3 have retrieved eggs and achieved pregnancies. Key factors:

  • Age under 35: Even 1–2 good-quality eggs can fertilise and produce a viable blastocyst
  • AMH correlation: AFC 2–3 usually corresponds to AMH 0.1–0.4 ng/mL
  • DHEA priming: 90 days before IVF can sometimes increase AFC response to stimulation
  • Stimulation protocol: High-dose antagonist protocol (300–450 IU FSH) maximises the chance of developing all visible follicles

DuoStim — Getting Two Retrievals in One Month

For AFC 2–4 patients who want to accumulate embryos before transfer: DuoStim (Dual Stimulation) allows two egg retrievals in one menstrual cycle — once in the follicular phase and once in the luteal phase. This doubles the eggs collected per month and is particularly valuable when AFC is very low.

Embryo Banking Strategy for Low AFC

With AFC 2–3, you may get 1 egg per cycle. The strategy: freeze any resulting embryos from multiple cycles, then transfer the best one(s). This banking approach — typically over 2–3 cycles — gives a cumulative success rate of 40–60% even when individual cycle yield is low.

Can AFC Improve?

AFC reflects the follicles your ovaries happen to have at that point in time. It can vary slightly cycle to cycle but does not significantly "improve." DHEA priming does not create new follicles — it makes existing follicles respond better to FSH. An AFC of 2 may yield 1 egg today and 3 eggs next month simply because of natural variation.
