High Risk Pregnancy Specialist near LB Nagar

Patients from LB Nagar, Vanasthalipuram, Saroornagar, and the broader South Hyderabad area benefit from having a high risk pregnancy specialist just 6–8 km away. A pregnancy is classified as high risk when one or more medical or obstetric factors are present that increase the possibility of complications for the mother, the baby, or both.

Common conditions that make a pregnancy high risk include:

  • Gestational Diabetes Mellitus (GDM): Requires regular blood glucose monitoring, dietary control, and sometimes insulin management throughout pregnancy
  • Hypertension in pregnancy: Including pre-eclampsia and eclampsia, which can progress rapidly and require close specialist oversight
  • Twin or multiple pregnancies: Significantly higher risk of preterm labour, intrauterine growth restriction, and birth complications
  • Advanced maternal age (35+): Higher risk of chromosomal abnormalities, placental problems, and pregnancy complications
  • Previous caesarean section: Risk of uterine rupture and placenta praevia in subsequent pregnancies warrants specialist monitoring
  • Thyroid disorders in pregnancy: Both hypothyroidism and hyperthyroidism can affect fetal brain development and pregnancy outcomes
  • Anaemia: Moderate to severe anaemia during pregnancy increases risk of preterm birth and low birth weight
  • Recurrent pregnancy loss: Two or more consecutive miscarriages that require thorough immunological and genetic investigation

At Mother Hospitals, Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy creates personalised monitoring plans for each high risk pregnancy. From the first trimester, the protocol is calibrated to the patient's specific risk profile — with more frequent scans, specialised blood work, and coordinated management when needed.

When Should You See a Gynaecologist?

Many women in South Hyderabad — including LB Nagar and Vanasthalipuram — delay seeking gynaecology care, either due to a lack of awareness or difficulty finding a trusted specialist close by. Here are ten signs that you should book an appointment at Mother Hospitals:

  1. Irregular periods: Cycles shorter than 21 days, longer than 35 days, or gaps of more than 3 months should be investigated
  2. Painful periods (dysmenorrhoea): Pain severe enough to affect daily activities may indicate endometriosis, adenomyosis, or fibroids
  3. PCOS signs: Unexplained weight gain, facial or body hair growth, acne, and irregular periods together form a classic PCOS picture
  4. Difficulty conceiving: After 12 months of trying (or 6 months if over age 35), a specialist evaluation is recommended
  5. Recurrent miscarriage: Two or more consecutive pregnancy losses require a systematic investigation into causes
  6. Abnormal vaginal discharge: Changes in colour, smell, or volume that persist beyond a few days
  7. Pelvic pain: Persistent or recurrent lower abdominal pain not related to the menstrual cycle
  8. Postcoital bleeding: Bleeding after intercourse requires prompt evaluation to rule out cervical pathology
  9. Urinary symptoms: Frequency, urgency, burning, or incontinence in women may have gynaecological origins
  10. Breast lumps or nipple discharge: Any breast change should be evaluated promptly by a specialist

None of these symptoms should be ignored. An early consultation with our gynaecology team can prevent a manageable condition from becoming a complex or costly one.
