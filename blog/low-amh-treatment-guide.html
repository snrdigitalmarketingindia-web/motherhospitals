Low AMH — What It Means, Treatment Options & IVF Success

✍️ Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy 📅 2026-05-07 ⏱️ 8 min read
Low AMHFertilityIVFOvarian Reserve

A low AMH report is one of the most distressing pieces of news a woman can receive from her gynaecologist. But before you give up hope, you need to understand what AMH actually tells us — and what it doesn't tell us about your fertility.

Key Message: Low AMH does not mean zero fertility. It means fewer eggs — but one good egg is all you need. At Mother Hospitals, we have helped many women with very low AMH (under 0.5 ng/mL) achieve successful pregnancies.

What is AMH (Anti-Müllerian Hormone)?

AMH is a hormone produced by small follicles in your ovaries. It is the best available marker for ovarian reserve — the quantity of eggs remaining in your ovaries. It does NOT measure egg quality.

AMH LevelInterpretation
> 2.0 ng/mLNormal / Good reserve
1.0–2.0 ng/mLAdequate / Lower normal
0.5–1.0 ng/mLLow — specialist care recommended
< 0.5 ng/mLVery low — urgent specialist attention
< 0.1 ng/mLSeverely diminished — requires individualised protocol

Common Causes of Low AMH

  • Age: AMH naturally declines with age, especially after 35
  • Premature Ovarian Insufficiency (POI): Early decline in ovarian function
  • Endometriosis: Damages ovarian tissue reducing egg reserve
  • Previous ovarian surgery: Cyst removal can damage surrounding follicles
  • Autoimmune conditions
  • Genetic factors: Turner syndrome, fragile X premutation carriers
  • Chemotherapy / Radiation history
  • Lifestyle factors: Smoking, environmental toxins

Does Low AMH Prevent Pregnancy?

No — low AMH means your ovarian reserve is lower than average, but:

  • Many women with low AMH conceive naturally
  • IVF can work with even 1-3 good quality eggs
  • Egg quality (determined by age and genetics) matters more than quantity
  • A younger woman with low AMH has better quality eggs than an older woman with normal AMH

Low AMH IVF Protocol at Mother Hospitals

Standard IVF protocols are designed for normal responders. Women with low AMH need modified protocols that prioritise quality over quantity:

  • Natural Cycle IVF: Retrieving the single egg that grows naturally — no aggressive stimulation needed
  • Mini IVF: Mild stimulation to grow 2-4 follicles — gentler on the ovaries
  • Flare Protocol: Short stimulation to maximise the follicle response in poor responders
  • DHEA supplementation: Some evidence supports DHEA pre-treatment for 3 months before IVF in very low AMH
  • Accumulation cycles: Banking embryos from multiple cycles before transfer

Can You Improve AMH Naturally?

AMH cannot be reliably increased through medication or supplements. However, some measures may support ovarian health:

  • Vitamin D supplementation (if deficient)
  • DHEA (under medical supervision only)
  • CoQ10 (antioxidant support for egg quality)
  • Avoiding smoking and environmental toxins
  • Maintaining healthy body weight
  • Omega-3 fatty acids

Note: None of these are proven to increase AMH levels significantly. Focus should be on using your existing eggs efficiently.

When to Seek Treatment for Low AMH

If you have low AMH and are planning pregnancy:

  • Do NOT delay. AMH continues to decline with time.
  • See a fertility specialist immediately — not after 12 months of trying
  • Consider egg freezing now if you are not ready for pregnancy yet
  • A specialist can design a protocol specifically for your AMH level and age

Frequently Asked Questions

What AMH level is considered low for IVF?

An AMH below 1.0 ng/mL is considered low. Below 0.5 ng/mL is very low and requires a specialist-modified IVF protocol. However, IVF can still succeed even with very low AMH if egg quality is good.

Can I get pregnant with AMH of 0.3?

Yes, pregnancy is possible with AMH of 0.3. Younger women (under 37) with low AMH often have good egg quality. Natural Cycle IVF or Mini IVF may be recommended to collect the best available eggs.

Does low AMH mean early menopause?

Low AMH can indicate diminished ovarian reserve, but it doesn't always mean early menopause is imminent. However, fertility treatment should not be delayed, and egg freezing should be considered if pregnancy is planned for later.

How is low AMH treated at Mother Hospitals?

We use individualised protocols including Natural Cycle IVF, Mini IVF, DHEA support, and accumulation cycles. The approach is tailored to your AMH level, age, and antral follicle count.

Should I freeze eggs if I have low AMH?

If you are not ready for pregnancy now, egg freezing is strongly recommended. This preserves your best quality eggs before the reserve declines further. Mother Hospitals offers egg freezing in Hyderabad.
