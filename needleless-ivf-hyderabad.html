What is Needleless IVF?

Pain-Free IVF — No Injections Needed

Conventional IVF involves 10–14 days of daily hormonal injections for ovarian stimulation. Needleless IVF replaces these with oral medications and alternative delivery methods — making the process far more comfortable and accessible.

How is it Different?

Instead of daily subcutaneous injections, Needleless IVF uses oral hormone tablets and nasal sprays for stimulation. The egg collection (OPU) still uses an ultrasound guide, but the stimulation phase is injection-free. For a full comparison of what each IVF medication does and what side effects to expect, see our patient guide.

Who is it Best For?

Patients with needle phobia, those who travel for treatment (NRI patients), women with small veins, and patients who want a less invasive IVF experience. Women with low AMH or poor ovarian reserve may need conventional stimulation for a better egg yield — your doctor will advise.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Needleless IVF as effective as conventional IVF?+

Needleless IVF has comparable success rates for many patient profiles, particularly those with normal to good ovarian reserve. Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy will assess if you are a good candidate during consultation.

Who is NOT a good candidate for Needleless IVF?+

Patients with very low AMH, poor ovarian responders, or those needing aggressive stimulation may still require conventional injection-based IVF for better egg yield. Your doctor will advise appropriately.

Is Needleless IVF more expensive than conventional IVF?+

The cost is similar to conventional IVF. Please contact us for current package details.

Does Mother Hospitals offer Natural Cycle IVF as well?+

Yes. In addition to Needleless IVF, we offer Natural Cycle IVF — which uses no or minimal stimulation and relies on the naturally selected dominant follicle. Ideal for patients who want the most natural approach.

What is the difference between Needleless IVF and Natural Cycle IVF?+

Needleless IVF uses oral medications for stimulation (multiple eggs). Natural Cycle IVF uses no stimulation — just the one egg the body naturally produces each month. Both avoid injections, but they differ in egg yield and stimulation approach.

What medications are used in Needleless IVF?+

Needleless IVF uses oral letrozole or clomiphene for ovarian stimulation, along with intranasal hMG sprays or transdermal gonadotropin patches where needed. The trigger is given via nasal spray. Vaginal progesterone pessaries provide luteal support — no injections are used at any stage.

How many eggs are typically collected in Needleless IVF?+

Needleless IVF typically yields 4–8 mature eggs per cycle — fewer than conventional IVF (8–15 eggs), but sufficient for most patients with normal ovarian reserve. Quality is comparable, and many patients achieve good blastocysts even with fewer eggs. Patients needing more eggs for embryo banking or PGT may be advised conventional IVF.

Can ICSI be combined with Needleless IVF?+

Yes — ICSI is fully compatible with Needleless IVF. The laboratory process (ICSI fertilisation, blastocyst culture, embryo transfer) is identical to conventional IVF. Needleless IVF + ICSI is our standard approach for couples with male factor infertility.

How many clinics in Hyderabad offer Needleless IVF?+

Very few. Needleless IVF requires specialist expertise and an individualised assessment protocol. Mother Hospitals & IVF Center, Boduppal is among the select fertility centres in Hyderabad offering this as a properly assessed treatment — not simply a marketing term.

What is the cost of Needleless IVF at Mother Hospitals?+

Needleless IVF is priced similarly to our standard IVF package, starting from all-inclusive. In some cases, oral medications are less expensive than injectable gonadotropins, which may reduce the medication component. Contact us for a personalised estimate: 97059 93366 or WhatsApp.

Who is needleless IVF best suited for?
Needleless IVF (oral stimulation protocol) suits: women with needle phobia or injection anxiety, those with very busy work schedules who cannot self-administer injections daily, and women with mild-moderate ovarian reserve (AMH 1.0+). For very low AMH, standard injectable protocols tend to produce more eggs.
Is the success rate of needleless IVF lower than standard IVF?
Slightly lower egg numbers compared to standard injectable IVF, but egg quality is comparable. For women with good ovarian reserve, success rates are similar. The key is that needleless IVF is completed — the cycle is not abandoned due to injection difficulties or missed doses, which actually improves real-world outcomes.
How does needleless IVF work exactly?
Oral tablets (letrozole + clomiphene combination, sometimes with low-dose FSH patches or nasal sprays) replace daily FSH injections for ovarian stimulation. Egg retrieval and the rest of the IVF process are identical to standard IVF. The 'needleless' refers to the stimulation phase only.
What medications are used in needleless IVF?
Letrozole (aromatase inhibitor), clomiphene citrate, and in some protocols low-dose FSH as a nasal spray or transdermal patch. The specific combination is tailored to your AMH, AFC, and previous response. Dr. Prashanthi designs the protocol at the first consultation.
Can needleless IVF be done for a second IVF cycle if the first standard cycle failed?
Yes — needleless IVF can be used as an alternative protocol after a failed standard cycle, especially if the patient had poor response or side effects from high-dose injections. It is also used as the protocol of choice for Mini-IVF in low-AMH patients.
