The Science of Conception Timing

What the Wilcox study tells us about the best days to get pregnant

The landmark Wilcox et al. study (2000, New England Journal of Medicine) followed 221 healthy women trying to conceive and mapped day-specific conception probabilities relative to ovulation day (Day 0). This is the most widely cited evidence base for conception timing advice worldwide.

Key Findings of the Wilcox Study

The fertile window is exactly 6 days. Peak probability days are Day -1 and Day 0 (both ~33% per cycle). Conception is impossible from Day +2 onwards in most cycles. Day -5 has only 4% probability — but not zero, which is why some women conceive from what they thought was "safe" sex.

Important Caveats

These probabilities are per cycle for a healthy couple with normal fertility. Actual chances also depend on sperm quality, egg quality (age), uterine receptivity, and timing accuracy. If cycles are irregular, OPKs or follicular tracking ultrasound may be needed.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does the Wilcox study show about conception probability?

Day -5: 4%, Day -4: 6%, Day -3: 10%, Day -2: 28%, Day -1: 33%, Day 0 (ovulation): 33%, Day +1: 2%. Highest probability is on the day before ovulation and ovulation day itself.

Does having intercourse on ovulation day guarantee pregnancy?

No. Even on the best days, probability is ~33% per cycle. Cumulative probability over 6 months is ~80–85% for normal couples. If not pregnant after 12 months (or 6 months over 35), seek fertility evaluation.

How does sperm survival affect conception timing?

Sperm can survive up to 5 days in the reproductive tract. This is why intercourse 5 days before ovulation can still result in conception. The egg survives only 12–24 hours after ovulation.

Should I track basal body temperature (BBT)?

BBT confirms ovulation has occurred (temperature rises after ovulation), but this comes AFTER the fertile window. For real-time detection, OPKs detect the LH surge 12–36 hours BEFORE ovulation.

Does male abstinence before sex improve chances?

Optimal abstinence is 2–4 days. Less than 2 days may lower total sperm count; more than 5 days can reduce motility. Every 1–2 days during fertile window is optimal.

What if I'm not pregnant after 6 months of timed intercourse?

Under 35: seek evaluation after 12 months. Over 35: after 6 months. Includes hormonal panel, pelvic ultrasound, and semen analysis. Call Mother Hospitals: 97059 93366.
