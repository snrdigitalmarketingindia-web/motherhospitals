Who Needs Donor Sperm IVF?

Conditions That Indicate Donor Sperm IVF

Donor sperm IVF is a medically established option for couples or single women where the male partner cannot provide viable sperm. Under the ART (Regulation) Act 2021, this treatment is legal, regulated, and confidential in India.

1. Non-Obstructive Azoospermia (NOA)

No sperm in ejaculate due to testicular failure, not a blockage. Surgical retrieval is often unsuccessful. Donor sperm IVF provides a direct path to parenthood.

2. Failed Surgical Sperm Retrieval

TESA, PESA, or MicroTESE procedures that yielded no viable sperm. Once all retrieval options are exhausted, donor sperm IVF is the next step.

3. Severe Genetic Conditions

Y-chromosome microdeletion or other heritable conditions the couple prefers not to transmit. Donor sperm avoids passing the condition to offspring.

4. Single Women

Medically eligible single women who wish to conceive using donor sperm. Permitted under ART Act 2021. Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy provides compassionate, confidential care.

5. Obstructive Azoospermia (No Surgical Option)

Where vas deferens reconstruction or sperm retrieval is not surgically feasible due to anatomy or prior treatment.

6. Unexplained Severe Oligospermia

Extreme low sperm count unresponsive to medical or surgical treatment, where IVF with partner sperm is unlikely to succeed.

Step by Step

Donor Sperm IVF Process at Mother Hospitals

1

Consultation

Semen analysis, hormonal profile (FSH, LH, testosterone), genetic karyotype, and surgical sperm retrieval assessment for the male partner. Female partner assessment includes AMH, antral follicle count, and uterine cavity evaluation.

2

Donor Selection

Anonymous screened donor matched for blood group compatibility. Full ART Act 2021 compliant program through a registered ART bank. Donor identity is strictly confidential.

3

Female Partner Preparation

Standard IVF ovarian stimulation with gonadotropin medications under ultrasound monitoring. Monitoring scans every 2–3 days until follicles reach optimal size.

4

Egg Retrieval (OPU)

Transvaginal ultrasound-guided egg pick-up under mild sedation. Takes approximately 20 minutes. Patient rests 2–3 hours and is discharged the same day.

5

Fertilisation with ICSI

Donor sperm is used to fertilise each mature egg via ICSI (Intracytoplasmic Sperm Injection) micromanipulation. Fertilisation is confirmed at 16–18 hours.

6

Embryo Culture & Grading

Embryos are cultured for 3–5 days to blastocyst stage. Each embryo is graded for quality. Only the best embryo is selected for transfer.

7

Embryo Transfer

The highest-quality embryo is transferred via a fine catheter through the cervix into the uterus under ultrasound guidance. No anaesthesia needed — takes 10–15 minutes.

8

Pregnancy Test

Beta hCG blood test at 14 days post-transfer confirms pregnancy. Positive result: viability ultrasound at 6–7 weeks confirms fetal heartbeat.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Is using donor sperm legal in India?+

Short answer: Yes — donor sperm IVF is fully legal under the ART (Regulation) Act 2021. Details: The ART Act 2021 permits anonymous sperm donation from registered ART banks. Donors are screened, anonymous, and cannot be related to the recipient couple. Mother Hospitals works with a registered ART bank and follows all ICMR guidelines. Donor identity is protected by law.

Will the baby be genetically related to the mother?+

Short answer: Yes — the baby is 100% genetically the mother's. Details: The baby carries the mother's complete genetic makeup since her own eggs are used. Only the paternal genetic contribution comes from the donor. The baby is not genetically related to the male partner (for couples) but is fully related to the female partner / mother.

How are donors screened at Mother Hospitals?+

Short answer: Comprehensive medical and genetic screening, per ICMR and ART Act 2021 standards. Screening includes: Complete STI panel (HIV, Hepatitis B/C, syphilis, chlamydia), genetic karyotype, semen quality analysis (count, motility, morphology), and a detailed personal and family health history review. Only donors who pass all criteria are included in the program.

Can I choose the donor's characteristics?+

Short answer: Matching is done on blood group compatibility only. Under ART Act 2021: Specific donor selection beyond medical compatibility (blood group) is not permitted in India. This protects donor anonymity and ensures ethical standards. The team will ensure appropriate medical matching. You cannot view, select, or identify specific donors.

What are the success rates for IVF with donor sperm?+

Short answer: Success rates are similar to standard IVF — 50–60% per transfer for women under 35 with good ovarian reserve. Key factors: Female partner's age, ovarian reserve (AMH), uterine health, and embryo quality. Since donor sperm from screened, high-quality donors is used, fertilisation rates are typically good. Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy will provide a personalised estimate at your consultation.
