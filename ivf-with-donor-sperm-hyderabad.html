Quick Answer

When is IVF with donor sperm needed in Hyderabad?

Donor sperm IVF is recommended when: (1) No sperm can be retrieved by TESA/PESA (non-obstructive azoospermia where no sperm exist in the testis); (2) The male partner has a severe genetic condition that must not be passed on; (3) Severe sperm DNA fragmentation not correctable by any means. Under ART Act 2021, donor sperm must come from an anonymous registered sperm bank. Call 97059 93366.

What We Offer

💧 ART Act 2021 Certified Sperm Bank

All donor sperm used at Mother Hospitals comes from ART Act 2021 registered sperm banks. Donors are anonymous, screened for genetics, infections, and blood group. Full legal documentation provided.

🔬 Donor Sperm Screening

Every sperm donor undergoes: semen analysis (count, motility, morphology), genetic karyotype, infectious disease panel (HIV, HBV, HCV, VDRL), blood group, and 6-month quarantine before release.

🧬 ICSI with Donor Sperm

Donor sperm is used for ICSI — single sperm injected directly into each egg. This maximises fertilisation rates and ensures the best donor sperm is selected under microscope.

⚖️ Fully Legal & Anonymous

Under ART Act 2021, donor identity is completely anonymous. The recipient couple receives no identifying information. Legal documentation is provided to register the child's birth.

🌱 IUI Option with Donor Sperm

For women with normal tubes and good ovarian reserve — IUI with donor sperm is a lower-cost, less invasive first option before moving to IVF.

📊 High Success Rates

Donor sperm IVF/ICSI success rates are determined by the female partner's age, AMH, and uterine factors. With good female factors, 50–65% per transfer is achievable.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is donor sperm IVF legal in India?
Yes — fully legal under ART (Regulation) Act 2021. Sperm must come from ART Act registered anonymous donors. Recipient must be a married heterosexual couple. Mother Hospitals is fully ART Act 2021 certified.
How is a sperm donor matched in Hyderabad?
Under ART Act 2021, matching is based on: blood group compatibility, physical characteristics (height, skin tone), and general health. The donor's identity is never revealed to the recipient couple.
Is TESA/PESA always tried before donor sperm in Hyderabad?
Yes — Dr. Prashanthi always recommends TESA (testicular sperm aspiration) or PESA (percutaneous epididymal sperm aspiration) before moving to donor sperm. Only when no sperm can be found surgically, or when the genetic risk is unacceptable, is donor sperm recommended.
Can we use donor sperm for IUI instead of IVF in Hyderabad?
Yes — if the female partner has open tubes and reasonable ovarian reserve, donor sperm IUI is a lower-cost first step. IUI is tried 2–3 cycles before escalating to IVF if unsuccessful.
What is the cost of donor sperm IVF in Hyderabad?
