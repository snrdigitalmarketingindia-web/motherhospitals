Did you know? Male infertility is responsible for 40–50% of all cases where a couple cannot conceive. A low sperm count or abnormal semen report does not mean fatherhood is impossible — ICSI can achieve pregnancy even with very few or abnormal sperm.

Common Causes of Male Infertility

Diagnosed and treated at Mother Hospitals, near Nagaram

📉
Low Sperm Count (Oligospermia)
Fewer than 15 million sperm per ml. Treated with ICSI — even a single healthy sperm is sufficient to fertilise an egg.
🚫
No Sperm (Azoospermia)
Complete absence of sperm in ejaculate. Obstructive azoospermia treated with TESA/PESA to retrieve sperm directly from the testis.
🐢
Poor Motility (Asthenospermia)
Sperm cannot swim effectively to reach the egg. ICSI bypasses motility completely — the sperm is injected directly.
🔬
Abnormal Morphology (Teratospermia)
Irregular sperm shape affects fertilisation ability. ICSI selects the best-shaped sperm for injection — morphology issues become manageable.
🧬
Sperm DNA Fragmentation
Damaged DNA inside sperm causes repeated IVF failure and miscarriages — even when sperm count looks normal. Tested and managed at Mother Hospitals.
⚗️
Hormonal Imbalance
Low testosterone or high FSH can impair sperm production. Hormone profile testing and medical management available.

Male Infertility Treatments near Nagaram

Evidence-based procedures performed at Mother Hospitals & IVF Center, Boduppal

Gold Standard

ICSI — Intracytoplasmic Sperm Injection

A single sperm is selected and injected directly into an egg using a fine needle under high magnification. ICSI is the definitive treatment for all forms of male factor infertility. Mother Hospitals has performed 5,000+ ICSI cycles — accessible to patients from Nagaram in just 12 minutes.

For Azoospermia

TESA — Testicular Sperm Aspiration

For men with obstructive azoospermia, sperm is retrieved directly from the testis using a fine needle. The retrieved sperm is immediately used in ICSI. Performed under local anaesthesia — a day procedure with minimal discomfort.

For Azoospermia

PESA — Percutaneous Epididymal Sperm Aspiration

Sperm is aspirated from the epididymis in men with obstruction. PESA is an alternative to TESA, chosen based on the specific cause and anatomy of azoospermia. Combined with ICSI for fertilisation.

First-Line Option

IUI — Intrauterine Insemination

Processed, concentrated sperm is placed directly into the uterus at the time of ovulation. Recommended for mild male factor infertility. A simpler, less expensive step before moving to IVF/ICSI.

Diagnosis First

Advanced Semen Analysis

Comprehensive semen analysis measures count, motility, morphology, volume, pH and — where indicated — sperm DNA fragmentation index (DFI). Results explained clearly. No confusing reports handed without explanation.

Pre-Treatment Support

Antioxidant & Medical Therapy

Antioxidants (CoQ10, Vitamin E, C, Selenium, L-Carnitine) reduce oxidative stress and improve sperm parameters over 3 months. Combined with hormonal treatment if required. Prescribed after reviewing semen analysis results.
