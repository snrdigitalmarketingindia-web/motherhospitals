Understanding the Treatment

What is Test Tube Baby Treatment?

In India, "test tube baby" and "IVF" (In Vitro Fertilisation) refer to the same treatment. The term originated in 1978 when the world's first IVF baby, Louise Brown, was born — popularly described as a "test tube baby" by the media. Today, millions of babies are born this way worldwide, and the procedure is highly safe, scientific, and successful.

At Mother Hospitals, test tube baby treatment follows a precise 5-step scientific process carried out in our advanced IVF laboratory by Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy (MBBS, DGO, PG Diploma in ART — Kiel University, Germany, TGMC Reg: 50624) with over 19 years of dedicated fertility experience.

1

Ovarian Stimulation

Hormonal medications stimulate your ovaries to produce multiple mature eggs instead of just one. This is monitored with regular ultrasound scans every 2–3 days over a period of 10–14 days. Mother Hospitals offers both conventional stimulation with injections and our pioneering Needleless IVF protocol using oral medications only — completely pain-free.

2

Egg Retrieval (OPU — Oocyte Pick-Up)

When your follicles reach the ideal size (18–20 mm), a trigger injection is given and egg retrieval is scheduled 36 hours later. The procedure is performed under mild sedation via a fine ultrasound-guided needle — completely painless. It typically takes 20–30 minutes and patients rest for 2–3 hours before discharge on the same day.

3

Fertilisation in the Lab (ICSI)

Retrieved eggs are taken to our advanced IVF laboratory where each mature egg is injected with a single prepared sperm using ICSI (Intracytoplasmic Sperm Injection) micromanipulation. This is the "test tube" stage — fertilisation confirmed at 16–18 hours. ICSI is more precise than conventional IVF and is the preferred technique for most couples, especially those with any male factor.

4

Embryo Culture

Fertilised eggs (embryos) are cultured in our incubator for 3–5 days under controlled temperature and gas conditions that closely mimic the natural environment of the fallopian tube. We prefer culturing embryos to Day 5 blastocyst stage — only the strongest, most viable embryos reach this stage, significantly improving the accuracy of embryo selection.

5

Embryo Transfer

The highest-quality embryo is selected and transferred into your uterus through a thin, flexible catheter guided by ultrasound. The procedure takes 10–15 minutes, requires no anaesthesia, and is virtually painless. Remaining good-quality embryos are frozen (vitrified) for future use. A pregnancy blood test (Beta hCG) is done 14 days after the transfer.

Indications

Who Needs Test Tube Baby Treatment?

Test tube baby treatment (IVF) is recommended when simpler treatments have not worked or when the diagnosis makes natural conception unlikely. Here are the most common conditions treated at Mother Hospitals:

🔗

Blocked Fallopian Tubes

When one or both tubes are damaged or blocked, eggs cannot meet sperm naturally. IVF bypasses the tubes entirely, making it the definitive treatment.

🔬

Male Infertility

Low sperm count, poor motility, or abnormal morphology. ICSI allows even a few good sperm to achieve fertilisation. Azoospermia treated with TESA/PESA.

🔄

PCOS (Polycystic Ovary Syndrome)

Women with PCOS who do not ovulate regularly and have not responded to ovulation induction or IUI are excellent candidates for IVF.

📉

Low AMH / Poor Ovarian Reserve

Low ovarian reserve requires specialised stimulation protocols to maximise egg yield. Dr. Prashanthi Reddy is experienced in low-AMH IVF with good outcomes.

❓

Unexplained Infertility

When all tests are normal but pregnancy hasn't occurred for 1–2 years. IVF — especially with ICSI and blastocyst culture — identifies and overcomes hidden fertilisation barriers.

💔

Recurrent Miscarriage

Multiple pregnancy losses may be due to chromosomally abnormal embryos. PGT-A genetic testing of embryos before transfer can significantly improve outcomes.

Is a test tube baby normal?+

Yes, absolutely. Test tube babies (IVF babies) are completely normal and healthy, identical to children conceived naturally. The only difference is that fertilisation occurs in the laboratory rather than inside the body. Decades of research and over 10 million IVF babies worldwide confirm that IVF babies develop normally, have the same intelligence, emotional development, and life expectancy as naturally conceived children. This is confirmed by major paediatric and fertility bodies globally.
