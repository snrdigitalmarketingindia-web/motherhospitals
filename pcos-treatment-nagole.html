PCOS Treatment Options — Nagole Patients

💊 Hormonal Treatment

OCP, progesterone, anti-androgens. Regulates cycle, reduces facial hair, clears acne, prevents endometrial buildup.

🔬 Insulin Resistance Treatment

Metformin, inositol, low-GI diet plan. Addresses root metabolic cause of PCOS — often restores natural ovulation.

PCOS FAQs — Nagole Patients

Can PCOS be cured permanently?
PCOS cannot be permanently cured, but it can be managed effectively with lifestyle changes, medications, and fertility treatment. Many women with PCOS go on to have successful pregnancies with the right treatment.
Can I get pregnant naturally with PCOS?
Yes — with ovulation induction (letrozole/clomiphene). Approximately 60–70% of PCOS patients with no other fertility issues conceive within 3–4 induced cycles. IUI or IVF is recommended if ovulation induction alone fails.
What tests are done for PCOS diagnosis?
Blood tests: LH, FSH, AMH, testosterone, prolactin, thyroid, fasting insulin & glucose. Pelvic ultrasound to check ovarian morphology and antral follicle count. Diagnosis by Rotterdam criteria: 2 of 3 criteria (irregular periods, elevated androgens, polycystic ovaries on scan).

Is PCOS now called PMOS?
Yes — in May 2026, the international consensus renamed PCOS to PMOS (Polycystic Morphology Ovarian Syndrome) to better reflect its ovarian morphology rather than implying multiple cysts. The condition, symptoms and treatment remain the same.
