About PCOS

What is PCOS / PCOD?

PCOS (Polycystic Ovary Syndrome) is a hormonal imbalance where the ovaries produce excess androgens (male hormones), causing irregular periods, cysts on the ovaries, and ovulation problems. It affects 1 in 5 women in India.

PCOS Symptoms

Irregular or missed periods, excessive hair growth (hirsutism), acne, weight gain, hair loss, difficulty getting pregnant, and multiple small cysts visible on ultrasound.

PCOS & Fertility

PCOS can cause anovulation (lack of ovulation), making natural pregnancy difficult. However, with proper treatment — from ovulation induction tablets to IVF — most women with PCOS can achieve pregnancy.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is PCOS — and why is it now called PMOS?+

Short answer: PCOS (Polycystic Ovary Syndrome) was officially renamed PMOS — Polycystic Ovarian Morphology Syndrome by the Endocrine Society in May 2026. The new name better describes the condition as ovarian morphology rather than a "syndrome". The treatment, symptoms, and management remain the same. Both names are currently used.

Can I get pregnant naturally with PCOS / PMOS?+

Short answer: Yes — many women with PCOS conceive naturally. Detailed answer: PCOS affects ovulation. With lifestyle changes (diet, weight management), many women resume regular cycles and conceive without fertility treatment. For those who need help, ovulation induction tablets (Letrozole) are the first step. Dr. Prashanthi Reddy always starts with the least invasive approach.

Does PCOS affect IVF success rates?+

Short answer: Women with PCOS generally have good IVF outcomes. Detailed answer: PCOS patients often have many follicles and produce multiple eggs in IVF, which is an advantage. The main risk is OHSS (ovarian hyperstimulation syndrome). At Mother Hospitals, we use modified protocols, trigger shot adjustments, and freeze-all strategies to prevent OHSS and maximise safety.

What is the treatment protocol for PCOS at Mother Hospitals?+

Dr. Prashanthi Reddy follows a step-up protocol: (1) Lifestyle modification + diet (low-GI, anti-inflammatory) (2) Metformin for insulin resistance (3) Ovulation induction with Letrozole or Clomiphene (4) IUI if ovulation induction alone doesn't achieve pregnancy (5) IVF with ICSI for resistant cases. Treatment is always tailored to your individual goals.

What medications are used for PCOS treatment?+

Short answer: Metformin, Letrozole, Clomiphene, and hormonal medications. Common medications include: Metformin (reduces insulin resistance), Letrozole or Clomiphene Citrate (induce ovulation), progesterone (regulate periods), and hormonal contraceptives (to manage symptoms when pregnancy is not the goal).

Is PCOS curable permanently?+

Short answer: PCOS cannot be permanently cured, but it is very manageable. Detailed answer: PCOS is a lifelong hormonal condition, but its symptoms — irregular periods, weight gain, excess hair — can be significantly controlled with lifestyle changes and medication. Most women with PCOS can regulate their cycles, manage symptoms effectively, and achieve pregnancy with the right treatment.

What is the best diet for PCOS / PMOS?+

Short answer: A low-glycaemic, high-protein, anti-inflammatory diet. What to eat: Vegetables, whole grains, legumes, lean protein (eggs, fish, chicken), healthy fats (nuts, avocado), low-sugar fruits. What to reduce: Refined sugar, white rice/bread, processed foods, sugary drinks. This diet reduces insulin resistance, which is the root driver of PCOS symptoms.
