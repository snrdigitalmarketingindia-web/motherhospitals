      Understanding Fertility Health

      The key factors that determine your fertility potential

      Fertility health is determined by multiple interconnected factors: hormonal balance (regulating ovulation), structural health (fallopian tubes, uterus), ovarian reserve (AMH), and lifestyle factors. A comprehensive evaluation at Mother Hospitals covers all these aspects with blood tests, ultrasound, and clinical assessment.

      Age and Fertility

      Age is the most important fertility factor for women. Fertility peaks in the early 20s and begins a gradual decline after 30, with a steep decline after 35. This is because egg quantity and quality decrease with age — specifically, the proportion of chromosomally abnormal eggs increases. If you are over 35, seek evaluation sooner rather than waiting 12 months.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      What factors most affect female fertility?

      The most important factors: age (egg quality declines significantly after 35), ovulation regularity (PCOS, thyroid), fallopian tube health (STI history, endometriosis), uterine health (fibroids, polyps), ovarian reserve (AMH level), BMI (both underweight and obesity reduce fertility), and lifestyle factors (smoking, alcohol, stress).

      At what age does female fertility decline?

      Female fertility begins to decline gradually after age 30, with a more significant drop after 35, and a steep decline after 38. By age 40, monthly conception probability is approximately 5-7%. This is due to declining egg quality (chromosomal abnormalities). Age is the single most important factor in female fertility.

      Can lifestyle changes improve fertility?

      Yes — lifestyle has a significant impact on fertility. Maintaining healthy BMI, quitting smoking, limiting alcohol, taking folic acid 400-800mcg daily, managing stress, getting adequate sleep, and regular moderate exercise all have evidence-based benefits for fertility.

      Does STI history affect fertility?

      Yes — untreated chlamydia and gonorrhoea are the most common preventable causes of tubal infertility in women. They can cause pelvic inflammatory disease (PID) which damages the fallopian tubes. A hysterosalpingogram (HSG) to check tubal patency is recommended if you have a history of STIs.

      Should I take folic acid before pregnancy?

      Yes. Folic acid 400-800mcg daily is recommended for all women trying to conceive, ideally starting 3 months before conception. It reduces the risk of neural tube defects by up to 70%. Women with a previous neural tube defect pregnancy should take 5mg daily under doctor supervision.

      When should I see a fertility specialist?

      See a fertility specialist if: you have been trying to conceive for 12 months (or 6 months if over 35), you have irregular or absent periods, you have been diagnosed with PCOS or endometriosis, your partner has an abnormal semen analysis, or you have had 2+ miscarriages.       ఏ వయసులో ఫెర్టిలిటీ తగ్గడం మొదలవుతుంది?

      లైఫ్‌స్టైల్ చేంజ్స్ ఫెర్టిలిటీ ఇంప్రూవ్ చేయగలవా?

      STI హిస్టరీ ఫెర్టిలిటీని అఫెక్ట్ చేస్తుందా?
