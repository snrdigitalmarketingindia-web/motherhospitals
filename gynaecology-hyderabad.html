When to See a Gynaecologist

Don't ignore these signs — early evaluation leads to better outcomes

Menstrual Concerns

  • ⚠ Periods more than 35 days apart or fewer than 21 days
  • ⚠ Very heavy bleeding (soaking a pad every hour)
  • ⚠ Severe period pain affecting daily activities
  • ⚠ Periods that have stopped for 3+ months (not pregnant)
  • ⚠ Bleeding between periods or after sex

Hormonal & Pelvic Symptoms

  • ⚠ Unexplained weight gain, hair loss, or acne
  • ⚠ Excess facial or body hair (hirsutism)
  • ⚠ Pelvic pain or pressure at any time in the cycle
  • ⚠ Pain or discomfort during intercourse
  • ⚠ Unusual vaginal discharge or odour

Fertility & Preventive Care

  • ⚠ Trying to conceive for 6–12 months without success
  • ⚠ Two or more miscarriages
  • ⚠ Hot flashes, night sweats, or mood changes (perimenopause)
  • ⚠ Annual Pap smear overdue (recommended every 3 years)
  • ⚠ First gynaecology visit (girls 13–15 years, or at first period)

Frequently Asked Questions

Common gynaecology questions answered by Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy

What is the difference between PCOS and PMOS? ▼

PMOS (Polycystic Metabolic and Ovulatory Syndrome) is the updated name for the condition previously known as PCOS (Polycystic Ovary Syndrome). In May 2026, the Endocrine Society officially recommended renaming PCOS to PMOS to better reflect the condition's broader metabolic and endocrine nature, moving beyond the ovarian-centric description. The underlying condition, symptoms, and treatment approach remain the same — only the name has been modernised. At Mother Hospitals, we treat PCOS/PMOS comprehensively and are fully up to date with the latest clinical guidance.

Can PCOS/PMOS be cured permanently? ▼

PCOS/PMOS cannot be completely cured, but it can be very effectively managed. With the right combination of lifestyle changes (diet, exercise, weight management), hormonal medications, and targeted fertility treatment when needed, most women with PCOS/PMOS lead healthy, normal lives and conceive successfully. Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy creates individualised treatment plans that address the root hormonal imbalances — not just the surface symptoms — giving women long-term control over their condition.

What laparoscopic procedures do you perform at Mother Hospitals? ▼

Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy performs a range of minimally invasive laparoscopic gynaecological procedures including: laparoscopic myomectomy (fibroid removal), laparoscopic ovarian cystectomy, laparoscopic surgery for endometriosis, laparoscopic hysterectomy where clinically indicated, and diagnostic laparoscopy for unexplained pelvic pain or infertility. Laparoscopic (keyhole) surgery offers faster recovery, smaller incisions, and significantly less post-operative pain compared to traditional open surgery.

At what age should girls first visit a gynaecologist? ▼

Girls should ideally visit a gynaecologist from the time of their first menstrual period, or around the age of 13–15 years. An adolescent gynaecology consultation helps address irregular periods, painful periods (dysmenorrhoea), questions about puberty, and early detection of conditions like PCOS/PMOS. These visits at Mother Hospitals are completely non-invasive and are conducted in a sensitive, confidential, and reassuring environment — comfortable for both young patients and their parents.

Do you treat menopausal symptoms at Mother Hospitals? ▼

Yes. Mother Hospitals offers comprehensive menopausal care including assessment of symptoms such as hot flashes, mood changes, sleep disturbances, and vaginal dryness. Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy discusses all available options including Hormone Replacement Therapy (HRT), non-hormonal alternatives, bone health monitoring (osteoporosis screening), and lifestyle guidance. Our goal is to help every woman navigate this natural life stage with confidence, comfort, and good health.
