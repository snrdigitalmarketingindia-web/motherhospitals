FAQs — Uppal Patients

What is a normal AMH level for my age near Uppal?
AMH levels decline with age. At 25–30: 2–6 ng/mL is typical. At 31–35: 1.5–4 ng/mL. At 36–40: 0.7–2.5 ng/mL. Below 1 ng/mL at any age suggests reduced reserve. Below 0.5 ng/mL is considered very low. However, AMH alone does not determine pregnancy success — egg quality matters too.
Can I get pregnant with low AMH near Uppal?
Yes — many women with low AMH conceive with the right IVF protocol. AMH measures egg quantity, not quality. Some women with very low AMH still produce 1–2 excellent quality embryos per cycle — which is all you need. Dr. Prashanthi Reddy has helped many low-AMH patients from east Hyderabad have babies.
What IVF protocol is best for low AMH?
It depends on your specific AMH, AFC, and FSH. Options include: high-dose standard antagonist, DuoStim (two retrievals in one cycle), Mini-IVF (low dose), POSEIDON protocol, or accumulation of embryos over multiple cycles. Dr. Prashanthi selects the right approach for your profile.

At what AMH level should I consider IVF near Uppal?
If your AMH is below 1 ng/mL and you have been trying to conceive for 6–12 months, consult a specialist immediately — do not wait. At very low AMH (below 0.3 ng/mL), IVF sooner rather than later gives the best chance, as reserve declines further with time.
