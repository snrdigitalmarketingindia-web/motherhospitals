How Pregnancy Dating Works

LMP, IVF dating, and ultrasound — which method is most accurate?

Gestational age is counted from the first day of the last menstrual period (LMP), even though conception occurs 2 weeks later. This is because LMP is a reliable known date, while the exact day of fertilisation is usually unknown in natural conception.

IVF Dating — Why It's More Precise

In IVF, the exact fertilisation date is known. For a Day 5 blastocyst transfer: EDD = transfer + 261 days. For Day 3 transfer: EDD = transfer + 263 days.

When to Confirm Your Due Date

Confirm your due date with a dating ultrasound (crown-rump length) at 7–12 weeks. If ultrasound differs from LMP by more than 7 days, the ultrasound date takes precedence. Mother Hospitals provides detailed early pregnancy scans.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is the due date calculated from LMP?

Add 280 days (40 weeks) to your LMP (Naegele's rule). The due date is an estimate — only 5% of babies are born on the exact date. A ±2 week window is normal.

How is the due date calculated for IVF?

Day 5 blastocyst transfer: EDD = transfer + 261 days. Day 3 transfer: EDD = transfer + 263 days. These are precise because fertilisation date is known exactly.

When do the trimesters start and end?

1st trimester: Weeks 1–13. 2nd trimester: Weeks 14–27. 3rd trimester: Weeks 28–40. After 37 weeks, baby is term.

How accurate is ultrasound dating?

First-trimester ultrasound (before 14 weeks) is accurate to ±5–7 days using crown-rump length (CRL). If it differs from LMP by more than 7 days, the due date is adjusted to match.

What is gestational age vs embryonic age?

Gestational age (from LMP) is always 2 weeks more than embryonic age (from conception). At your 8-week scan, the embryo is 6 weeks from fertilisation.

What are the key pregnancy milestones?

Week 6–7: heartbeat. Week 12: nuchal scan. Week 20: anatomy scan. Week 24: viability. Week 37: full term. Week 40: EDD. Mother Hospitals provides all antenatal scans.
