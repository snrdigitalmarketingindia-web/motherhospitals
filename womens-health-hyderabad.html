Adolescent Gynaecology

The teenage years are when gynaecological health lays the foundation for future fertility and wellbeing. Dr. Prashanthi provides a safe, non-judgmental space for girls and young women to discuss any concern — however sensitive.

1

First Period & Menstrual Education

Guidance on what is normal, what to track, and when to seek medical advice. Simple, age-appropriate explanations for girls and parents.

2

Irregular or Absent Periods

Hormonal blood tests and pelvic ultrasound identify thyroid dysfunction, PCOS, premature ovarian insufficiency, or low body weight as the cause.

3

Heavy or Painful Periods

Evaluation for endometriosis, adenomyosis, uterine polyps, or bleeding disorders — with non-invasive treatment options tailored for teenagers.

4

Teenage PCOS

Lifestyle, dietary, and hormonal management to regularise cycles, manage acne, reduce excess hair, and protect future fertility.

When Should a Teenager See a Gynaecologist?

Any of the following should prompt an early gynaecology visit:

No period by age 16
Periods lasting more than 7 days or soaking more than 1 pad/hour
Severe period pain interfering with school or daily life
Periods more than 45 days apart or fewer than 21 days apart
Acne, excess facial hair, or unexplained weight gain
Pelvic pain outside of periods
Unusual vaginal discharge or odour

HPV Vaccination at Mother Hospitals

The HPV vaccine (Gardasil 9) provides protection against the strains of Human Papillomavirus that cause cervical cancer, vulval cancer, and genital warts. It is most effective when given before sexual debut, ideally between ages 9–14, but is available and recommended for women up to age 45. We offer a 2-dose or 3-dose schedule depending on age.

Reproductive Health & Fertility Counselling

Whether you are planning to conceive now, in the future, or simply want to understand your reproductive health, our team provides evidence-based assessments and guidance.

🧪

AMH Testing

Anti-Müllerian Hormone (AMH) is the most reliable marker of ovarian reserve. A single blood test — done on any day of your cycle — gives a clear picture of your egg count and fertility timeline.

📅

Ovulation Assessment

Serial transvaginal ultrasound tracking (follicle monitoring) to confirm ovulation, time intercourse or IUI, and detect anovulation (cycles where no egg is released).

💊

PCOS Management

Individualised treatment combining lifestyle modification, dietary guidance, Metformin, ovulation induction (Letrozole/Clomiphene), and IUI or IVF where needed.

💉

Hormonal Evaluation

FSH, LH, Prolactin, TSH, Testosterone, DHEA-S, Oestradiol, and Progesterone panels to identify hormonal imbalances affecting fertility or menstrual regularity.

Pre-Conception Care

Folic acid supplementation, rubella immunity, thyroid screening, anaemia correction, and lifestyle optimisation to improve pregnancy outcomes from day one.

Contraception Counselling

Gynaecological Conditions We Treat

Many gynaecological conditions are underdiagnosed because women normalise their symptoms. If something doesn't feel right, it is worth getting checked — early diagnosis always leads to better outcomes.

🔴 Uterine Fibroids

Non-cancerous growths in the uterus that cause heavy periods, pelvic pressure, and sometimes difficulty conceiving. Treatment depends on size, location, and symptoms — and may include medical management or surgical removal (myomectomy) preserving the uterus.

🟣 Endometriosis

Tissue similar to the uterine lining grows outside the uterus, causing severe period pain, deep pelvic pain, and infertility in up to 30–40% of affected women. Laparoscopic excision and targeted hormonal therapy can significantly improve quality of life and fertility.

🟡 Ovarian Cysts

Most cysts are functional and resolve on their own. Persistent or complex cysts (endometriomas, dermoid cysts, cystadenomas) may require surgical removal. We assess each cyst carefully with serial ultrasound and CA-125 to guide management safely.

🟤 Pelvic Pain

Chronic pelvic pain has many causes — endometriosis, adenomyosis, pelvic inflammatory disease (PID), adhesions, or irritable bowel syndrome (IBS). A thorough history, examination, and targeted investigations help identify the root cause rather than just masking the pain.

🔵 Abnormal Uterine Bleeding

Heavy, prolonged, irregular, or post-menopausal bleeding always deserves investigation. We use ultrasound, hysteroscopy, and endometrial biopsy where needed to rule out polyps, fibroids, hormonal imbalance, or endometrial pathology.

🟢 Vaginal Discharge & Infections

Bacterial vaginosis, vaginal candidiasis (thrush), trichomoniasis, and cervicitis cause discharge, odour, and discomfort. Accurate diagnosis through high vaginal swab and culture guides effective treatment — rather than repeated self-treatment that may not address the underlying issue.

Fertility Treatments at Mother Hospitals

If you have been trying to conceive for 12 months (or 6 months if over 35) without success, a fertility evaluation is the next logical step. Our IVF centre in Boduppal offers the full spectrum of assisted reproduction — including India's only Needleless IVF protocol.

🧫

IVF (In Vitro Fertilisation)

Eggs are retrieved and fertilised in the embryology laboratory. The best embryo is transferred to the uterus. Recommended for tubal factor, unexplained infertility, and severe male factor.

💉

Needleless IVF

Mother Hospitals' pioneering protocol uses oral and nasal medications instead of daily injections — making IVF pain-free and stress-free, especially for injection-phobic patients. Learn more →

🔬

ICSI

A single healthy sperm is injected directly into each egg — the gold standard for male factor infertility, low sperm count, poor motility, or previous IVF fertilisation failure.

💧

IUI (Intrauterine Insemination)

Washed and concentrated sperm are placed directly into the uterus at ovulation. A simpler, less invasive first-line treatment for mild male factor, unexplained infertility, and PCOS with ovulation induction.

📊

Ovulation Induction

Oral medications (Letrozole or Clomiphene) stimulate the ovaries to produce one or two eggs in women who ovulate irregularly. Combined with timed intercourse or IUI for best results.

🧊

Fertility Preservation

Egg freezing (oocyte cryopreservation) allows women to preserve fertility for the future — whether due to cancer treatment, career planning, or personal choice. Embryo freezing is also available after IVF.

Menopause Care

Menopause is a natural transition — but it does not have to be suffered through. Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy offers evidence-based therapies to manage symptoms, protect long-term health, and ensure quality of life does not diminish in this important phase.

1

Perimenopause Assessment

FSH, LH, oestradiol, and AMH levels help confirm whether symptoms (irregular periods, hot flushes, sleep disturbance, mood changes) are peri-menopausal or have another cause.

2

Symptom Management

Hormone Replacement Therapy (HRT) for eligible women, non-hormonal options (SSRIs, Clonidine, dietary supplements), vaginal oestrogen for dryness, and sleep hygiene guidance.

3

Bone Health

Oestrogen loss accelerates bone loss after menopause. DEXA scan (bone density), calcium and vitamin D optimisation, and lifestyle advice to reduce fracture risk.

4

Cardiovascular & Metabolic Health

Post-menopausal women face increased risk of heart disease, hypertension, and type 2 diabetes. Annual blood pressure, lipid, and fasting glucose monitoring with lifestyle counselling.

Post-Menopausal Bleeding — Always Investigate

Any vaginal bleeding after 12 months without a period must be investigated promptly. While it is often benign (atrophic vaginitis, polyps), it can rarely indicate endometrial or cervical pathology. An ultrasound and endometrial biopsy will be arranged promptly — please do not delay seeking assessment.

Preventive Health Screening for Women

Prevention is always better than cure. Regular health checks catch problems before they become serious and give you the reassurance of knowing your body is healthy.

🔍

Cervical Cancer Screening

Pap smear from age 21 (every 3 years). HPV co-test from age 30 (every 5 years). Colposcopy and biopsy for abnormal results. HPV vaccination up to age 45.

🩺

Breast Examination

Clinical breast examination at every annual visit. Mammogram from age 40 (or earlier if family history of breast cancer). Teaching self-examination technique.

🩸

Anaemia & Iron Screening

Heavy periods frequently cause iron-deficiency anaemia in women of reproductive age. Annual haemoglobin, serum ferritin, and blood count to guide iron therapy.

🦋

Thyroid Function Test

Thyroid disorders are significantly more common in women. TSH testing detects hypothyroidism or hyperthyroidism — both of which affect periods, fertility, pregnancy, and energy levels.

🍬

Blood Sugar & Lipid Panel

Fasting glucose, HbA1c, and lipid profile from age 30 (or earlier with PCOS, family history, or obesity). Identifies diabetes and cardiovascular risk before complications develop.

🦴

Bone Density (DEXA Scan)

Recommended from menopause or earlier for women on long-term steroids, with eating disorders, or with other osteoporosis risk factors. Guides calcium and vitamin D therapy.

Frequently Asked Questions — Women's Health

Common questions patients ask Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy about women's health care at Mother Hospitals.

What is a women's health specialist? +
A women's health specialist is a doctor — typically a gynaecologist and obstetrician — who focuses on medical conditions and health needs unique to women across all life stages: from adolescence and the reproductive years, through pregnancy and childbirth, to menopause and beyond. Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy at Mother Hospitals covers this complete spectrum of care.
At what age should a girl first see a gynaecologist? +
The Indian Academy of Paediatrics recommends an adolescent girl's first gynaecology visit between ages 13–15, or earlier if she has concerns such as irregular periods, heavy bleeding, or pelvic pain. Early consultation helps identify conditions like PCOS, hypothyroidism, or hormonal imbalances before they affect fertility in later years.
What are the signs of PCOS and when should I seek treatment? +
Common signs of PCOS include irregular or missed periods, excess facial or body hair (hirsutism), acne, weight gain around the abdomen, thinning scalp hair, and difficulty conceiving. If you experience two or more of these symptoms, an ultrasound and hormonal blood tests (LH, FSH, AMH, insulin) can confirm the diagnosis. Early treatment significantly improves fertility outcomes.
Can I still get pregnant if I have PCOS or low AMH? +
Yes. PCOS is one of the most treatable causes of infertility — ovulation induction, lifestyle changes, and IUI or IVF achieve good pregnancy rates. Low AMH means fewer eggs but does not mean zero fertility. At Mother Hospitals, Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy tailors protocols to your ovarian reserve so that even women with low AMH have a clear pathway to pregnancy.
What routine health checks should women have every year? +
Women should have annual pelvic examination, Pap smear (every 3 years from age 21, or every 5 years with HPV co-test from age 30), breast examination, blood pressure, blood sugar and lipid screening, thyroid function test, vitamin D and haemoglobin levels, and BMI assessment. Mother Hospitals offers a Women's Wellness Package covering all of these.
What is the difference between perimenopause and menopause? +
Perimenopause is the transition phase — usually starting in the mid-40s — when oestrogen levels begin to fluctuate. Periods become irregular, and symptoms like hot flushes, sleep disturbance, and mood changes appear. Menopause is confirmed when periods have stopped for 12 consecutive months (typically around age 50–51). Post-menopause is the phase after that. Dr. Prashanthi offers hormone therapy and non-hormonal options to ease this transition.
How is cervical cancer screening done and how often? +
Cervical cancer screening uses a Pap smear (where cells from your cervix are gently collected and examined) and an HPV DNA test. Current guidelines recommend Pap smear alone every 3 years for women aged 21–29, and Pap + HPV co-test every 5 years for women aged 30–65. Abnormal results are followed up with colposcopy. The HPV vaccine (Gardasil) is also available for women up to age 45.
