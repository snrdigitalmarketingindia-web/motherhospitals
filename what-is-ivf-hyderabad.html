What is IVF? — In Vitro Fertilisation Explained Simply

IVF (In Vitro Fertilisation) is a process where eggs and sperm are combined in a laboratory to create an embryo, which is then placed in the uterus. Here is everything you need to know, in plain language.

IVF Meaning

What Does IVF Mean?

"In Vitro" is Latin for "in glass" — referring to fertilisation happening outside the body in a laboratory dish, not inside the fallopian tube. The term "test tube baby" comes from this, though a dish is used, not an actual test tube.

The History of IVF

IVF was developed in 1978. The world's first IVF baby, Louise Brown, was born on 25 July 1978 in England. Since then, over 10 million babies have been born worldwide through IVF. It is now the most established and widely used assisted reproductive technology globally.

IVF in Simple Terms

Normally, an egg and sperm meet inside the fallopian tube to create an embryo, which then travels to the uterus. In IVF, this fertilisation step is done in a laboratory. The embryo is grown for 3–5 days and then placed directly into the uterus.

Who Needs IVF?

Blocked or damaged fallopian tubes
Severe male infertility (low sperm count / motility)
Unexplained infertility after failed IUI cycles

Recurrent miscarriage
Genetic disease screening (PGT-A)
Fertility preservation before cancer treatment
Step by Step

How Does IVF Work? — 8 Steps

Here is the complete IVF process explained step by step, from your first assessment to the pregnancy test.

1

Fertility Assessment

Blood tests (AMH, FSH, LH) measure your ovarian reserve and hormone levels. An ultrasound scan checks the antral follicle count. A semen analysis assesses sperm count, motility, and morphology. This creates the baseline for your personalised IVF plan.

2

Ovarian Stimulation (10–14 days)

Daily hormone injections stimulate the ovaries to grow multiple eggs instead of just one. Growing multiple eggs increases the chances of having a good quality embryo to transfer.

3

Monitoring Scans

Ultrasound scans every 2–3 days track follicle growth (the fluid sacs that contain the eggs). Blood hormone tests are done in parallel to guide medication adjustments.

4

Trigger Injection

When the follicles reach 18–20 mm, a trigger injection is given. This causes the eggs to complete their final maturation. Egg retrieval is scheduled 36 hours after the trigger.

5

Egg Retrieval (OPU)

A minor procedure done under mild sedation (you are asleep). A fine needle guided by ultrasound collects eggs from the follicles through the vaginal wall. The procedure takes approximately 20 minutes. You go home the same day.

6

Fertilisation with ICSI

In the laboratory, a single prepared sperm is injected directly into each mature egg using a technique called ICSI (Intracytoplasmic Sperm Injection). Fertilisation is confirmed the following morning.

7

Embryo Culture & Transfer

Fertilised eggs (embryos) are monitored in an incubator for 3–5 days. On Day 5, the best quality blastocyst embryo is selected and gently placed into the uterus through the cervix using a fine catheter. No anaesthesia needed — it takes about 10 minutes.

8

Pregnancy Test

A blood hCG test is done 14 days after embryo transfer. A positive result confirms pregnancy. An ultrasound at 6–7 weeks confirms the fetal heartbeat.

IVF vs IUI

IVF vs IUI — What is the Difference?

IUI (Intrauterine Insemination) and IVF are both fertility treatments, but they work very differently. Understanding the difference helps you know which treatment is right for you.

Feature IUI IVF
What happens Prepared sperm is placed directly inside the uterus. Fertilisation happens naturally inside the body. Eggs are retrieved from the ovaries and fertilised with sperm in a laboratory. The embryo is then transferred into the uterus.
Complexity Simpler, less invasive, lower cost More complex, involves egg retrieval procedure and lab fertilisation
Best for Mild male factor, unexplained infertility, cervical factor — with open tubes Blocked tubes, severe male infertility, failed IUI, low AMH, endometriosis, genetic screening
Success per cycle Lower (typically 10–20% per cycle) Higher (varies significantly by age and diagnosis)

IVF is recommended when IUI has not worked or when the diagnosis makes IUI unlikely to succeed. Dr. E. Prashanthi Reddy will advise the right treatment for your specific case at your first consultation.

IVF Glossary

Common IVF Terms Explained

New to IVF? Here are six terms you will hear often — explained simply.

ICSI

Intracytoplasmic Sperm Injection. A single sperm is injected directly into an egg in the laboratory. Used in most IVF cycles — especially important when sperm quality is low.

Blastocyst

An embryo that has developed to Day 5. Only the strongest embryos reach this stage. Blastocyst transfer gives better implantation rates than Day 3 transfer.

FET (Frozen Embryo Transfer)

A previously frozen embryo is thawed and transferred into the uterus in a later cycle. Allows extra embryos to be used without repeating the full stimulation and egg retrieval.

PGT-A

Preimplantation Genetic Testing for Aneuploidies. A small biopsy from the embryo is tested to check for the correct number of chromosomes. Helps select genetically normal embryos — especially useful after recurrent miscarriage or failed IVF.

AMH

Anti-Müllerian Hormone. A blood test that measures ovarian reserve — how many eggs remain. A low AMH means fewer eggs but does not mean IVF will not work. Dr. Prashanthi Reddy specialises in low AMH management.

Trigger Shot

An injection given when follicles are mature (18–20 mm). It causes the eggs to complete their final maturation, 36 hours before the planned egg retrieval procedure.

Your Questions Answered

Frequently Asked Questions About IVF

Does IVF hurt?+

The injections cause mild discomfort, similar to a routine vaccination. Egg retrieval is done under sedation — you will not feel pain during the procedure. Some mild cramping and bloating for 1–2 days after egg retrieval is normal. The embryo transfer is painless and takes about 10 minutes. Most patients find IVF physically manageable.

How many IVF cycles does it take to get pregnant?+

Many couples conceive in the first or second cycle. Success depends on age, diagnosis, embryo quality, and individual response to treatment. Women under 35 have higher per-cycle success rates. Cumulative success rates improve with each cycle. Mother Hospitals offers honest, case-specific success estimates at the first consultation — we do not give blanket numbers.

What is the difference between IVF and test tube baby?+

They are exactly the same thing. "Test tube baby" is the common name used in India for IVF. The term originated from the early days of the procedure when the laboratory equipment resembled test tubes. Fertilisation actually takes place in a small flat dish, not a test tube. Both terms refer to the same procedure: In Vitro Fertilisation.

Is IVF safe for the mother?+

IVF is a well-established and safe medical procedure with decades of safety data. The main risk is Ovarian Hyperstimulation Syndrome (OHSS) — an excessive response to the stimulation hormones. This is carefully monitored with regular scans and blood tests, and medication is adjusted to prevent it. The risk of multiple pregnancy is reduced by transferring a single embryo (Single Embryo Transfer — SET), which is the standard at Mother Hospitals.
