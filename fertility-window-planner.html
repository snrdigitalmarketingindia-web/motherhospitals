How to Use Your Fertility Planner

Making the most of your fertile window

Your fertility window is the 6 days when pregnancy is possible. Having intercourse every 1–2 days throughout the green and dark green days gives you the best chance of conception. You do not need to have intercourse on one specific "perfect day" — sperm wait in the fallopian tubes for the egg to arrive.

Intercourse Timing Tips

Peak fertility days are the day before and day of ovulation (dark green). Having intercourse on these days is strongly recommended. Including the 3–4 days before ovulation is also important as sperm can survive 5 days. Once every 1–2 days is optimal.

When to Use OPKs (Ovulation Predictor Kits)

If you have irregular cycles or want to confirm ovulation, start OPK testing from Day 10 of your cycle. A positive OPK means ovulation will occur within 12–36 hours. OPKs are available at any pharmacy for approximately ₹100–₹200 per test.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the fertility window?

The fertility window is the 6-day period when conception is possible: the 5 days before ovulation and ovulation day itself. Sperm can survive up to 5 days, but the egg survives only 12–24 hours. Intercourse 2–3 days before ovulation gives the highest pregnancy chances.

What days are best for conception?

The Wilcox study shows highest probability on Days -1 and 0 (~33% each), Day -2 (~28%), Day -3 (~10%). Day +1 is only ~2%. The 2 days before ovulation plus ovulation day are the best days.

How often should I have intercourse during the fertile window?

Every 1–2 days throughout the fertile window is optimal. Daily is equally effective. Avoid abstinence longer than 5 days as it reduces sperm motility.

Can the fertile window shift?

Yes — stress, illness, travel, weight change, intense exercise, and hormonal fluctuations can shift ovulation timing. Even regular cycles can vary by 2–4 days. For irregular cycles, use BBT or OPKs alongside this calendar.

Does lubricant affect sperm?

Most commercial lubricants (including saliva) can reduce sperm motility. Use sperm-friendly lubricants such as Pre-Seed or Conceive Plus if needed.

What if I have irregular cycles?

If cycles vary by more than 7 days, dates here are approximate only. Use OPKs from Day 10. If you have PCOS or have been trying 6+ months without success, consult Mother Hospitals: 97059 93366.
