Understanding AMH

What Does Low AMH Mean?

AMH (Anti-Müllerian Hormone) is produced by small follicles in the ovaries and is the best marker of ovarian reserve — the number of eggs remaining. A low AMH indicates diminished ovarian reserve (DOR), but it does NOT mean zero eggs, and pregnancy is still possible.

What is a Normal AMH Level?

Normal AMH for reproductive age women is 1.0–3.5 ng/mL. Low AMH is generally below 1.0 ng/mL. Very low AMH is below 0.5 ng/mL. However, even very low AMH patients have achieved pregnancy at Mother Hospitals.

Does Low AMH Mean No Eggs?

No. AMH reflects ovarian reserve quantity, not quality. Some women with low AMH still produce good quality eggs. With the right stimulation protocol, we can retrieve eggs for IVF treatment at Mother Hospitals even with very low AMH. Learn more: low AMH and IVF pregnancy chances.

Your AMH, Your Options

"My AMH is ___ — Can I Get Pregnant?"

The answer is almost always: possibly, yes. Here's what each range typically means for IVF at Mother Hospitals.

AMH 1.0 – 2.0 ng/mL — Borderline Low

What this means: Your reserve is below the ideal range for your age but there are still usable follicles. This is one of the most common situations we see at Mother Hospitals.
IVF approach: Standard antagonist protocol with moderate stimulation. Most patients in this range get 4–8 eggs, sufficient for IVF. DHEA is sometimes recommended for 8–12 weeks before stimulation.
Success outlook: Good to moderate — comparable to standard IVF in good egg quality cases. Many patients in this range succeed on their first cycle.

AMH 0.5 – 1.0 ng/mL — Low Reserve

What this means: Diminished ovarian reserve. Fewer eggs per cycle, but pregnancy is very much achievable.
IVF approach: Modified antagonist or agonist flare protocol to maximise follicle recruitment. DHEA 25–75mg/day for 8–12 weeks before IVF is recommended. Higher gonadotropin doses may be used. Expect 2–5 eggs per retrieval.
Success outlook: Moderate — 35–50% cumulative success with good quality eggs if age is under 38. Embryo banking (2 cycles) may be advised to improve chances.

AMH 0.2 – 0.5 ng/mL — Very Low Reserve

What this means: Significantly diminished reserve. IVF is still an option but requires a specialist low-reserve protocol.
IVF approach: Natural Cycle IVF (no stimulation) or Mini-IVF with low-dose stimulation. DHEA 8–12 weeks pre-treatment. Testosterone priming may be added. Embryo banking over 2–3 cycles before transfer.
Success outlook: 25–40% cumulative success (under 35). Requires patience and realistic expectations. Many patients in this group have achieved pregnancy at Mother Hospitals — quality still matters more than quantity.

AMH Below 0.2 ng/mL — Severely Low Reserve

What this means: Very few follicles remain. This is the most challenging scenario, but not hopeless.
IVF approach: Natural Cycle IVF — collecting the single naturally recruited egg per month, avoiding stimulation altogether. DHEA pre-treatment. Pooling embryos over multiple natural cycles before transfer is standard.
Success outlook: Lower success per cycle (10–25%), but cumulative success with multiple natural cycles is real. Donor egg IVF is also discussed as an alternative — the uterus is healthy, and donor eggs can achieve excellent success rates. Dr. Prashanthi Reddy will counsel you honestly about both options.

Pre-IVF Preparation

The DHEA Protocol for Low AMH

DHEA (dehydroepiandrosterone) is the most evidence-backed supplement for women with low ovarian reserve preparing for IVF. Here's everything you need to know.

How DHEA Works

DHEA is a precursor hormone that converts to androgens (testosterone) inside the ovarian follicle. Low androgen levels in the ovary are associated with poor response to stimulation. DHEA raises intra-ovarian androgens, improving the microenvironment that follicles need to grow and mature normally.

The Evidence

Multiple randomised trials (including a landmark 2010 study in Human Reproduction) show DHEA pre-treatment improves egg number, fertilisation rate, and embryo quality in poor responders. A 2020 meta-analysis confirmed statistically significant improvements in live birth rates when DHEA was used for ≥8 weeks before IVF in low AMH patients.

Dosage & Duration

Standard dose: 25–75 mg/day (micronised DHEA)
Duration: 8–12 weeks minimum before egg retrieval
Why 8–12 weeks? Follicle development takes approximately 85–90 days (3 months) from early recruitment to ovulation. DHEA needs to be present during this window to have effect.

Monitoring During DHEA

Dr. Prashanthi Reddy monitors DHEA-S blood levels monthly to ensure levels stay in the therapeutic range (300–400 mcg/dL). Side effects (acne, oily skin, mild hair changes) are uncommon at therapeutic doses and reversible. DHEA should only be taken under medical supervision.

Combined Protocol

DHEA works best alongside:
• CoQ10 300–600mg/day (mitochondrial energy for eggs)
• Vitamin D3 (target 40–60 ng/mL blood level)
• Omega-3 DHA 1,000mg/day
• Folic acid 400–800mcg/day
Start all supplements together 3 months before IVF. Full supplement guide →

Who Benefits Most

DHEA is most beneficial for:
• Women with AMH below 1.0 ng/mL
• Poor responders (fewer than 3 eggs in a previous IVF cycle)
• Women with unexplained premature ovarian ageing
• Patients aged 35–43 with low AFC
DHEA is not indicated for women with normal ovarian reserve or PCOS.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I get pregnant with low AMH?+

Yes. While low AMH means fewer eggs, it does not mean zero eggs. Many patients with low AMH have had successful pregnancies at Mother Hospitals using Natural Cycle IVF or Mini IVF protocols.

What AMH level is too low for IVF?+

There is no hard cut-off. Even patients with AMH as low as 0.1 ng/mL have achieved pregnancy through IVF. Dr. Prashanthi Reddy evaluates antral follicle count (AFC) on ultrasound alongside AMH to plan your treatment.

Can AMH levels improve?+

AMH reflects the natural decline of ovarian reserve over time and cannot be significantly reversed. However, DHEA supplementation and optimised protocols can improve response and egg quality in some patients.

How many eggs are needed for IVF with low AMH?+

Even 1–2 mature eggs can result in a successful IVF pregnancy if the embryo quality is good. Natural Cycle IVF works with a single egg by design.

What is the IVF success rate with low AMH?+

Success rates with low AMH depend on age, egg quality, and protocol. At Mother Hospitals: AMH 0.5–1.0 ng/mL yields 35–50% cumulative success (under 38); AMH 0.2–0.5 ng/mL yields 25–40%; below 0.2 yields 10–25% per cycle. Cumulative success with embryo banking is higher. See our dedicated low AMH IVF success rate page.

What is DHEA and should I take it for low AMH?+

DHEA (dehydroepiandrosterone) is a hormone precursor that improves the androgen microenvironment of ovarian follicles. Studies show it improves egg count and quality in poor responders when taken 8–12 weeks before IVF. At Mother Hospitals, DHEA 25–75mg/day is prescribed under medical supervision with monthly DHEA-S blood monitoring. Do not self-medicate — consult Dr. Prashanthi Reddy first.

What supplements should I take before IVF with low AMH?+

Dr. Prashanthi Reddy recommends: DHEA (if prescribed) 8–12 weeks before IVF; CoQ10 300–600mg/day; Vitamin D3 (target 40–60 ng/mL); Omega-3 DHA 1,000mg/day; and Folic acid 400–800mcg/day. Start 3 months before your planned egg retrieval. See our supplement guide for full details.

Is Natural Cycle IVF better than conventional IVF for low AMH?+

For very low AMH (below 0.5 ng/mL), Natural Cycle IVF (no stimulation) can be a good option — it avoids stimulation that may produce a poor response anyway, and it's less expensive per cycle. Pooling embryos from 2–3 natural cycles before transfer (embryo banking) improves cumulative success. Dr. Prashanthi Reddy will advise the most appropriate approach for your specific AMH and AFC.

Should I try naturally or go straight to IVF with low AMH?+

AMH does not predict natural conception rate — only IVF egg yield. If your AMH is between 0.5–1.0 ng/mL and you're under 35, trying naturally for 6 months while doing DHEA pre-treatment is reasonable. If AMH is below 0.5 ng/mL or you're over 35, every month matters — earlier IVF consultation is strongly recommended. Read: Can low AMH patients conceive naturally?
